<span lang="pt"><i>Moçambique</i></span> or <i><span lang="pt">República de Moçambique</span></i>, <small>pronounced:&#160;</small><span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Portuguese_and_Galician" title="Help:IPA for Portuguese and Galician">[ʁɛˈpublikɐ di musɐ̃ˈbiki]</a></span>), is a country in <a and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zambia" title="Zambia">Zambia</a> to the northwest, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zimbabwe" title="Zimbabwe">Zimbabwe</a> to the west, and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Swaziland" title="Swaziland">Swaziland</a> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">South Africa</a> to the southwest. It is separated from <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Madagascar" title="Madagascar">Madagascar</a> by the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mozambique_Channel" title="Mozambique Channel">Mozambique Channel</a> to the east. The capital and largest city is <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maputo" title="Maputo">Maputo</a> (previously called <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maputo" title="Maputo">Lourenço Marques</a> before independence).</p>
<p>Between the 1st and 5th centuries AD, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bantu_languages" title="Bantu languages">Bantu</a>-speaking peoples migrated from farther north and west. Swahili, and later also Arab, commercial ports existed along the coasts until the arrival of Europeans. The area was explored by <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vasco_da_Gama" title="Vasco da Gama">Vasco da Gama</a> in 1498 and colonized by <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">Portugal</a> from 1505. After over four centuries of Portuguese rule, Mozambique <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mozambican_War_of_Independence" title="Mozambican War of Independence">gained independence</a> in 1975, becoming the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/People%27s_Republic_of_Mozambique" title="People's Republic of Mozambique">People's Republic of Mozambique</a> shortly thereafter. After only two years of independence, the country descended into an intense and protracted <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mozambican_Civil_War" title="Mozambican Civil War">civil war</a> lasting from 1977 to 1992. In 1994, Mozambique held its first multiparty elections and has remained a relatively stable presidential republic since.</p>
<p>Mozambique is endowed with rich and extensive natural resources. The country's economy is based largely on agriculture, but industry, mainly food and beverages, chemical manufacturing, aluminium and petroleum production, is growing. The country's tourism sector is also growing. South Africa is Mozambique's main trading partner and source of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Foreign_direct_investment" title="Foreign direct investment">foreign direct investment</a>. Portugal, Brazil, Spain and Belgium are also among the country's most important economic partners. Since 2001, Mozambique's annual average GDP growth has been among the world's highest. However, the country ranks among the lowest in <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_countries_by_GDP_(PPP)_per_capita" title="List of countries by GDP (PPP) per capita">GDP per capita</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_countries_by_Human_Development_Index" title="List of countries by Human Development Index">human development</a>, measures of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_countries_by_inequality-adjusted_HDI" title="List of countries by inequality-adjusted HDI">inequality</a>, and average <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Life_expectancy" title="Life expectancy">life expectancy</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-CIA_6-0" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mozambique#cite_note-CIA-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The only official language of Mozambique is Portuguese, which is spoken mostly as a second language by about half of the population. Common native languages include <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Swahili_language" title="Swahili language">Swahili</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Makhuwa_language" title="Makhuwa language">Makhuwa</a> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sena_language" title="Sena language">Sena</a>. The country's population of around 24 million is composed overwhelmingly of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bantu_peoples" title="Bantu peoples">Bantu people</a>. The largest religion in Mozambique is Christianity, with significant minorities following Islam and African traditional religions. Mozambique is a member of the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/African_Union" title="African Union">African Union</a>, the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Commonwealth_of_Nations" title="Commonwealth of Nations">Commonwealth of Nations</a>, the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Community_of_Portuguese_Language_Countries" title="Community of Portuguese Language Countries">Community of Portuguese Language Countries</a>, the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Latin_Union" title="Latin Union">Latin Union</a>, the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Non-Aligned_Movement" title="Non-Aligned Movement">Non-Aligned Movement</a> and the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Southern_African_Development_Community" title="Southern African Development Community">Southern African Development Community</a>, and an observer at <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Organisation_internationale_de_la_Francophonie" title="Organisation internationale de la Francophonie">La Francophonie</a>.</p>
