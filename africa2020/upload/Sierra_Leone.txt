href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/West_Africa" title="West Africa">West Africa</a>. It is bordered by <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guinea" title="Guinea">Guinea</a> in the north-east, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Liberia" title="Liberia">Liberia</a> in the south-east, and the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic Ocean</a> in the south-west. Sierra Leone has a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tropical_climate" title="Tropical climate">tropical climate</a>, with a diverse environment ranging from <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Savannah" title="Savannah" class="mw-redirect">savannah</a> to <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rainforests" title="Rainforests" class="mw-redirect">rainforests</a>. Sierra Leone has a total area of 71,740&#160;km<sup>2</sup> (27,699&#160;sq&#160;mi)<sup id="cite_ref-Sierra_Leone_7-0" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sierra_Leone#cite_note-Sierra_Leone-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup> and an estimated population of 6 million (2011 United Nations estimate).<sup id="cite_ref-Sierra_Leone_profile_8-0" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sierra_Leone#cite_note-Sierra_Leone_profile-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Sierra_Leone_Geography_9-0" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sierra_Leone#cite_note-Sierra_Leone_Geography-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup> <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Freetown" title="Freetown">Freetown</a> is the capital, largest city, and its economic and political centre. <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bo,_Sierra_Leone" title="Bo, Sierra Leone">Bo</a> is the second largest city and second major economic center in the country. The country is divided into four geographical regions: the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Northern_Province,_Sierra_Leone" title="Northern Province, Sierra Leone">Northern Province</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eastern_Province,_Sierra_Leone" title="Eastern Province, Sierra Leone">Eastern Province</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Southern_Province,_Sierra_Leone" title="Southern Province, Sierra Leone">Southern Province</a> and the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Western_Area" title="Western Area">Western Area</a>, which are further divided into <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Districts_of_Sierra_Leone" title="Districts of Sierra Leone">fourteen districts</a>.</p>
<p>About sixteen <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ethnic_groups" title="Ethnic groups" class="mw-redirect">ethnic groups</a> inhabit Sierra Leone, each with their own language and custom. The two largest and most influential are the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Temne_people" title="Temne people">Temne</a> and the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mende_people" title="Mende people">Mende people</a>. The Temne are predominantly found in the north of the country, while the Mende are predominant in the south-east. Although English is the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Official_language" title="Official language">official language</a> spoken at schools and government administration, the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Krio_language" title="Krio language">Krio language</a> is the most widely spoken language in the country and unites all the different ethnic groups in the country, especially in their trade and social interaction with each other.</p>
<p>Sierra Leone is a predominantly <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Muslim" title="Muslim">Muslim</a> country,<sup id="cite_ref-r1_10-0" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sierra_Leone#cite_note-r1-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sierra_Leone#cite_note-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sierra_Leone#cite_note-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup> though with an influential <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christian" title="Christian">Christian</a> minority. Sierra Leone is regarded as one of the most religiously tolerant nations in the world. Muslims and Christians collaborate and interact with each other peacefully. Religious violence is very rare in the country.</p>
<p>Sierra Leone has relied on mining, especially <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diamonds" title="Diamonds" class="mw-redirect">diamonds</a>, for its economic base. It is also among the largest producers of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Titanium" title="Titanium">titanium</a> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bauxite" title="Bauxite">bauxite</a>, a major producer of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gold" title="Gold">gold</a>, and has one of the world's largest deposits of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rutile" title="Rutile">rutile</a>. Sierra Leone is home to the third-largest natural harbour in the world. Despite exploitation of this natural wealth, 70% of its people live in <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Poverty" title="Poverty">poverty</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sierra_Leone#cite_note-13"><span>[</span>13<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Sierra Leone became independent in 1961. <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Government_corruption" title="Government corruption" class="mw-redirect">Government corruption</a> and mismanagement of the country's natural resources contributed to the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sierra_Leone_Civil_War" title="Sierra Leone Civil War">Sierra Leone Civil War</a> (1991 to 2002), which over more than a decade devastated the country. It left more than 50,000 people dead, much of the country's infrastructure destroyed, and over two million people displaced as <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Refugees" title="Refugees" class="mw-redirect">refugees</a> in neighbouring countries.</p>
<p>More recently, the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2014_Ebola_outbreak" title="2014 Ebola outbreak" class="mw-redirect">2014 Ebola outbreak</a> threatens to lead the country into a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Humanitarian_crisis" title="Humanitarian crisis">humanitarian crisis</a> situation and a negative spiral of weaker economic growth. The country has an extremely <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_countries_by_life_expectancy" title="List of countries by life expectancy">low life expectancy</a>.</p>
