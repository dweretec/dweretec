<p><b>Tourism</b> is travel for <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Recreation" title="Recreation">recreational</a>, leisure or business purposes. The <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_Tourism_Organization" title="World Tourism Organization">World Tourism Organization</a> defines <b>tourists</b> as people who "travel to and stay in places outside their usual environment for more than twenty-four (24) hours and not more than one consecutive year for leisure, business and other purposes not related to the exercise of an activity remunerated from within the place visited".<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tourism#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> Tourism has become a popular global leisure activity. In 2008, there were over 922 million international tourist arrivals, with a growth of 1.9% as compared to 2007. International tourism receipts grew to <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">US$</a>944 billion (<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Euro" title="Euro">euro</a> 642 billion) in 2008, corresponding to an increase in <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Real_versus_nominal_value" title="Real versus nominal value">real terms</a> of 1.8%.<sup id="cite_ref-WTOjune09_0-1" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tourism#cite_note-WTOjune09-0"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>As a result of the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Late-2000s_recession" title="Late-2000s recession">Late-2000s recession</a>, international <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Travel_behavior" title="Travel behavior">travel demand</a> suffered a strong slowdown beginning in June 2008, with growth in international tourism arrivals worldwide falling to 2% during the boreal summer months,<sup id="cite_ref-WTO2008_3-0" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tourism#cite_note-WTO2008-3"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> and this negative trend intensified as international tourist arrivals fell by 8% during the first four months of 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-WTOjune09_0-2" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tourism#cite_note-WTOjune09-0"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> Thereafter this declining trend was exacerbated in some regions due to the outbreak of the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2009_flu_pandemic" title="2009 flu pandemic">influenza AH1N1 virus</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-WTOjune09_0-3" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tourism#cite_note-WTOjune09-0"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Tourism is vital for many countries, such as the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Arab_Emirates" title="United Arab Emirates">U.A.E</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egypt</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greece" title="Greece">Greece</a> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thailand" title="Thailand">Thailand</a>, and many <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Island_nation" title="Island nation" class="mw-redirect">island nations</a>, such as <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Bahamas" title="The Bahamas">The Bahamas</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fiji" title="Fiji">Fiji</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maldives" title="Maldives">Maldives</a> and the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Seychelles" title="Seychelles">Seychelles</a>, due to the large intake of money for businesses with their <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Goods_and_services" title="Goods and services">goods and services</a> and the opportunity for employment in the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tertiary_sector_of_the_economy" title="Tertiary sector of the economy">service industries</a> associated with tourism. These service industries include transportation services, such as airlines, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cruise_ship" title="Cruise ship">cruise ships</a> and taxis, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hospitality_service" title="Hospitality service">hospitality services</a>, such as accommodations, including hotels and resorts, and entertainment venues, such as <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amusement_park" title="Amusement park">amusement parks</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Casino" title="Casino">casinos</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shopping_mall" title="Shopping mall">shopping malls</a>, various <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Music_venue" title="Music venue">music venues</a> and the theatre.</p>
