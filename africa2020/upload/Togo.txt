English">/</a></span></span>), officially the <b>Togolese Republic</b> (<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>: <span lang="fr"><i>République Togolaise</i></span>), is a country in <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/West_Africa" title="West Africa">West Africa</a> bordered by <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ghana" title="Ghana">Ghana</a> to the west, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Benin" title="Benin">Benin</a> to the east and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Burkina_Faso" title="Burkina It extends south to the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gulf_of_Guinea" title="Gulf of Guinea">Gulf of Guinea</a>, where its <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Capital_city" title="Capital city">capital</a> <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lom%C3%A9" title="Lomé">Lomé</a> is located. Togo covers an area of approximately 57,000 square kilometres (22,000&#160;sq&#160;mi) with a population of approximately 6.7 million.</p>
<p>Togo is a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tropical" title="Tropical" class="mw-redirect">tropical</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sub-Saharan" title="Sub-Saharan" class="mw-redirect">sub-Saharan</a> nation, highly dependent on <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Agriculture" title="Agriculture">agriculture</a>, with a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate" title="Climate">climate</a> that provides good growing seasons. Togo is one of the smallest countries in all of Africa. The official language is French, with many other languages spoken in Togo, particularly those of the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gbe_languages" title="Gbe languages">Gbe family</a>. The largest <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Religious_group" title="Religious group" class="mw-redirect">religious group</a> in Togo are those with indigenous beliefs, and there are significant <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christian" title="Christian">Christian</a> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Muslim" title="Muslim">Muslim</a> minorities. Togo is a member of the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/African_Union" title="African Union">African Union</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Organisation_of_Islamic_Cooperation" title="Organisation of Islamic Cooperation">Organisation of Islamic Cooperation</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Atlantic_Peace_and_Cooperation_Zone" title="South Atlantic Peace and Cooperation Zone">South Atlantic Peace and Cooperation Zone</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/La_Francophonie" title="La Francophonie" class="mw-redirect">La Francophonie</a> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Economic_Community_of_West_African_States" title="Economic Community of West African States">Economic Community of West African States</a>.</p>
<p>From the 11th to the 16th century, various tribes entered the region from all directions. From the 16th century to the 18th century, the coastal region was a major trading centre for Europeans in search of slaves, earning Togo and the surrounding region the name "The <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Slave_Coast" title="Slave Coast">Slave Coast</a>". In 1884, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/German_Empire" title="German Empire">Germany</a> declared <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Togoland" title="Togoland">Togoland</a> a protectorate. After <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a>, rule over Togo was transferred to France. Togo gained its independence from France in 1960.<sup id="cite_ref-cia_2-1" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Togo#cite_note-cia-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In 1967, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gnassingb%C3%A9_Eyad%C3%A9ma" title="Gnassingbé Eyadéma">Gnassingbé Eyadéma</a> led a successful military coup, after which he became president. At the time of his death in 2005, Eyadéma was the longest-serving leader in modern African history, after having been president for 38 years.<sup id="cite_ref-Obituary_7-0" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Togo#cite_note-Obituary-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup> In 2005, his son <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Faure_Gnassingb%C3%A9" title="Faure Gnassingbé">Faure Gnassingbé</a> was elected president.</p>
