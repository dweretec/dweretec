<p><b>Libya</b> (<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arabic_language" title="Arabic language">Arabic</a>: <span lang="ar" dir="rtl"><span style="font-size: 120%;">‏ليبيا</span></span>‎, <i><span title="DIN 31635 Arabic" class="Unicode" style="white-space:normal; text-decoration: none">Lībyā</span></i>; <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Berber_language" title="Berber language" class="mw-redirect">Berber</a>: <b>Libya</b>, <b>ⵍⵉⴱⵢⴰ</b>), officially the <b>State of Libya</b>,<sup id="cite_ref-Herald_StateofLibya_6-0" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Libya#cite_note-Herald_StateofLibya-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-state-libya_7-0" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Libya#cite_note-state-libya-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup> is a country in the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maghreb" title="Maghreb">Maghreb</a> region of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/North_Africa" title="North Africa">North Africa</a> bordered by the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mediterranean_Sea" title="Mediterranean Sea">Mediterranean Sea</a> to the north, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egypt</a> to the east, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sudan" title="Sudan">Sudan</a> to the southeast, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chad" title="Chad">Chad</a> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Niger" title="Niger">Niger</a> to the south, and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Algeria" title="Algeria">Algeria</a> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tunisia" title="Tunisia">Tunisia</a> to the west. The three traditional parts of the country are <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tripolitania" title="Tripolitania">Tripolitania</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fezzan" title="Fezzan">Fezzan</a> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyrenaica" title="Cyrenaica">Cyrenaica</a>. With an area of almost 1.8&#160;million square kilometres (700,000&#160;sq&#160;mi), Libya is the fourth largest country in Africa, and is the 17th <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_countries_and_outlying_territories_by_total_area" title="List of countries and outlying territories by total area" class="mw-redirect">largest country in the world</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Libya#cite_note-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup> Libya has the 10th-largest proven <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oil_reserves" title="Oil reserves">oil reserves</a> of any country in the world.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Libya#cite_note-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The largest city and capital, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tripoli" title="Tripoli">Tripoli</a>, is located in western Libya and contains over one million<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Libya#cite_note-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup> of Libya's six million people. The other large city is <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Benghazi" title="Benghazi">Benghazi</a>, which is located in eastern Libya.</p>
<p>Libya has been inhabited by <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Berber_people" title="Berber people" class="mw-redirect">Berbers</a> since the late Bronze Age. The <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phoenicia" title="Phoenicia">Phoenicians</a> established trading posts in western Libya, and Ancient Greek colonists established city-states in eastern Libya. Libya was variously ruled by <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Persia" title="Persia" class="mw-redirect">Persians</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egyptians</a> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ptolemaic_Kingdom" title="Ptolemaic Kingdom">Greek-Egyptians</a> before becoming a part of the Roman Empire. Libya was an early center of Christianity. After the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fall_of_the_Western_Roman_Empire" title="Fall of the Western Roman Empire">fall of the Western Roman Empire</a>, the area of Libya was mostly occupied by the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vandalic_Kingdom" title="Vandalic Kingdom" class="mw-redirect">Vandals</a> until the 7th century, when invasions brought <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Islam" title="Islam">Islam</a> and Arab colonization. In the sixteenth century, the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanish_Empire" title="Spanish Empire">Spanish Empire</a> and the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Knights_of_St_John" title="Knights of St John" class="mw-redirect">Knights of St John</a> occupied <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tripoli" title="Tripoli">Tripoli</a>, until <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ottoman_Empire" title="Ottoman Empire">Ottoman</a> rule began in 1551. Libya was involved in the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Barbary_Wars" title="Barbary Wars">Barbary Wars</a> of the 18th and 19th centuries. Ottoman rule continued until the twentieth-century <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Italian_Libya" title="Italian Libya">Italian occupation of Libya</a> and large-scale Italian <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Immigration" title="Immigration">immigration</a>. Italian rule ended during the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Second_World_War" title="Second World War" class="mw-redirect">Second World War</a>, during which Libya was an <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/North_African_Campaign" title="North African Campaign">important area of warfare</a>. The Italian population then went into decline. Libya became an independent kingdom in 1951.</p>
<p>In 1969, a military <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coup" title="Coup" class="mw-redirect">coup</a> overthrew <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Idris_of_Libya" title="Idris of Libya">King Idris I</a>, beginning a period of improved living standards and brutal suppression of dissent. Within a year of the coup, the remaining 20,000 Italians and 37,000 Jews were expelled. The most prominent coup conspirator, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Muammar_Gaddafi" title="Muammar Gaddafi">Muammar Gaddafi</a>, was ultimately able to fully concentrate power in his own hands during the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Libyan_Cultural_Revolution" title="Libyan Cultural Revolution" class="mw-redirect">Libyan Cultural Revolution</a>. Muammar Gaddafi remained in power until the Libyan Revolution<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Libya#cite_note-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup> of 2011 overthrew <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Libyan_Arab_Jamahiriya" title="Libyan Arab Jamahiriya" class="mw-redirect">his regime</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-vision_12-0" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Libya#cite_note-vision-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-vision2_13-0" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Libya#cite_note-vision2-13"><span>[</span>13<span>]</span></a></sup> Protests in Benghazi on 15 February 2011 led to clashes with security forces and ultimately escalated into an armed conflict.<sup id="cite_ref-AmnestyRape_14-0" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Libya#cite_note-AmnestyRape-14"><span>[</span>14<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Libya#cite_note-15"><span>[</span>15<span>]</span></a></sup> The <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations_Security_Council" title="United Nations Security Council">United Nations Security Council</a> authorized the use of force in <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations_Security_Council_Resolution_1973" title="United Nations Security Council Resolution 1973">U.N. Security Council Resolution 1973</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Libya#cite_note-16"><span>[</span>16<span>]</span></a></sup> The subsequent <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2011_military_intervention_in_Libya" title="2011 military intervention in Libya">intervention</a> by <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/NATO" title="NATO">NATO</a> and some Arab states ensured the fall of Gaddafi. Since then, Libya has experienced instability and political violence which has severely affected both commerce and oil production.</p>
<p>The internationally recoginized government of Libya is that of the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Council_of_Deputies" title="Council of Deputies">Council of Deputies</a> elected in the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Libyan_House_of_Representatives_election,_2014" title="Libyan House of Representatives election, 2014" class="mw-redirect">June 2014 elections</a>. However, its control of the country is limited by the current <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Libyan_Civil_War_(2014)" title="Libyan Civil War (2014)" class="mw-redirect">civil war</a>. Rival Islamist groups have set up rival governments: the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Libya_Dawn" title="Libya Dawn" class="mw-redirect">Libya Dawn</a> movement's <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_General_National_Congress" title="New General National Congress">new General National Congress</a> in Tripoli,<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Libya#cite_note-17"><span>[</span>17<span>]</span></a></sup> Ansar Al Sharia in <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Benghazi" title="Benghazi">Benghazi</a>, and the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Islamic_State" title="Islamic State" class="mw-redirect">Islamic State</a> in <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Derna" title="Derna" class="mw-disambig">Derna</a>.</p>
<p>The <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_General_National_Congress" title="New General National Congress">new General National Congress</a> purports to be the legal continuation of the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/General_National_Congress" title="General National Congress">General National Congress</a>, which was <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Libyan_General_National_Congress_election,_2012" title="Libyan General National Congress election, 2012">elected</a> in July 2012 and was dissolved following the June 2014 elections, but purportedly reconvened by an Islamist minority of its representatives after the election results were not in their favor.<sup id="cite_ref-rival_18-0" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Libya#cite_note-rival-18"><span>[</span>18<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Libya#cite_note-19"><span>[</span>19<span>]</span></a></sup> The Supreme Court in <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Libya_Dawn" title="Libya Dawn" class="mw-redirect">Libya Dawn</a>-controlled Tripoli declared the recognized government unconstitutional in November 2014,<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Libya#cite_note-20"><span>[</span>20<span>]</span></a></sup> but the internationally recognized government has rejected the ruling as made under threat of violence and continues to be recognized by the international community.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Libya#cite_note-21"><span>[</span>21<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
