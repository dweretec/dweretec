<p><b>Microfinance</b> is a source of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Financial_services" title="Financial services">financial services</a> for entrepreneurs and small businesses lacking access to <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Banking" title="Banking" class="mw-redirect">banking</a> and related services. The two main mechanisms for the delivery of financial services to such clients are: (1) relationship-based banking for individual entrepreneurs and small businesses; and (2) group-based models, where several entrepreneurs come together to apply for loans and other services as a group.</p>
<p>In some regions, for example <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Southern_Africa" title="Southern Africa">Southern Africa</a>, microfinance is used to describe the supply of financial services to low-income employees, which is closer to the retail finance model prevalent in mainstream banking.</p>
<p>For some, microfinance is a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Social_movement" title="Social movement">movement</a> whose object is "a world in which as many poor and near-poor households as possible have permanent access to an appropriate range of high quality financial services, including not just credit but also <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Savings" title="Savings" class="mw-redirect">savings</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Insurance" title="Insurance">insurance</a>, and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Remittances" title="Remittances" class="mw-redirect">fund transfers</a>."<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Micro_Finance#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> Many of those who promote microfinance generally believe that such access will help poor people out of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Poverty" title="Poverty">poverty</a>, including participants in the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Microcredit_Summit_Campaign" title="Microcredit Summit Campaign">Microcredit Summit Campaign</a>. For others, microfinance is a way to promote economic development, employment and growth through the support of micro-entrepreneurs and small businesses.</p>
<p>Microfinance is a broad category of services, which includes <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Microcredit" title="Microcredit">microcredit</a>. Microcredit is provision of credit services to poor clients. <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Microcredit" title="Microcredit">Microcredit</a> is one of the aspects of microfinance and the two are often confused. Critics may attack microcredit while referring to it indiscriminately as either 'microcredit' or 'microfinance'. Due to the broad range of microfinance services, it is difficult to assess impact, and very few studies have tried to assess its full impact.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Micro_Finance#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> Proponents often claim that microfinance lifts people out of poverty, but the evidence is mixed. What it does do, however, is to enhance <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Financial_inclusion" title="Financial inclusion">financial inclusion</a>.</p>
