<p><b>Eritrea</b> (pronounced <span title="Pronunciation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:IPA_for_English" title="Wikipedia:IPA for English">/ˌɛrɪˈtreɪə/</a></span>, <span title="Pronunciation in IPA" class="IPA"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:IPA_for_English" title="Wikipedia:IPA for English">/ˌɛrɪˈtriːə/</a></span>) (<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ge%27ez_alphabet" title="Ge'ez alphabet" class="mw-redirect">Ge'ez</a>: <span class="Unicode">ኤርትራ</span> <span class="Unicode"><i>ʾErtrā</i></span>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arabic_language" title="Arabic language">Arabic</a>: إرتريا <i>Iritriya</i>), officially the <b>State of Eritrea</b>, is a country in the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Horn_of_Africa" title="Horn of Africa">Horn of Africa</a>. It is bordered by <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sudan" title="Sudan">Sudan</a> in the west, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ethiopia" title="Ethiopia">Ethiopia</a> in the south, and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Djibouti" title="Djibouti">Djibouti</a> in the southeast. The east and northeast of the country have an extensive coastline on the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Red_Sea" title="Red Sea">Red Sea</a>, directly across from <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Saudi_Arabia" title="Saudi Arabia">Saudi Arabia</a> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yemen" title="Yemen">Yemen</a>. The <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dahlak_Archipelago" title="Dahlak Archipelago">Dahlak Archipelago</a> and several of the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hanish_Islands" title="Hanish Islands">Hanish Islands</a> are part of Eritrea. Its size is just under 118,000&#160;km<sup>2</sup> (45,560&#160;sq&#160;mi) with an estimated population of 5 million. The capital is <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Asmara" title="Asmara">Asmara</a>.</p>
<p>Eritrea was once home to the Kingdom of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/D%CA%BFmt" title="Dʿmt">Dʿmt</a> and formed part of the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aksumite_Empire" title="Aksumite Empire">Aksumite Empire</a>. Italy conquered Eritrea and the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italian</a> government formally consolidated it into a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Colony" title="Colony">colony</a> on January 1, 1890. In 1936 it became a province of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Italian_East_Africa" title="Italian East Africa">Italian East Africa</a> (Africa Orientale Italiana), along with Ethiopia and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Italian_Somaliland" title="Italian Somaliland">Italian Somaliland</a>. The British armed forces expelled the Italian armed forces in 1941<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eritrea#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> and took over the administration of the country which had been set up by the Italians. The British continued to administer the territory under a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/UN_Mandate" title="UN Mandate">UN Mandate</a> until 1951 when Eritrea was federated with Ethiopia as per UN resolution 390(A) under the prompting of the United States adopted in December 1950; the resolution was adopted after a referendum to consult the people of Eritrea.</p>
<p>The strategic importance of Eritrea due to its Red Sea coastline and mineral resources was the main cause for the federation with Ethiopia, which was the first step in the annexing of Eritrea as its 14th province in 1962, even though many nations opted for the independence of Eritrea. This was the culmination of a gradual process of takeover by the Ethiopian authorities, a process which included an edict in 1959 establishing the compulsory teaching of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amharic" title="Amharic">Amharic</a>, the main language of Ethiopia, in all Eritrean schools. The lack of regard for the Eritrean population led to an Eritrean independence movement formed in the early 1960s which erupted into a 30-year long <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/War" title="War">war</a> against successive Ethiopian governments that ended in 1991. Following a UN-supervised referendum in Eritrea dubbed <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/UNOVER" title="UNOVER" class="mw-redirect">UNOVER</a> in which the Eritrean people overwhelmingly voted for independence from Ethiopia, Eritrea declared its independence and gained international recognition in 1993.<sup id="cite_ref-Britannica_Eritrea-page20_3-0" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eritrea#cite_note-Britannica_Eritrea-page20-3"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a> is used in the government's international communication and is the language of instruction in all formal education beyond the fifth grade.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eritrea#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Eritrea is a single-party state - while its constitution, adopted in 1997, stipulates that the state is a presidential republic with a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Unicameral" title="Unicameral" class="mw-redirect">unicameral</a> <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Parliamentary_system" title="Parliamentary system">parliamentary democracy</a>, it has yet to be implemented. According to the government<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eritrea#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup>, this is due to the prevailing border conflict with Ethiopia which began in May 1998.</p>
