country">landlocked country</a> in the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/African_Great_Lakes" title="African Great Lakes">African Great Lakes</a> region of <a It is also sometimes considered part of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Central_Africa" title="Central Africa">Central Africa</a>. Burundi's capital is <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bujumbura" title="Bujumbura">Bujumbura</a>. Although the country is landlocked, much of the southwestern border is adjacent to <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lake_Tanganyika" title="Lake Tanganyika">Lake Tanganyika</a>.</p>
<p>The <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Great_Lakes_Twa" title="Great Lakes Twa">Twa</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hutu" title="Hutu">Hutu</a> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tutsi" title="Tutsi">Tutsi</a> peoples have lived in Burundi for at least five hundred years. For more than 200 years, Burundi had an indigenous <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kingdom_of_Burundi" title="Kingdom of Burundi">kingdom</a>. At the beginning of the twentieth century, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/German_Empire" title="German Empire">Germany</a> colonized the region. After the First World War and its defeat, it ceded the territory to <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Belgium" title="Belgium">Belgium</a>. The Belgians ruled Burundi and Rwanda as a European colony known as <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ruanda-Urundi" title="Ruanda-Urundi">Ruanda-Urundi</a>. Their intervention exacerbated social differences between the Tutsi and Hutu, which contributed to political unrest in the region. There was civil war in Burundi as it fought for independence in the middle of the twentieth century. Presently, Burundi is governed as a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Presidential_system" title="Presidential system">presidential</a> <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Representative_democracy" title="Representative democracy">representative democratic</a> <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Republic" title="Republic">republic</a>.</p>
<p>Burundi is one of the five <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_countries_by_GDP_(PPP)_per_capita" title="List of countries by GDP (PPP) per capita">poorest countries in the world</a>. It has one of the lowest <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Per_capita_GDP" title="Per capita GDP" class="mw-redirect">per capita GDPs</a> of any nation in the world.<sup id="cite_ref-xlix_8-0" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Burundi#cite_note-xlix-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup> The country has suffered from warfare, corruption and poor access to education. Burundi is densely populated and has had substantial <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Emigration" title="Emigration">emigration</a> as young people seek opportunities elsewhere. According to a 2012 <i>DHL Global Connectedness Index</i>, Burundi is the least globalized of 140 surveyed countries.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Burundi#cite_note-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>According to the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Global_Hunger_Index" title="Global Hunger Index">Global Hunger Index</a> of 2013, Burundi has an indicator ratio of 38.8, earning the nation the distinction of being the hungriest country in the world in terms of percentage.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Burundi#cite_note-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
