href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Algeria" title="Algeria">Algeria</a> to the west, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Libya" title="Libya">Libya</a> to the southeast, and the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mediterranean_Sea" title="Mediterranean Sea">Mediterranean Sea</a> to the north and east.</p>
<p>As of 2013, its population is estimated at just under 10.8 million.<sup id="cite_ref-ins_5-1" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tunisia#cite_note-ins-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> Its name is derived from its capital city, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tunis" title="Tunis">Tunis</a>, located on the country's northeast coast.</p>
<p>Geographically, Tunisia contains the eastern end of the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Atlas_Mountains" title="Atlas Mountains">Atlas Mountains</a> and the northern reaches of the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sahara" title="Sahara">Sahara</a> desert. Much of the rest of the country's land is fertile soil. Its 1,300 kilometres (810&#160;mi) of coastline includes the African conjunction of the western and eastern parts of the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mediterranean_Basin" title="Mediterranean Basin">Mediterranean Basin</a> and, by means of the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sicilian_Strait" title="Sicilian Strait" class="mw-redirect">Sicilian Strait</a> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sardinia" title="Sardinia">Sardinian</a> Channel, features the African mainland's second and third nearest points to Europe after <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gibraltar" title="Gibraltar">Gibraltar</a>.</p>
<p>Tunisia has a high human development index.<sup id="cite_ref-HDI_8-1" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tunisia#cite_note-HDI-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup> It has an <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/European_Union_Association_Agreement" title="European Union Association Agreement">association agreement</a> with the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a> and is a member of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/La_Francophonie" title="La Francophonie" class="mw-redirect">La Francophonie</a>, the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arab_Maghreb_Union" title="Arab Maghreb Union">Arab Maghreb Union</a>, the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arab_League" title="Arab League">Arab League</a>, and the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/African_Union" title="African Union">African Union</a>. Close relations with Europe &#160;–  in particular <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/France-Tunisia_relations" title="France-Tunisia relations" class="mw-redirect">with France</a><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tunisia#cite_note-13"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Italy-Tunisia_relations" title="Italy-Tunisia relations" class="mw-redirect">with Italy</a><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tunisia#cite_note-14"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tunisia#cite_note-15"><span>[</span>13<span>]</span></a></sup>&#160;–  have been forged through economic cooperation, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Privatisation" title="Privatisation" class="mw-redirect">privatisation</a> and industrial modernization.</p>
<p>In 2011, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tunisian_Revolution" title="Tunisian Revolution">a revolution</a> resulted in the overthrow of the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Autocracy" title="Autocracy">autocratic</a> President <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zine_El_Abidine_Ben_Ali" title="Zine El Abidine Ben Ali">Zine El Abidine Ben Ali</a> followed by the country's first <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tunisian_Constituent_Assembly_election,_2011" title="Tunisian Constituent Assembly election, 2011">free elections</a>. Since then, Tunisia has been <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Democratic_consolidation" title="Democratic consolidation">consolidating democracy</a>. The country held its first Parliamentary elections since the 2011 <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arab_Spring" title="Arab Spring">Arab Spring</a> on October 26, 2014,<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tunisia#cite_note-16"><span>[</span>14<span>]</span></a></sup> and its Presidentials on November 23, 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tunisia#cite_note-17"><span>[</span>15<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
