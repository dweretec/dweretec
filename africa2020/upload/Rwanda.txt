href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>: <span lang="fr"><i>République du Rwanda</i></span>), is a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sovereign_state" title="Sovereign state">sovereign state</a> in <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Central_Africa" title="Central Africa">central</a> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/East_Africa" title="East Africa">east Africa</a>. Located a few degrees south of the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Equator" title="Equator">Equator</a>, Rwanda is bordered by <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Uganda" title="Uganda">Uganda</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tanzania" title="Tanzania">Tanzania</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Burundi" title="Burundi">Burundi</a> and the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Democratic_Republic_of_the_Congo" title="Democratic Republic of the Congo">Democratic Republic of the Congo</a>. Rwanda is in the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/African_Great_Lakes" title="African Great Lakes">African Great Lakes</a> region and is highly elevated; its geography dominated by mountains in the west and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Savanna" title="Savanna">savanna</a> to the east, with numerous lakes throughout the country. The climate of the country is temperate to subtropical, with two rainy seasons and two dry seasons each year.</p>
<p>The population is young and predominantly rural, with a density among the highest in Africa. Rwandans are composed of three ethnic groups: the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hutu" title="Hutu">Hutu</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tutsi" title="Tutsi">Tutsi</a> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Great_Lakes_Twa" title="Great Lakes Twa">Twa</a>. The Twa are a forest-dwelling <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pygmy_peoples" title="Pygmy peoples">pygmy people</a> descended from Rwanda's earliest inhabitants. Scholars disagree on the origins of and differences between the Hutu and Tutsi; some believe differences are derived from former social <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Caste" title="Caste">castes</a>, while others view them as being ethnicities or tribes. Christianity is the largest religion in the country; the principal language is <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kinyarwanda" title="Kinyarwanda">Kinyarwanda</a>, spoken by most Rwandans, with French and English serving as official languages. Rwanda has a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Presidential_system" title="Presidential system">presidential system</a> of government. The president is <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paul_Kagame" title="Paul Kagame">Paul Kagame</a> of the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rwandan_Patriotic_Front" title="Rwandan Patriotic Front">Rwandan Patriotic Front</a> (RPF), who took office in 2000. Rwanda today has low corruption compared with neighbouring countries, although human rights organisations report suppression of opposition groups, intimidation and restrictions on freedom of speech. The country has been governed by a strict administrative hierarchy since precolonial times; there are five <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Provinces_of_Rwanda" title="Provinces of Rwanda">provinces</a> delineated by borders drawn in 2006. Rwanda has the world's highest proportion of females in government positions in proportion to the population.</p>
<p><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hunter_gatherer" title="Hunter gatherer" class="mw-redirect">Hunter gatherers</a> settled the territory in the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stone_age" title="Stone age" class="mw-redirect">stone</a> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iron_age" title="Iron age" class="mw-redirect">iron</a> ages, followed later by <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bantu_peoples" title="Bantu peoples">Bantu peoples</a>. The population coalesced first into <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Clans_of_Rwanda" title="Clans of Rwanda" class="mw-redirect">clans</a> and then into kingdoms. The <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kingdom_of_Rwanda" title="Kingdom of Rwanda">Kingdom of Rwanda</a> dominated from the mid-eighteenth century, with the Tutsi kings conquering others militarily, centralising power, and later enacting anti-Hutu policies. <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/German_colonial_empire" title="German colonial empire">Germany</a> colonised Rwanda in 1884 as part of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/German_East_Africa" title="German East Africa">German East Africa</a>, followed by <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Belgian_colonial_empire" title="Belgian colonial empire">Belgium</a>, which invaded in 1916 during <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a>. Both European nations ruled through the kings and perpetuated a pro-Tutsi policy. The Hutu population revolted in 1959. They massacred numerous Tutsi and ultimately established an independent, Hutu-dominated state in 1962. The Tutsi-led <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rwandan_Patriotic_Front" title="Rwandan Patriotic Front">Rwandan Patriotic Front</a> launched a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rwandan_Civil_War" title="Rwandan Civil War">civil war</a> in 1990. Social tensions erupted in the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rwandan_Genocide" title="Rwandan Genocide">1994 genocide</a>, in which Hutu extremists killed an estimated 500,000 to 1 million Tutsi and moderate Hutu. The RPF ended the genocide with a military victory.</p>
<p>Rwanda's economy suffered heavily during the 1994 Rwandan Genocide, but has since strengthened. The economy is based mostly on <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Subsistence_agriculture" title="Subsistence agriculture">subsistence agriculture</a>. Coffee and tea are the major cash crops for export. <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tourism_in_Rwanda" title="Tourism in Rwanda">Tourism</a> is a fast-growing sector and is now the country's leading foreign exchange earner. Rwanda is one of only two countries in which <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mountain_gorilla" title="Mountain gorilla">mountain gorillas</a> can be visited safely, and visitors are prepared to pay high prices for gorilla tracking permits. Music and dance are an integral part of Rwandan culture, particularly drums and the highly choreographed <i>intore</i> dance. Traditional arts and crafts are produced throughout the country, including <i><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Imigongo" title="Imigongo">imigongo</a></i>, a unique cow dung art.</p>
