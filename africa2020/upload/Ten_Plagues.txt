			<p>The <b>Plagues of Egypt</b> (<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hebrew_language" title="Hebrew language">Hebrew</a>: <span style="white-space:nowrap;">מכות מצרים,</span> <i><span class="Unicode">Makot Mitzrayim</span></i>), the <b>Biblical Plagues</b> or the <b>Ten Plagues</b> (<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hebrew_language" title="Hebrew language">Hebrew</a>: <span style="white-space:nowrap;">עשר המכות,</span> <i><span class="Unicode">Eser Ha-Makot</span></i>) are the ten calamities imposed upon <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ancient_Egypt" title="Ancient Egypt">Egypt</a> by <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Names_of_God_in_Judaism" title="Names of God in Judaism">God</a> in the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bible" title="Bible">Bible</a> (as recounted in the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Book_of_Exodus" title="Book of Exodus">Book of Exodus</a>, Chapters 7–12), to convince <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pharaoh_of_the_Exodus" title="Pharaoh of the Exodus">Pharaoh</a><sup id="cite_ref-0" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ten_Plagues#cite_note-0"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> to let the poorly treated <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Israelite" title="Israelite" class="mw-redirect">Israelite</a> slaves go. The Plagues of Egypt are recognized by <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jews" title="Jews" class="mw-redirect">Jews</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christians" title="Christians" class="mw-redirect">Christians</a>, and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Muslims" title="Muslims" class="mw-redirect">Muslims</a>.</p>
