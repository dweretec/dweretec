<p><b>South Africa</b>, officially the <b>Republic of South Africa</b> (<b>RSA</b>), is a country located in <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Southern_Africa" title="Southern Africa">Southern Africa</a>. It has 2,798 kilometres (1,739&#160;mi) of coastline that stretches along the South Atlantic and Indian oceans.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Africa#cite_note-10"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Africa#cite_note-11"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-safacts_12-0" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Africa#cite_note-safacts-12"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup> To the north lie the neighbouring countries of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Namibia" title="Namibia">Namibia</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Botswana" title="Botswana">Botswana</a> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zimbabwe" title="Zimbabwe">Zimbabwe</a>; to the east are <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mozambique" title="Mozambique">Mozambique</a> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Swaziland" title="Swaziland">Swaziland</a>; and within it lies <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lesotho" title="Lesotho">Lesotho</a>, an <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Enclave_and_exclave" title="Enclave and exclave">enclave</a> surrounded by South African territory.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Africa#cite_note-13"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup> South Africa is the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_countries_and_outlying_territories_by_total_area" title="List of countries and outlying territories by total area" class="mw-redirect">25th-largest country</a> in the world by land area, and with close to <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Demographics_of_South_Africa" title="Demographics of South Africa">53 million people</a>, is the world's <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_countries_by_population" title="List of countries by population" class="mw-redirect">25th-most populous nation</a>.</p>
<p>South Africa is a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Multiethnic_society" title="Multiethnic society" class="mw-redirect">multiethnic society</a> encompassing a wide variety of cultures, languages, and religions. Its pluralistic makeup is reflected in the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Constitution_of_South_Africa" title="Constitution of South Africa">constitution</a>'s recognition of 11 official languages, which is among the highest number of any country in the world.<sup id="cite_ref-safacts_12-1" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Africa#cite_note-safacts-12"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup> Two of these languages are of European origin: <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Afrikaans" title="Afrikaans">Afrikaans</a> developed from <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dutch_language" title="Dutch language">Dutch</a> and serves as the first language of most <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/White_South_African" title="White South African">white</a> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coloured" title="Coloured">coloured</a> South Africans, based on history; <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_African_English" title="South African English">English</a> reflects the legacy of British colonialism, and is commonly used in public and commercial life, though it is fourth-ranked as a spoken first language.<sup id="cite_ref-safacts_12-2" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Africa#cite_note-safacts-12"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>About 80 percent of South Africans are of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sub-Saharan_Africa" title="Sub-Saharan Africa">sub-Saharan African</a> ancestry,<sup id="cite_ref-census2011-inbrief_6-1" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Africa#cite_note-census2011-inbrief-6"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> divided among a variety of ethnic groups speaking different <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bantu_languages" title="Bantu languages">Bantu languages</a>, nine of which have official status.<sup id="cite_ref-safacts_12-3" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Africa#cite_note-safacts-12"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup> The remaining population consists of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>'s largest communities of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ethnic_groups_in_Europe" title="Ethnic groups in Europe">European</a> (<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/White_South_Africans" title="White South Africans" class="mw-redirect">white</a>), <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Asian_people" title="Asian people">Asian</a> (<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Asian_South_Africans" title="Asian South Africans" class="mw-redirect">Indian</a>), and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Multiracial" title="Multiracial">multiracial</a> (<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coloured" title="Coloured">coloured</a>) ancestry. Since 1994, all ethnic and linguistic groups have had political representation in the country's <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Liberal_democracy" title="Liberal democracy">constitutional democracy</a>, which comprises a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Parliamentary_republic" title="Parliamentary republic">parliamentary republic</a> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Provinces_of_South_Africa" title="Provinces of South Africa">nine provinces</a>. South Africa is often referred to as the "<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rainbow_Nation" title="Rainbow Nation" class="mw-redirect">Rainbow Nation</a>," a term coined by Archbishop <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Desmond_Tutu" title="Desmond Tutu">Desmond Tutu</a> and later adopted by then-President <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nelson_Mandela" title="Nelson Mandela">Nelson Mandela</a> as a metaphor to describe the country's newly developing multicultural diversity in the wake of segregationist <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Apartheid" title="Apartheid">apartheid</a> ideology.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Africa#cite_note-14"><span>[</span>13<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The country is one of the few in Africa never to have had a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coup_d%27%C3%A9tat" title="Coup d'état">coup d'état</a>, and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elections_in_South_Africa" title="Elections in South Africa">regular elections</a> have been held for almost a century. But the vast majority of black South Africans were not <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Enfranchised" title="Enfranchised" class="mw-redirect">enfranchised</a> until 1994. During the 20th century, the black majority sought to recover its rights from the dominant white minority, with this struggle playing a large role in the country's recent history and politics. The <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_Party_(South_Africa)" title="National Party (South Africa)">National Party</a> imposed <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Apartheid" title="Apartheid">apartheid</a> in 1948, institutionalizing previous racial segregation. After a long and sometimes violent struggle by the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/African_National_Congress" title="African National Congress">African National Congress</a> and other anti-apartheid activists, discriminatory laws began to be repealed or abolished from 1990 onwards.</p>
<p>South Africa is ranked as an upper-middle income economy by the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_Bank" title="World Bank">World Bank</a>, and is considered to be a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Newly_industrialized_country" title="Newly industrialized country">newly industrialised country</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Africa#cite_note-15"><span>[</span>14<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Waugh2000_16-0" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Africa#cite_note-Waugh2000-16"><span>[</span>15<span>]</span></a></sup> Its economy is the second-largest in Africa, and the 34th-largest in the world.<sup id="cite_ref-imf2_7-4" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Africa#cite_note-imf2-7"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup> In terms of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Purchasing_power_parity" title="Purchasing power parity">purchasing power parity</a>, South Africa has the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_countries_by_GDP_(PPP)_per_capita" title="List of countries by GDP (PPP) per capita">seventh-highest per capita income</a> in Africa. But poverty and inequality remain widespread, with about a quarter of the population unemployed and living on less than US$1.25 a day.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Africa#cite_note-17"><span>[</span>16<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Africa#cite_note-18"><span>[</span>17<span>]</span></a></sup> Nevertheless, South Africa has been identified as a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Middle_power" title="Middle power">middle power</a> in international affairs, and maintains significant regional influence.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Africa#cite_note-19"><span>[</span>18<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Lynch2010_20-0" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Africa#cite_note-Lynch2010-20"><span>[</span>19<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
