<p>A <b>school</b> (from <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greek_language" title="Greek language">Greek</a> <span lang="grc" xml:lang="grc">σχολή</span> <i>(<span lang="grc-Latn" xml:lang="grc-Latn">scholē</span></i>), originally meaning "<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Leisure" title="Leisure">leisure</a>", and also "that in which leisure is employed", "school"),<sup id="cite_ref-0" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/School#cite_note-0"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> is an <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Institution" title="Institution">institution</a> designed to allow and encourage <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Student" title="Student">students</a> (or "pupils") to <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Education" title="Education">learn</a>, under the supervision of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Teacher" title="Teacher">teachers</a>. Most countries have systems of formal <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Education" title="Education">education</a>, which is commonly <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Compulsory_education" title="Compulsory education">compulsory</a>. In these systems, students progress through a series of schools. The names for these schools vary by country (discussed in the <i>Regional</i> section below), but generally include <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Primary_school" title="Primary school">primary school</a> for young children and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Secondary_school" title="Secondary school">secondary school</a> for teenagers who have completed primary education.</p>
<p>In addition to these core schools, students in a given country may also have access to and attend schools both before and after primary and secondary education. <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kindergarten" title="Kindergarten">Kindergarten</a> or <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Education" title="Education">pre-school</a> provide some schooling to very young children (typically ages 3-5). <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University" title="University">University</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vocational_school" title="Vocational school">vocational school</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/College" title="College">college</a> or <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Seminary" title="Seminary">seminary</a> may be available after (or in lieu of) secondary school. A school may also be dedicated to one particular field, such as a school of economics or a school of dance. <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alternative_school" title="Alternative school">Alternative schools</a> may provide nontraditional curriculum and methods.</p>
<p>There are also non-government schools, called private schools. Private schools may be for children with special needs when the government does not supply for them; religious, such as <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christian_Schools" title="Christian Schools" class="mw-redirect">Christian Schools</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Khalsa_Schools&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Khalsa Schools (page does not exist)">Khalsa Schools</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Torah_Schools&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Torah Schools (page does not exist)">Torah Schools</a> and others; or schools that have a higher standard of education or seek to foster other personal achievements. Schools for adults include institutions of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Corporate_training" title="Corporate training" class="mw-redirect">corporate training</a> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Military_education_and_training" title="Military education and training">Military education and training</a>.</p>
<p>In <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Homeschooling" title="Homeschooling">homeschooling</a> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/E-learning" title="E-learning" class="mw-redirect">online schools</a>, teaching and learning take place outside of a traditional school building.</p>
