<p><b>Tunisia</b> (<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arabic_language" title="Arabic language">Arabic</a>: <span lang="ar" xml:lang="ar">تونس</span>‎ <i>Tūnis</i>), officially the <b>Tunisian Republic</b> (<span lang="ar" dir="rtl" xml:lang="ar">الجمهورية التونسية</span>‎ <i>al-Jumhūriyya at-Tūnisiyya</i>), is a country located in <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/North_Africa" title="North Africa">North Africa</a>. It is bordered by <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Algeria" title="Algeria">Algeria</a> to the west and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Libya" title="Libya">Libya</a> to the southeast. Tunisia is located southwest of the island of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sicily" title="Sicily">Sicily</a> and south of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sardinia" title="Sardinia">Sardinia</a>. Its size is almost 165,000&#160;km² with an estimated population of just over 10.3 million. Its name is derived from the capital <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tunis" title="Tunis">Tunis</a>.</p>
<p>Tunisia is the northernmost country on the African continent, and the smallest of the nations situated along the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Atlas_mountains" title="Atlas mountains" class="mw-redirect">Atlas mountain</a> range. Approximately 40% of the country is composed of the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sahara_desert" title="Sahara desert" class="mw-redirect">Sahara desert</a>, with much of the remainder consisting of particularly fertile soil and a 1,300&#160;km coastline. Both played a prominent role in ancient times, first with the famous <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phoenicia" title="Phoenicia">Phoenician</a> city of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carthage" title="Carthage">Carthage</a>, then as the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Africa_Province,_Roman_Empire" title="Africa Province, Roman Empire" class="mw-redirect">Africa Province</a> which was known as the bread basket of the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Roman_Empire" title="Roman Empire">Roman Empire</a>, and then as the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maghreb" title="Maghreb">Maghreb</a> region of various medieval <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Islamic_state" title="Islamic state">Islamic states</a>.</p>
<p>Tunisia consistently ranks at the top among Arab and African nations in reports released by The <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_Economic_Forum" title="World Economic Forum">World Economic Forum</a>.</p>
