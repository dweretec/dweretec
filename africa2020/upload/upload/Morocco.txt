<p><b>Morocco</b>, officially the <b>Kingdom of Morocco</b>,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Morocco#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> is a country located in <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/North_Africa" title="North Africa">North Africa</a> with a population of nearly 32 million and an area just under 447,000&#160;square kilometres (173,000&#160;sq&#160;mi). Its capital is <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rabat" title="Rabat">Rabat</a>, and its largest city is <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Casablanca" title="Casablanca">Casablanca</a>. Morocco has a coast on the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic Ocean</a> that reaches past the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Strait_of_Gibraltar" title="Strait of Gibraltar">Strait of Gibraltar</a> into the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mediterranean_Sea" title="Mediterranean Sea">Mediterranean Sea</a>. It is bordered by <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Algeria" title="Algeria">Algeria</a> to the east, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a> to the north (a water border through the Strait and land borders with three small Spanish <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Enclaves" title="Enclaves" class="mw-redirect">enclaves</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ceuta" title="Ceuta">Ceuta</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Melilla" title="Melilla">Melilla</a>, and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pe%C3%B1%C3%B3n_de_V%C3%A9lez_de_la_Gomera" title="Peñón de Vélez de la Gomera">Peñón de Vélez de la Gomera</a>), and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mauritania" title="Mauritania">Mauritania</a> to the south via its <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Western_Sahara" title="Western Sahara">Western Saharan</a> territories.<sup id="cite_ref-border_5-0" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Morocco#cite_note-border-5"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Several dialects of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arabic" title="Arabic" class="mw-redirect">Arabic</a> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Berber" title="Berber">Berber</a> are spoken in Morocco. However, this linguistic diversity doesn't affect the ethnic situation as the population is largely homogenous.</p>
<p>Morocco is the only country in Africa that is not currently a member of the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/African_Union" title="African Union">African Union</a> and it has shown no interest in joining, due to other African nations recognizing Western Sahara as a sovereign state. However, it is a member of the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arab_League" title="Arab League">Arab League</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arab_Maghreb_Union" title="Arab Maghreb Union">Arab Maghreb Union</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Francophonie" title="Francophonie" class="mw-redirect">Francophonie</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Organisation_of_the_Islamic_Conference" title="Organisation of the Islamic Conference">Organisation of the Islamic Conference</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mediterranean_Dialogue" title="Mediterranean Dialogue">Mediterranean Dialogue</a> group, and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Group_of_77" title="Group of 77">Group of 77</a>. It is also a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Major_non-NATO_ally" title="Major non-NATO ally">major non-NATO ally of the United States</a>.</p>
