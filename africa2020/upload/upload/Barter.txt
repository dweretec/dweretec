<p><b>Barter</b> is a method of exchange by which <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Good_(economics)" title="Good (economics)">goods</a> or <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Service_(economics)" title="Service (economics)">services</a> are directly exchanged for other goods or services without using a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Medium_of_exchange" title="Medium of exchange">medium of exchange</a>, such as <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Money" title="Money">money</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-0" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Barter#cite_note-0"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> It is usually <a href="http://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/bilateral" class="extiw" title="wiktionary:bilateral">bilateral</a>, but may be <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Multilateral" title="Multilateral" class="mw-redirect">multilateral</a>, and usually exists parallel to monetary systems in most developed countries, though to a very limited extent. Barter usually replaces money as the method of exchange in times of monetary crisis, when the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Currency" title="Currency">currency</a> is unstable and devalued by <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hyperinflation" title="Hyperinflation">hyperinflation</a>.</p>
