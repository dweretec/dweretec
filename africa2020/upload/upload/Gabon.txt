<p><b>Gabon</b> (pronounced <span title="Pronunciation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:IPA_for_English" title="Wikipedia:IPA for English">/ɡəˈbɒn/</a></span>; <small>French pronunciation:&#160;</small><span title="Pronunciation in IPA" class="IPA"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:IPA_for_French" title="Wikipedia:IPA for French">[ɡaˈbõ]</a></span>) is a country in west central <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a> sharing borders with the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gulf_of_Guinea" title="Gulf of Guinea">Gulf of Guinea</a> to the west, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Equatorial_Guinea" title="Equatorial Guinea">Equatorial Guinea</a> to the northwest, and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cameroon" title="Cameroon">Cameroon</a> to the north, with the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Republic_of_the_Congo" title="Republic of the Congo">Republic of the Congo</a> curving around the east and south. Its size is almost 270,000&#160;km² with an estimated population of	1,500,000. The capital and largest city is <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Libreville" title="Libreville">Libreville</a>. Since its independence from <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/France" title="France">France</a> on <span class="mw-formatted-date" title="1960-08-17"><span class="mw-formatted-date" title="08-17"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/August_17" title="August 17">August 17</a></span>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1960" title="1960">1960</a></span>, the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Republic" title="Republic">Republic</a> has been ruled by three presidents. In the early 1990s, Gabon introduced a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Multi-party_system" title="Multi-party system">multi-party system</a> and a new <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Democratic" title="Democratic" class="mw-redirect">democratic</a> <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Constitution" title="Constitution">constitution</a> that allowed for a more transparent electoral process and reformed many governmental institutions. The small <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> together with abundant <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Natural_resources" title="Natural resources" class="mw-redirect">natural resources</a> and foreign private <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Investment" title="Investment">investment</a> have helped make Gabon one of the most prosperous countries in the region, with the highest <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Human_Development_Index" title="Human Development Index">HDI</a> in Sub-Saharan Africa.<sup id="cite_ref-hdi_3-0" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gabon#cite_note-hdi-3"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
