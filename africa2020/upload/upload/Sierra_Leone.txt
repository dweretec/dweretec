<p><b>Sierra Leone</b> (<span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">sieɪrə liˈoːn</span>), officially the <b>Republic of Sierra Leone</b>, is a country in <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/West_Africa" title="West Africa">West Africa</a>. It is bordered by <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guinea" title="Guinea">Guinea</a> in the north, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Liberia" title="Liberia">Liberia</a> in the southeast, and the Atlantic Ocean in the southwest. Sierra Leone covers a total area of 71,740&#160;km<sup>2</sup> (27,699&#160;sq&#160;mi) <sup id="cite_ref-Sierra_Leone_1-0" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sierra_Leone#cite_note-Sierra_Leone-1"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> and has a population estimated at 6.4 million. The country has a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tropical_climate" title="Tropical climate">tropical climate</a>, with a diverse environment ranging from <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Savannah" title="Savannah" class="mw-redirect">savannah</a> to <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rainforests" title="Rainforests" class="mw-redirect">rainforests</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Sierra_Leone_Geography_2-0" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sierra_Leone#cite_note-Sierra_Leone_Geography-2"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Freetown" title="Freetown">Freetown</a> is the capital, seat of the central government, and largest city.<sup id="cite_ref-Sierra_Leone_1-1" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sierra_Leone#cite_note-Sierra_Leone-1"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bo,_Sierra_Leone" title="Bo, Sierra Leone">Bo</a> is the second largest city. Other major cities in the country with a population over 100,000 are <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kenema" title="Kenema">Kenema</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Koidu" title="Koidu" class="mw-redirect">Koidu Town</a> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Makeni" title="Makeni">Makeni</a>. The country is home to <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fourah_Bay_College" title="Fourah Bay College">Fourah Bay College</a>, the oldest <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University" title="University">university</a> in West Africa, established in 1827. The <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Njala_University" title="Njala University">Njala University</a> in the country's second largest city of Bo is the other major university in the country. Sierra Leone is home to the third largest <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Natural_harbour" title="Natural harbour" class="mw-redirect">natural harbours</a> in the world, the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Queen_Elizabeth_II_Quay" title="Queen Elizabeth II Quay">Queen Elizabeth II Quay</a> (also known as the QE II Quay and locally as the Deep Water Quay or Government Warf).<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sierra_Leone#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sierra_Leone#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>English is the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Official_language" title="Official language">official language</a> of Sierra Leone<sup id="cite_ref-Sierra_Leone_Overview_5-0" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sierra_Leone#cite_note-Sierra_Leone_Overview-5"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup> spoken at <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/School" title="School">schools</a>, government administration and by the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mass_media" title="Mass media">media</a>. The <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Krio_language" title="Krio language">Krio language</a> (a language derived from English and several African languages and native to the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sierra_Leone_Krio_people" title="Sierra Leone Krio people" class="mw-redirect">Sierra Leone Krio people</a>) is the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_language" title="National language">national language</a> of Sierra Leone and is the most widely spoken language in virtually all parts of Sierra Leone. The Krio language is spoken by 97% of Sierra Leone's population and unites all the different <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ethnic_group" title="Ethnic group">ethnic groups</a>, especially in their trade and interaction with each other.<sup id="cite_ref-https:.2F.2Feprints.soas.ac.uk.2F181_6-0" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sierra_Leone#cite_note-https:.2F.2Feprints.soas.ac.uk.2F181-6"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup>. Sierra Leone is officially home to fifteen ethnic groups, each with its own <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Language" title="Language">language</a> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Costume" title="Costume">costume</a>; however, the two largest are the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mende_people" title="Mende people">Mende</a> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Temne_people" title="Temne people">Temne</a>, each comprises 30% of the population. The Mende are predominantly found in South-eastern Sierra Leone and the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Temne_people" title="Temne people">Temne</a> predominate in Northern Sierra Leone. The two ethnic groups are major rivals, particularly in <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Politics" title="Politics">politics</a>.</p>
<p>Early inhabitants of Sierra Leone included the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sherbro_people" title="Sherbro people">Sherbro</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Temne_people" title="Temne people">Temne</a> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Limba_people_(Sierra_Leone)" title="Limba people (Sierra Leone)">Limba</a>, and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tyra" title="Tyra">Tyra</a> peoples, and later the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mende_people" title="Mende people">Mende</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-Precolonial_tribes_7-0" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sierra_Leone#cite_note-Precolonial_tribes-7"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup> who knew the country as Romarong, and the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kono_people" title="Kono people">Kono</a> who settled in the East of the country.<sup id="cite_ref-Sierra_Leone2_8-0" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sierra_Leone#cite_note-Sierra_Leone2-8"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup> In 1462, it was visited by the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portuguese_people" title="Portuguese people">Portuguese</a> explorer <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pedro_da_Cintra" title="Pedro da Cintra">Pedro da Cintra</a>, who gave it its name Serra de Leão, meaning 'Lion Mountains'.<sup id="cite_ref-Placenames_of_the_World_9-0" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sierra_Leone#cite_note-Placenames_of_the_World-9"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ReferenceA_10-0" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sierra_Leone#cite_note-ReferenceA-10"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Sierra Leone became an important centre of the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Atlantic_slave_trade" title="Atlantic slave trade">transatlantic trade</a> in human beings (i.e., slaves), until 1792 when Freetown was founded by the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sierra_Leone_Company" title="Sierra Leone Company">Sierra Leone Company</a> as a home for formerly <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Slavery" title="Slavery">enslaved</a> <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/African_American" title="African American">African Americans</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-History_of_Sierra_Leone_11-0" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sierra_Leone#cite_note-History_of_Sierra_Leone-11"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup> In 1808, Freetown became a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/British_Empire" title="British Empire">British</a> <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crown_Colony" title="Crown Colony" class="mw-redirect">Crown Colony</a>, and in 1896, the interior of the country became a British <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Protectorate" title="Protectorate">Protectorate</a>;<sup id="cite_ref-Sierra_Leone2_8-1" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sierra_Leone#cite_note-Sierra_Leone2-8"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup> in 1961, the two combined and gained <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Independence" title="Independence">independence</a>.</p>
<p>Over two decades of government neglect of the interior followed by the spilling over of the Liberian conflict into its borders eventually led to the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sierra_Leone_Civil_War" title="Sierra Leone Civil War">Sierra Leone Civil War</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-Causes_of_civil_war_12-0" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sierra_Leone#cite_note-Causes_of_civil_war-12"><span>[</span>13<span>]</span></a></sup> which began in 1991 and was resolved in 2000 after the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations</a> led by Nigeria defeated the rebel forces and restored the civilian government elected in 1998 to Freetown.</p>
<p>Since then, almost 72,500 former combatants have been disarmed<sup id="cite_ref-Conflict_and_Collusion_in_Sierra_Leone_13-0" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sierra_Leone#cite_note-Conflict_and_Collusion_in_Sierra_Leone-13"><span>[</span>14<span>]</span></a></sup> and the country has reestablished a functioning <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Democracy" title="Democracy">democracy</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-CIA_14-0" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sierra_Leone#cite_note-CIA-14"><span>[</span>15<span>]</span></a></sup> The <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Special_Court_for_Sierra_Leone" title="Special Court for Sierra Leone">Special Court for Sierra Leone</a> was set up in 2002 to deal with <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/War_crimes" title="War crimes" class="mw-redirect">war crimes</a> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crimes_against_humanity" title="Crimes against humanity" class="mw-redirect">crimes against humanity</a> committed since 1996.<sup id="cite_ref-About_the_Special_Court_for_Sierra_Leone_15-0" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sierra_Leone#cite_note-About_the_Special_Court_for_Sierra_Leone-15"><span>[</span>16<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Sierra Leone is the lowest ranked country on the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Human_Development_Index" title="Human Development Index">Human Development Index</a> and seventh lowest on the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Human_Poverty_Index" title="Human Poverty Index">Human Poverty Index</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-Sierra_Leone_-_The_Human_Development_Index_16-0" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sierra_Leone#cite_note-Sierra_Leone_-_The_Human_Development_Index-16"><span>[</span>17<span>]</span></a></sup> suffering from endemic <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Political_corruption" title="Political corruption">corruption</a><sup id="cite_ref-Corruption_in_Sierra_Leone_17-0" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sierra_Leone#cite_note-Corruption_in_Sierra_Leone-17"><span>[</span>18<span>]</span></a></sup> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Press_freedom" title="Press freedom" class="mw-redirect">suppression of the press</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Media_body_curbs_press_freedom_18-0" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sierra_Leone#cite_note-Media_body_curbs_press_freedom-18"><span>[</span>19<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
