<p>The <b>Republic of Botswana</b> (<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tswana_language" title="Tswana language">Tswana</a>: <span lang="tn" xml:lang="tn"><i>Lefatshe la Botswana</i></span>) is a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Landlocked" title="Landlocked" class="mw-redirect">landlocked</a> country in <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Southern_Africa" title="Southern Africa">Southern Africa</a>. Citizens of Botswana are called "Batswana" (singular: Motswana), regardless of ethnicity. Formerly a British <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Protectorate" title="Protectorate">protectorate</a> of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bechuanaland_Protectorate" title="Bechuanaland Protectorate">Bechuanaland</a>, Botswana adopted its new name after becoming independent within the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Commonwealth_of_Nations" title="Commonwealth of Nations">Commonwealth</a> on 30 September 1966. It is bordered by <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">South Africa</a> to the south and southeast, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Namibia" title="Namibia">Namibia</a> to the west and north, and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zimbabwe" title="Zimbabwe">Zimbabwe</a> to the northeast. It meets <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zambia" title="Zambia">Zambia</a> at a single point.</p>
<p>Geographically the country is flat and up to 70% of Botswana is covered by the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kalahari_Desert" title="Kalahari Desert">Kalahari Desert</a>. Botswana was one of the most impoverished countries in Africa when it became independent in 1966. Today, it is home to a relatively stable political system and a rapidly developing <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Market_economy" title="Market economy">market economy</a>. Being closely tied with the economy of South Africa, the country's economy is one of the most successful in <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a> and is dominated by the fast-growing service sector, world-renowned diamond industry, tourism, and manufacturing.</p>
<p>About 60% of the population live above the international poverty line of US$1.25 a day.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Botswana#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> Botswana's economic growth rate has outpaced the economic growth of even the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Asian_Tigers" title="Asian Tigers" class="mw-redirect">Asian Tigers</a>, and the World Bank cites Botswana as one of the world's great development success stories.<sup id="cite_ref-worldbankbotswana_3-0" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Botswana#cite_note-worldbankbotswana-3"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
