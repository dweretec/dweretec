<p><b>Tanganyika</b> was an <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/East_Africa" title="East Africa">East African</a> territory lying between the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indian_Ocean" title="Indian Ocean">Indian Ocean</a> and the largest of the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/African_Great_Lakes" title="African Great Lakes">African great lakes</a>: <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lake_Victoria" title="Lake Victoria">Lake Victoria</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lake_Malawi" title="Lake Malawi">Lake Malawi</a> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lake_Tanganyika" title="Lake Tanganyika">Lake Tanganyika</a>. From 9 December 1961 to 26 April 1964 it was also an independent nation. Once part of the colony of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/German_East_Africa" title="German East Africa">German East Africa</a> (<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a>: <span lang="de" xml:lang="de"><i>Deutsch-Ostafrika</i></span>), it comprised today's <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rwanda" title="Rwanda">Rwanda</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Burundi" title="Burundi">Burundi</a>, and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tanzania" title="Tanzania">Tanzania</a> with the exclusion of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zanzibar" title="Zanzibar">Zanzibar</a>. After <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a> the parts that are today's <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rwanda" title="Rwanda">Rwanda</a> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Burundi" title="Burundi">Burundi</a> became a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/League_of_Nations" title="League of Nations">League of Nations</a> mandate governed by <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Belgium" title="Belgium">Belgium</a>. The major part, however, came under <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/British_empire" title="British empire" class="mw-redirect">British military</a> rule and was transferred to Britain under the 1919 <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Treaty_of_Versailles" title="Treaty of Versailles">Treaty of Versailles</a>. This was confirmed by a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/League_of_Nations_Mandate" title="League of Nations Mandate" class="mw-redirect">League of Nations Mandate</a> in 1922, later becoming a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations_Trust_Territory" title="United Nations Trust Territory" class="mw-redirect">United Nations Trust Territory</a>. Britain changed the name to the <i><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tanganyika_Territory" title="Tanganyika Territory">Tanganyika Territory</a></i>.</p>
<p>On 9 December 1961 Tanganyika became independent as a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Commonwealth_Realm" title="Commonwealth Realm" class="mw-redirect">Commonwealth Realm</a>, and on 9 December 1962 it became the <i>Republic of Tanganyika</i> within the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Commonwealth_of_Nations" title="Commonwealth of Nations">Commonwealth of Nations</a>. In 1964, it joined with the islands of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zanzibar" title="Zanzibar">Zanzibar</a> to form the <i>United Republic of Tanganyika and Zanzibar</i>, later in the year changed to the <i><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tanzania" title="Tanzania">United Republic of Tanzania</a></i>.</p>
<p>Although Tanganyika still exists within Tanzania, the name is no longer used formally for the territory. These days the name <i>Tanganyika</i> is used almost exclusively to refer to the lake.</p>
