<p><b>Infrastructure</b> can be defined as the basic physical and organizational structures needed for the operation of a society or enterprise, <sup id="cite_ref-0" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Infrastructure#cite_note-0"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> or the services and facilities necessary for an economy to function. <sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Infrastructure#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> The term typically refers to the technical structures that support a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Society" title="Society">society</a>, such as <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Road" title="Road">roads</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Water_supply" title="Water supply">water supply</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sewage" title="Sewage">sewers</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Power_grids" title="Power grids" class="mw-redirect">power grids</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Telecommunication" title="Telecommunication">telecommunications</a>, and so forth. Viewed functionally, infrastructure <i>facilitates</i> the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manufacturing" title="Manufacturing">production</a> of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Good_(economics)" title="Good (economics)">goods</a> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Service_(economics)" title="Service (economics)">services</a>; for example, roads enable the transport of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Raw_material" title="Raw material">raw materials</a> to a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Factory" title="Factory">factory</a>, and also for the distribution of finished products to <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Market" title="Market">markets</a>. In some contexts, the term may also include basic social services such as schools and hospitals <sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Infrastructure#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup>. In military parlance, the term refers to the buildings and permanent installations necessary for the support, redeployment, and operation of military forces <sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Infrastructure#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup>.</p>
<p>In this article, <i>infrastructure</i> will be used in the sense of technical structures or physical networks that support society, unless specified otherwise.</p>
