class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Sudan#cite_note-factbook-7"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup> is a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Landlocked_country" title="Landlocked country">landlocked country</a> located between <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Central_Africa" title="Central Africa">Central Africa</a> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eastern_Africa" title="Eastern Africa" class="mw-redirect">Eastern Africa</a>. Its current capital is <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Juba" title="Juba">Juba</a>, which is also its largest city; the capital city is planned to be moved to the more centrally-located <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ramciel" title="Ramciel">Ramciel</a> in the future.<sup id="cite_ref-BBCprofile_8-0" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Sudan#cite_note-BBCprofile-8"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup> South Sudan is bordered by <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ethiopia" title="Ethiopia">Ethiopia</a> to the east, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kenya" title="Kenya">Kenya</a> to the southeast, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Uganda" title="Uganda">Uganda</a> to the south, the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Democratic_Republic_of_the_Congo" title="Democratic Republic of the Congo">Democratic Republic of the Congo</a> to the southwest, the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Central_African_Republic" title="Central African Republic">Central African Republic</a> to the west, and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sudan" title="Sudan">Sudan</a> to the north. South Sudan includes the vast <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Swamp" title="Swamp">swamp</a> region of the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sudd" title="Sudd">Sudd</a> formed by the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/White_Nile" title="White Nile">White Nile</a>, locally called the <i><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/White_Nile" title="White Nile">Bahr al Jabal</a></i>.</p>
<p>What are now the modern states of South Sudan and Sudan were part of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egypt</a> under the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Muhammad_Ali_Dynasty" title="Muhammad Ali Dynasty">Muhammad Ali Dynasty</a>, later being governed as an <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anglo-Egyptian_Sudan" title="Anglo-Egyptian Sudan">Anglo-Egyptian condominium</a> until Sudanese independence was achieved in 1956. Following the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/First_Sudanese_Civil_War" title="First Sudanese Civil War">First Sudanese Civil War</a>, the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Southern_Sudan_Autonomous_Region" title="Southern Sudan Autonomous Region" class="mw-redirect">Southern Sudan Autonomous Region</a> was formed in 1972 and lasted until 1983. A <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Second_Sudanese_civil_war" title="Second Sudanese civil war" class="mw-redirect">second Sudanese civil war</a> soon developed and ended with the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Comprehensive_Peace_Agreement" title="Comprehensive Peace Agreement">Comprehensive Peace Agreement</a> of 2005. Later that year, southern autonomy was restored when an <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Autonomous_Government_of_Southern_Sudan" title="Autonomous Government of Southern Sudan" class="mw-redirect">Autonomous Government of Southern Sudan</a> was formed. South Sudan became an independent state on 9 July 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Sudan#cite_note-9"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Sudan#cite_note-10"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup> On 14 July 2011, South Sudan became a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations_member_state" title="United Nations member state" class="mw-redirect">United Nations member state</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Sudan#cite_note-11"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Sudan#cite_note-12"><span>[</span>13<span>]</span></a></sup> It joined the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/African_Union" title="African Union">African Union</a> on 28 July 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-au54_13-0" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Sudan#cite_note-au54-13"><span>[</span>14<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>South Sudan is one of the poorest countries with possibly the worst health situation in the world.<sup id="cite_ref-health_14-0" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Sudan#cite_note-health-14"><span>[</span>15<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
