<p><b>Namibia</b>, officially the <b>Republic of Namibia</b> (<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Afrikaans" title="Afrikaans">Afrikaans</a>: <i>Republiek van Namibië</i>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a>: <i>Republik Namibia</i>), is a country in <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Southern_Africa" title="Southern Africa">Southern Africa</a> whose western border is the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic Ocean</a>. It shares borders with <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Angola" title="Angola">Angola</a> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zambia" title="Zambia">Zambia</a> to the north, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Botswana" title="Botswana">Botswana</a> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zimbabwe" title="Zimbabwe">Zimbabwe</a> to the east, and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">South Africa</a> to the south and east. It gained independence from South Africa on 21 March 1990 following the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Namibian_War_of_Independence" title="Namibian War of Independence">Namibian War of Independence</a>. Its capital and largest city is <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Windhoek" title="Windhoek">Windhoek</a> (<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a>: <span lang="de" xml:lang="de"><i>Windhuk</i></span>).</p>
<p>Namibia is a member state of the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations</a> (UN), the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Southern_African_Development_Community" title="Southern African Development Community">Southern African Development Community</a> (SADC), the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/African_Union" title="African Union">African Union</a> (AU), the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Commonwealth_of_Nations" title="Commonwealth of Nations">Commonwealth of Nations</a> and many other international organisations. It has been given many names: the land of contrasts, the land God made in anger, the ageless land. For many years it was known only as <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_West_Africa" title="South West Africa">South West Africa</a>, but it adopted the name Namibia, after the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Namib_Desert" title="Namib Desert">Namib Desert</a>. It is the second least densely populated country in the world, after <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mongolia" title="Mongolia">Mongolia</a>.</p>
<p>The dry lands of Namibia were inhabited since early times by <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bushmen" title="Bushmen">Bushmen</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Damara_(people)" title="Damara (people)" class="mw-redirect">Damara</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Namaqua" title="Namaqua">Namaqua</a>, and since about the 14th century AD, by immigrating <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bantu_peoples" title="Bantu peoples">Bantu</a> who came with the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bantu_expansion" title="Bantu expansion">Bantu expansion</a>. It was visited by the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">British</a> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Dutch</a> missionaries during the late 18th century. It was also visited by <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dorsland" title="Dorsland" class="mw-redirect">Dorsland</a> trekkers in 1879<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Namibia#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup>, but became a German protectorate in 1884. In 1920, the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/League_of_Nations" title="League of Nations">League of Nations</a> mandated the country to <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">South Africa</a>, which imposed their laws and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Africa_under_apartheid" title="South Africa under apartheid">apartheid</a> policy.</p>
<p>In 1966, uprisings and demands by African leaders led the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations</a> to assume direct responsibility over the territory, changing the name to Namibia in 1968 and recognizing <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SWAPO" title="SWAPO">South West Africa People’s Organization</a> (SWAPO) as official representative of the Namibian people in 1973. Namibia, however, remained under South African administration during this time. Following internal violence, South Africa installed an interim administration in Namibia in 1985. Namibia obtained full independence from South Africa in 1990 (with the exception of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Walvis_Bay" title="Walvis Bay">Walvis Bay</a> - a city that remained under South African control until 1994)</p>
<p>Namibia has a population of 1.8 million people and a stable <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Multi-party_system" title="Multi-party system">multiparty</a> <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Parliamentary_democracy" title="Parliamentary democracy" class="mw-redirect">parliamentary</a> <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Democracy" title="Democracy">democracy</a>. <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Agriculture" title="Agriculture">Agriculture</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Herding" title="Herding">herding</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tourism_in_Namibia" title="Tourism in Namibia">tourism</a> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mineral_industry_of_Namibia" title="Mineral industry of Namibia">mining of precious stones and metals</a> form the backbone of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Economy_of_Namibia" title="Economy of Namibia">Namibia's economy</a>. Approximately half the population live below the international poverty line of U.S.$1.25 a day,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Namibia#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> and the nation has suffered heavily from the effects of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/HIV/AIDS_in_Namibia" title="HIV/AIDS in Namibia">HIV/AIDS</a>, with 15% of the adult population infected with HIV in 2007.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Namibia#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
