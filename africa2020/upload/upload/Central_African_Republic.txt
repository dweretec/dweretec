<p>The <b>Central African Republic</b> (<b>CAR</b>) (<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>: <span lang="fr" xml:lang="fr"><i>République centrafricaine</i></span>, <small>pronounced:&#160;</small><span title="Pronunciation in IPA" class="IPA"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:IPA_for_French" title="Wikipedia:IPA for French">[ʀepyblik sɑ̃tʀafʀikɛn]</a></span>, or <i>Centrafrique</i> <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">[sɑ̃tʀafʀik]</span>; <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sango" title="Sango" class="mw-redirect">Sango</a> <i>Ködörösêse</i> <i>tî Bêafrîka</i>), is a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Landlocked_country" title="Landlocked country">landlocked country</a> in <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Central_Africa" title="Central Africa">Central Africa</a>. It borders <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chad" title="Chad">Chad</a> in the north, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sudan" title="Sudan">Sudan</a> in the east, the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Republic_of_the_Congo" title="Republic of the Congo">Republic of the Congo</a> and the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Democratic_Republic_of_the_Congo" title="Democratic Republic of the Congo">Democratic Republic of the Congo</a> in the south, and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cameroon" title="Cameroon">Cameroon</a> in the west.</p>
<p>Most of the CAR consists of Sudano-Guinean savannas but it also includes a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sahel" title="Sahel">Sahelo</a>-<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sudanian_Savanna" title="Sudanian Savanna">Sudanese</a> zone in the north and an <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Northern_Congolian_forest-savanna_mosaic" title="Northern Congolian forest-savanna mosaic">equatorial forest zone</a> in the south. Two thirds of the country lies in the basins of the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ubangi_River" title="Ubangi River">Ubangi River</a>, which flows south into the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Congo_River" title="Congo River">Congo River</a>, while the remaining third lies in the basin of the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chari_River" title="Chari River">Chari River</a>, which flows north into <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lake_Chad" title="Lake Chad">Lake Chad</a>.</p>
<p>Since most of the territory is located in the Ubangi and Shari river basins, the French called the colony it carved out in this region <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ubangi-Shari" title="Ubangi-Shari">Ubangi-Chari</a>, or <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oubangui-Chari" title="Oubangui-Chari" class="mw-redirect">Oubangui-Chari</a> in French. This French colony of Ubangi-Chari became a semi-autonomous territory of the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/French_Community" title="French Community">French Community</a> in 1958 and then an independent nation on 13 August 1960. For over three decades after independence, the CAR was ruled by presidents who were not chosen in multi-party democratic elections or who took power by force. Local discontent with this system was eventually reinforced by international pressure, following the end of the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cold_War" title="Cold War">Cold War</a>.</p>
<p>The first multi-party democratic elections were held in 1993 with resources provided by the country's donors and help from the UN Office for Electoral Affairs. They brought <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ange-F%C3%A9lix_Patass%C3%A9" title="Ange-Félix Patassé">Ange-Félix Patassé</a> to power, but President Patassé lost popular support and was <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coup_d%27%C3%A9tat" title="Coup d'état">overthrown</a> by French-backed General <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fran%C3%A7ois_Boziz%C3%A9" title="François Bozizé">François Bozizé</a> in 2003. General Bozizé won a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Central_African_Republic_elections,_2005" title="Central African Republic elections, 2005" class="mw-redirect">democratic election</a> in May 2005. Inability to pay workers in the public sector led to strikes in 2007, forcing the resignation of the government in early 2008. A new Prime Minister, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Faustin-Archange_Touad%C3%A9ra" title="Faustin-Archange Touadéra">Faustin-Archange Touadéra</a>, was named on January 22, 2008.</p>
<p>The Central African Republic is one of the poorest countries in the world and among the ten poorest countries in Africa. The Human Development Index for Central African Republic is 0.352, which gives the country a rank of 178th out of 179 countries with data.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Central_African_Republic#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> In 2001, <i><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Ecologist" title="The Ecologist" class="mw-redirect">The Ecologist</a></i> magazine estimated that Central African Republic is the world's leading country in <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sustainable_development" title="Sustainable development">sustainable development</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Central_African_Republic#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
