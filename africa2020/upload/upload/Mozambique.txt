<p><b>Mozambique</b>, officially the <b>Republic of Mozambique</b> (<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">Portuguese</a>: <span lang="pt" xml:lang="pt"><i>Moçambique</i></span> or <i>República de Moçambique</i>, <small>pronounced&#160;</small><span title="Pronunciation in IPA" class="IPA"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:IPA_for_Portuguese" title="Wikipedia:IPA for Portuguese">[ʁɛˈpublikɐ di musɐ̃ˈbiki]</a></span>), is a country in southeastern <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a> bordered by the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indian_Ocean" title="Indian Ocean">Indian Ocean</a> to the east, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tanzania" title="Tanzania">Tanzania</a> to the north, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Malawi" title="Malawi">Malawi</a> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zambia" title="Zambia">Zambia</a> to the northwest, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zimbabwe" title="Zimbabwe">Zimbabwe</a> to the west and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Swaziland" title="Swaziland">Swaziland</a> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">South Africa</a> to the southwest. It was explored by <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vasco_da_Gama" title="Vasco da Gama">Vasco da Gama</a> in 1498 and colonized by <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">Portugal</a> in 1505. Mozambique became independent in 1975, to which it became the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/People%27s_Republic_of_Mozambique" title="People's Republic of Mozambique">People's Republic of Mozambique</a> shortly after, and was the scene of an intense <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mozambican_Civil_War" title="Mozambican Civil War">civil war</a> lasting from 1977 to 1992. The country is a member of the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Community_of_Portuguese_Language_Countries" title="Community of Portuguese Language Countries">Community of Portuguese Language Countries</a> and the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Commonwealth_of_Nations" title="Commonwealth of Nations">Commonwealth of Nations</a> and an observer of the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Francophonie" title="Francophonie" class="mw-redirect">Francophonie</a>. The country was named Moçambique by the Portuguese after <i>Msumbiji</i>, the Swahili name of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mozambique_Island" title="Mozambique Island" class="mw-redirect">Mozambique Island</a> and port-town. Mozambique's life expectancy and infant mortality rates are both among the worst ranked in the world. Its <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Human_Development_Index" title="Human Development Index">Human Development Index</a> is one of the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_countries_by_Human_Development_Index" title="List of countries by Human Development Index">lowest on earth</a>.</p>
