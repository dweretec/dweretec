<p><b>People</b>:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Islamic_view_of_Moses" title="Islamic view of Moses">Islamic view of Moses</a>, Musa is the name of Moses in Arabic, Malay, Indonesian and other Asian and African languages; it is used in both Islamic and Christian scriptures</li>
<li><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Musa_al-Kadhim" title="Musa al-Kadhim">Musa al-Kadhim</a>, seventh <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Imamah_(Shi%27a_Twelver_doctrine)" title="Imamah (Shi'a Twelver doctrine)">Imām</a> of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Twelvers" title="Twelvers" class="mw-redirect">Twelver Shī‘ah Muslims</a></li>
<li><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/M%C3%ADrz%C3%A1_M%C3%BAs%C3%A1" title="Mírzá Músá">Mírzá Músá</a> (d. 1887) surnamed Áqáy-i-Kalím was the brother of Bahá'u'lláh, the founder of the Bahá'í Faith</li>
<li><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Musa_of_Parthia" title="Musa of Parthia">Musa of Parthia</a>, queen of Parthia c. 2 BC – AD 4</li>
<li><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Musa_bin_Nusair" title="Musa bin Nusair">Musa bin Nusair</a> (640-716), Yemeni Muslim governor and general under the Umayyads, Viceroy of North Africa since 698, invaded Spain in 711</li>
<li><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Musa_bin_Shakir" title="Musa bin Shakir" class="mw-redirect">Musa bin Shakir</a>, Persian engineer and astronomer</li>
<li>The <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Banu_Musa" title="Banu Musa" class="mw-redirect">Banu Musa</a> brothers, Persian scholar and sons of Musa bin Shakir</li>

<li><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Musa_C%C3%A4lil" title="Musa Cälil">Musa Cälil</a>, Tatar poet and anti-Nazi resistance fighter</li>
<li><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Musa_Cooper" title="Musa Cooper">Musa Cooper</a> <i><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/So_You_Think_You_Can_Dance" title="So You Think You Can Dance" class="mw-redirect">So You Think You Can Dance</a></i> finalist</li>

<li><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quintus_Pomponius_Musa" title="Quintus Pomponius Musa">Quintus Pomponius Musa</a> a first century Roman banker and moneyer (creator of coinage)</li>
</ul>
