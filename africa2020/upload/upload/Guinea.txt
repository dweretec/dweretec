<p><b>Guinea</b>, officially <b>Republic of Guinea</b> (pronounced <span title="Pronunciation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:IPA_for_English" title="Wikipedia:IPA for English">/ɡɪni/</a></span>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>: <span lang="fr" xml:lang="fr"><i>République de Guinée</i></span>), is a country in <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/West_Africa" title="West Africa">West Africa</a> formerly known as <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/French_Guinea" title="French Guinea">French Guinea</a> (<i>Guinée française</i>). The country's current population is estimated at 10,211,437 (<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CIA" title="CIA" class="mw-redirect">CIA</a> 2008 estimate).<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guinea#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Guinea's size is almost 246,000&#160;square kilometres (94,981&#160;sq&#160;mi). Its territory has a crescent shape, with its western border on the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic Ocean</a>, curving inland to the east and south. The Atlantic coast borders Guinea to the west, along with <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guinea-Bissau" title="Guinea-Bissau">Guinea-Bissau</a>. <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Senegal" title="Senegal">Senegal</a> forms its inland northern border, along with <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mali" title="Mali">Mali</a>, to the north and north-east. <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/C%C3%B4te_d%27Ivoire" title="Côte d'Ivoire">Côte d'Ivoire</a> is to the south-east, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Liberia" title="Liberia">Liberia</a> to the south and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sierra_Leone" title="Sierra Leone">Sierra Leone</a> to the southwest. The <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Niger_River" title="Niger River">Niger River</a> runs through the nation, providing both water and irregular transportation.</p>
<p><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Conakry" title="Conakry">Conakry</a> is the capital, seat of the national government, and largest city. The nation is sometimes called <b>Guinea-Conakry</b> to distinguish it from its neighbor <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guinea-Bissau" title="Guinea-Bissau">Guinea-Bissau</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guinea#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> Guinea is home to twenty four ethnic groups, the three largest and most dominant are the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fula_people" title="Fula people">Fula</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mandinka_people" title="Mandinka people">Mandinka</a> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Susu_people" title="Susu people">Susu</a>.</p>
