<p><b>Angola</b>, officially the <b>Republic of Angola</b> (<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">Portuguese</a>: <span lang="pt" xml:lang="pt"><i>República de Angola</i></span>, <small>pronounced&#160;</small><span title="Pronunciation in IPA" class="IPA"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:IPA_for_Portuguese" title="Wikipedia:IPA for Portuguese">[ʁɛˈpublikɐ dɨ ɐ̃ˈɡɔlɐ]</a></span>; <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kongo_language" title="Kongo language">Kongo</a>: <span lang="kg" xml:lang="kg">Repubilika ya Ngola</span>), is a country in south-central <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a> bordered by <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Namibia" title="Namibia">Namibia</a> on the south, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Democratic_Republic_of_the_Congo" title="Democratic Republic of the Congo">Democratic Republic of the Congo</a> on the north, and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zambia" title="Zambia">Zambia</a> on the east; its west coast is on the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic Ocean</a>. The <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Enclave_and_exclave" title="Enclave and exclave">exclave</a> province of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cabinda_(province)" title="Cabinda (province)" class="mw-redirect">Cabinda</a> has a border with the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Republic_of_the_Congo" title="Republic of the Congo">Republic of the Congo</a> and the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Democratic_Republic_of_the_Congo" title="Democratic Republic of the Congo">Democratic Republic of the Congo</a>. Angola was a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portuguese_West_Africa" title="Portuguese West Africa">Portuguese overseas territory</a> from the 16th century to <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1975" title="1975">1975</a>. After independence, Angola was the scene of an intense <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Angolan_Civil_War" title="Angolan Civil War">civil war from 1975 to 2002</a>. The country is the second-largest <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Petroleum" title="Petroleum">petroleum</a> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diamond" title="Diamond">diamond</a> producer in <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sub-Saharan" title="Sub-Saharan" class="mw-redirect">sub-Saharan</a> Africa; however, its <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Life_expectancy" title="Life expectancy">life expectancy</a> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Infant_mortality" title="Infant mortality">infant mortality</a> rates are both among the worst ranked in the world. In August 2006, a peace treaty was signed with a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Faction" title="Faction">faction</a> of the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Front_for_the_Liberation_of_the_Enclave_of_Cabinda" title="Front for the Liberation of the Enclave of Cabinda">FLEC</a>, a separatist guerrilla group from the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cabinda_Province" title="Cabinda Province">Cabinda</a> exclave in the North, which is still active.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Angola#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> About 65% of Angola's oil comes from that region.</p>
