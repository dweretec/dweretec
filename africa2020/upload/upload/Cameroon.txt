<p>The <b>Republic of Cameroon</b> (<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>: <span lang="fr" xml:lang="fr"><i>République du Cameroun</i></span>) is a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Unitary_state" title="Unitary state">unitary republic</a> of central and western <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>. It is bordered by <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nigeria" title="Nigeria">Nigeria</a> to the west; <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chad" title="Chad">Chad</a> to the northeast; the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Central_African_Republic" title="Central African Republic">Central African Republic</a> to the east; and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Equatorial_Guinea" title="Equatorial Guinea">Equatorial Guinea</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gabon" title="Gabon">Gabon</a>, and the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Republic_of_the_Congo" title="Republic of the Congo">Republic of the Congo</a> to the south. Cameroon's coastline lies on the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bight_of_Bonny" title="Bight of Bonny">Bight of Bonny</a>, part of the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gulf_of_Guinea" title="Gulf of Guinea">Gulf of Guinea</a> and the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic Ocean</a>. The country is called "<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tourism_in_Cameroon" title="Tourism in Cameroon">Africa in miniature</a>" for its geological and cultural diversity. Natural features include beaches, deserts, mountains, rainforests, and savannas. The highest point is <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mount_Cameroon" title="Mount Cameroon">Mount Cameroon</a> in the southwest, and the largest cities are <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Douala" title="Douala">Douala</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yaound%C3%A9" title="Yaoundé">Yaoundé</a>, and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Garoua" title="Garoua">Garoua</a>. Cameroon is home to over 200 different ethnic and linguistic groups. The country is well known for its native styles of music, particularly <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Makossa" title="Makossa">makossa</a> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bikutsi" title="Bikutsi">bikutsi</a>, and for its successful <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cameroon_national_football_team" title="Cameroon national football team">national football team</a>. English and French are the official languages.</p>
<p>Early inhabitants of the territory included the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sao_civilisation" title="Sao civilisation">Sao civilisation</a> around <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lake_Chad" title="Lake Chad">Lake Chad</a> and the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Baka_(Cameroon_and_Gabon)" title="Baka (Cameroon and Gabon)">Baka</a> hunter-gatherers in the southeastern rainforest. Portuguese explorers reached the coast in the 15th century and named the area <i>Rio dos Camarões</i> ("River of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prawns" title="Prawns" class="mw-redirect">Prawns</a>"), the name from which <i>Cameroon</i> derives. <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fula_people" title="Fula people">Fulani</a> soldiers founded the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Adamawa_Emirate" title="Adamawa Emirate">Adamawa Emirate</a> in the north in the 19th century, and various ethnic groups of the west and northwest established powerful chiefdoms and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fon_(Cameroon)" title="Fon (Cameroon)">fondoms</a>. Cameroon became a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/German_Empire" title="German Empire">German</a> <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Colonialism" title="Colonialism">colony</a> in 1884. After World War I, the territory was divided between <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/French_Third_Republic" title="French Third Republic">France</a> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/British_Empire" title="British Empire">Britain</a> as <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/League_of_Nations_mandate" title="League of Nations mandate">League of Nations mandates</a>. The <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Union_of_the_Peoples_of_Cameroon" title="Union of the Peoples of Cameroon">Union des Populations du Cameroun</a> political party advocated independence but was outlawed in the 1950s. It waged war on French and Cameroonian forces until 1971. In 1960, French <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cameroun" title="Cameroun">Cameroun</a> became independent as the Republic of Cameroun under President <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ahmadou_Ahidjo" title="Ahmadou Ahidjo">Ahmadou Ahidjo</a>. The <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Southern_Cameroons" title="Southern Cameroons">southern part</a> of British <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cameroons" title="Cameroons">Cameroons</a> merged with it in 1961 to form the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Federal_republic" title="Federal republic">Federal Republic</a> of Cameroon. The country was renamed the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Unitary_state" title="Unitary state">United Republic</a> of Cameroon in 1972 and the Republic of Cameroon in 1984.</p>
<p>Compared to other African countries, Cameroon enjoys relatively high political and social stability. This has permitted the development of agriculture, roads, railways, and large petroleum and timber industries. Nevertheless, large numbers of Cameroonians live in poverty as subsistence farmers. Power lies firmly in the hands of the president, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paul_Biya" title="Paul Biya">Paul Biya</a>, and his <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cameroon_People%27s_Democratic_Movement" title="Cameroon People's Democratic Movement">Cameroon People's Democratic Movement</a> party, and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Political_corruption" title="Political corruption">corruption</a> is widespread. The <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anglophone_Cameroonian" title="Anglophone Cameroonian">Anglophone</a> community has grown increasingly alienated from the government, and Anglophone politicians have called for greater decentralisation and even the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Southern_Cameroons" title="Southern Cameroons">secession</a> of the former British-governed territories.</p>
