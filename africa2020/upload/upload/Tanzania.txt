<p>Tanzania's population of 44.9 million is highly diverse, composed of numerous ethnic, linguistic, and religious groups. Islam is biggest religion followed by Christianity. Tanzania is a presidential constitutional republic, and since 1996, its official capital has been <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dodoma" title="Dodoma">Dodoma</a>, where the President's Office, the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_Assembly_(Tanzania)" title="National Assembly (Tanzania)">National Assembly</a>, and some government ministries are located.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tanzania#cite_note-14"><span>[</span>13<span>]</span></a></sup> <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dar_es_Salaam" title="Dar es Salaam">Dar es Salaam</a>, the former capital, retains most government offices and is the country's largest city, principal port, and leading commercial center.<sup id="cite_ref-factbook_15-0" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tanzania#cite_note-factbook-15"><span>[</span>14<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-official_website_16-0" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tanzania#cite_note-official_website-16"><span>[</span>15<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tanzania#cite_note-17"><span>[</span>16<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>European colonialism began in mainland Tanzania during the late 19th century when Germany formed <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/German_East_Africa" title="German East Africa">German East Africa</a>, which gave way to British rule following <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a>. The mainland was governed as <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tanganyika" title="Tanganyika">Tanganyika</a>, with the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zanzibar_Archipelago" title="Zanzibar Archipelago">Zanzibar Archipelago</a> remaining a separate colonial jurisdiction. Following their respective independence in 1961 and 1963, the two entities merged in April 1964 to form the United Republic of Tanzania.<sup id="cite_ref-factbook_15-1" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tanzania#cite_note-factbook-15"><span>[</span>14<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
