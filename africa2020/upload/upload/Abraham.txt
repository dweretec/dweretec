<p><b>Abraham</b> (<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hebrew_language" title="Hebrew language">Hebrew</a>: <span style="white-space:nowrap; font-size:125%; font-family:David, Narkisim, Arial"><span lang="he" xml:lang="he">אַבְרָהָם</span>,</span> <small><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hebrew_language#Modern_Hebrew" title="Hebrew language">Modern</a></small>&#160;<i><span lang="he-Latn" title="he transliteration" class="Unicode" style="white-space:normal; text-decoration: none" xml:lang="he-Latn">Avraham</span></i> <small><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tiberian_vocalization" title="Tiberian vocalization">Tiberian</a></small>&#160;<i><span lang="he-Latn" title="Hebrew transliteration" class="Unicode" style="white-space:normal; text-decoration: none" xml:lang="he-Latn">ʾAḇrāhām</span></i>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arabic_language" title="Arabic language">Arabic</a>: <span lang="ar" xml:lang="ar"><big>إبراهيم</big></span>‎, <i><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ibrahim" title="Ibrahim"><span class="Unicode">Ibrāhīm</span></a></i>, <i><span class="Unicode">ʾAbrəham</span></i>) is the founding <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Patriarchs_(Bible)" title="Patriarchs (Bible)">patriarch</a> of the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Israelites" title="Israelites">Israelites</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ishmael" title="Ishmael">Ishmaelites</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Midianites" title="Midianites" class="mw-redirect">Midianites</a> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Edom" title="Edom">Edomite</a> peoples, as described in the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Book_of_Genesis" title="Book of Genesis">book of Genesis</a>. He is widely regarded as the patriarch of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jews" title="Jews" class="mw-redirect">Jews</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christians" title="Christians" class="mw-redirect">Christians</a>, and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Muslims" title="Muslims" class="mw-redirect">Muslims</a>.</p>
<p>According to Genesis, Abraham was originally named <i>Abram</i> and was of the tenth generation from <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Noah" title="Noah">Noah</a> and the 20th from <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Adam" title="Adam">Adam</a>.<span class="Z3988" title="ctx_ver=Z39.88-2004&amp;rft_val_fmt=info%3Aofi%2Ffmt%3Akev%3Amtx%3Abook&amp;rft.genre=book&amp;rft.btitle=&amp;rfr_id=info:sid/en.wikipedia.org:Abraham"><span style="display: none;">&#160;</span></span> His father was <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Terah" title="Terah">Terah</a>, and his brothers were <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nahor" title="Nahor">Nahor</a> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Haran" title="Haran">Haran</a>. Abraham was sent by God from his home in <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ur_Ka%C5%9Bdim" title="Ur Kaśdim">Ur Kaśdim</a> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Haran" title="Haran">Haran</a> (perhaps <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Harran" title="Harran">Harran</a>) to <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Canaan" title="Canaan">Canaan</a>, the land promised to his descendants by <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tetragrammaton" title="Tetragrammaton">YHWH</a>.</p>
<blockquote class="toccolours" style="float:none; padding: 10px 15px 10px 15px; display:table;">
<p>The L<small>ORD</small> had said to Abram, "Leave your country, your people and your father's household and go to the land I will show you. I will make you into a great nation and I will bless you; I will make your name great, and you will be a blessing. I will bless those who bless you, and whoever curses you I will curse; and all peoples on earth will be blessed through you."</p>
<p style="text-align: right;">– <cite><span class="plainlinks"><a href="http://www.biblegateway.com/bible?passage=Genesis%2012:1-3;&amp;version=NIV;" class="external text" rel="nofollow">Genesis&#160;12:1-3</a></span> (NIV)</cite></p>
</blockquote>
<p>In Canaan, Abraham entered into a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Covenant_(biblical)" title="Covenant (biblical)">covenant</a>: in exchange for recognition of YHWH as his God, Abraham will be blessed with innumerable progeny and the land would belong to his descendants.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Abraham#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> According to <a href="http://bibref.hebtools.com/?book=%20Genesis&amp;verse=17:5&amp;src=NIV" class="external text" rel="nofollow">Genesis 17:5</a>, his name was changed by God from Abram (probably meaning "the father is exalted") to Abraham, a name which Genesis explains as meaning "father of many nations." <span class="plainlinks"><a href="http://www.biblegateway.com/bible?passage=Genesis%2017:5;&amp;version=ESV;" class="external text" rel="nofollow">Genesis&#160;17:5</a></span>.</p>
<p>Judaism, Christianity and Islam are sometimes referred to as the "<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Abrahamic_religion" title="Abrahamic religion" class="mw-redirect">Abrahamic religions</a>" because of the progenitor role Abraham plays in their holy books. In the Jewish tradition, he is called <i>Avraham Avinu</i> or "Abraham, our Father". God promised Abraham that through his offspring, all the nations of the world will come to be blessed (<a href="http://bibref.hebtools.com/?book=%20Genesis&amp;verse=12:3&amp;src=HE" class="external text" rel="nofollow">Genesis 12:3</a>), interpreted in Christian tradition as a reference particularly to <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Genealogy_of_Jesus" title="Genealogy of Jesus">Christ</a>. Jews, Christians, and Muslims consider him father of the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Israelites" title="Israelites">people of Israel</a> through his son <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Isaac" title="Isaac">Isaac</a> (cf. <a href="http://bibref.hebtools.com/?book=%20Exodus&amp;verse=6:3&amp;src=HE" class="external text" rel="nofollow">Exodus 6:3</a>, <a href="http://bibref.hebtools.com/?book=%20Exodus&amp;verse=32:13&amp;src=HE" class="external text" rel="nofollow">Exodus 32:13</a>) by his wife Sarah. For Muslims, he is a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prophet_of_Islam" title="Prophet of Islam" class="mw-redirect">prophet of Islam</a> and the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ancestor" title="Ancestor">ancestor</a> of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Muhammad" title="Muhammad">Muhammad</a> through his other son <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ishmael" title="Ishmael">Ishmael</a> – born to him by Sarah's servant, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hagar_(Bible)" title="Hagar (Bible)">Hagar</a>. (Jews and Christians refer to Hagar as Sarah's servant). Abraham is also a progenitor of the Semitic tribes of the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Negev" title="Negev">Negev</a> who trace their descent from their common ancestor <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sheba" title="Sheba">Sheba</a> (<a href="http://bibref.hebtools.com/?book=%20Genesis&amp;verse=10:28&amp;src=" class="external text" rel="nofollow">Genesis 10:28</a>).</p>
