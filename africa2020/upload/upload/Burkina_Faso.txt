<p><b>Burkina Faso</b> (pronounced <span title="Pronunciation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:IPA_for_English" title="Wikipedia:IPA for English">/bərˌkiːnə ˈfɑːsoʊ/</a></span> <span title="English pronunciation respelling"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Pronunciation_respelling_key" title="Wikipedia:Pronunciation respelling key"><i>burr-<small>KEE</small>-nə</i></a></span> <span title="English pronunciation respelling"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Pronunciation_respelling_key" title="Wikipedia:Pronunciation respelling key"><i><small>FAH</small>-soh</i></a></span>; <small>French:&#160;</small><span title="Pronunciation in IPA" class="IPA"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:IPA_for_French" title="Wikipedia:IPA for French">[byʁkina faso]</a></span>), also known by its short-form name <b>Burkina</b>, is a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Landlocked" title="Landlocked" class="mw-redirect">landlocked</a> nation in <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/West_Africa" title="West Africa">West Africa</a>. It is surrounded by six countries: <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mali" title="Mali">Mali</a> to the north, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Niger" title="Niger">Niger</a> to the east, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Benin" title="Benin">Benin</a> to the south east, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Togo" title="Togo">Togo</a> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ghana" title="Ghana">Ghana</a> to the south, and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/C%C3%B4te_d%27Ivoire" title="Côte d'Ivoire">Côte d'Ivoire</a> to the south west.</p>
<p>Its size is 274,000&#160;km² with an estimated population of more than	13,200,000. Formerly called the <b>Republic of Upper Volta</b>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Geographical_renaming" title="Geographical renaming">it was renamed</a> on August 4, 1984, by President <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thomas_Sankara" title="Thomas Sankara">Thomas Sankara</a> to mean "the land of upright people" in <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/More_language" title="More language" class="mw-redirect">Moré</a> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dioula_language" title="Dioula language">Dioula</a>, the major native languages of the country. Literally, "Burkina" may be translated, "men of integrity," from the Moré language, and "Faso" means "father's house" in Dioula. The inhabitants of Burkina Faso are known as <i>Burkinabè</i> (pronounced <span title="Pronunciation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:IPA_for_English" title="Wikipedia:IPA for English">/bərˈkiːnəbeɪ/</a></span> <span title="English pronunciation respelling"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Pronunciation_respelling_key" title="Wikipedia:Pronunciation respelling key"><i>burr-<small>KEE</small>-nə-bay</i></a></span>).</p>
<p>Burkina Faso's capital is <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ouagadougou" title="Ouagadougou">Ouagadougou</a>. After gaining independence from <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/France" title="France">France</a> in 1960, the country underwent many governmental changes until arriving at its current form, a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Semi-presidential_system" title="Semi-presidential system">semi-presidential</a> <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Republic" title="Republic">republic</a>. The president is <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Blaise_Compaor%C3%A9" title="Blaise Compaoré">Blaise Compaoré</a>. The country occupies the sixth to last place on the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Human_Development_Index" title="Human Development Index">Human Development Index</a>.</p>
