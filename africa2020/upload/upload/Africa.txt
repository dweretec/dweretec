<p><b>Africa</b> is the world's second-largest and second most-populous <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Continent" title="Continent">continent</a>, after <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a>. At about 30.2 million km² (11.7 million <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Square_mile" title="Square mile">sq&#160;mi</a>) including adjacent islands, it covers 6% of the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Earth" title="Earth">Earth</a>'s total surface area and 20.4% of the total land area.<sup id="cite_ref-Sayre_1-0" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Africa#cite_note-Sayre-1"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> With a billion people (as of 2009, see <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Africa#Territories_and_regions">table</a>) in 61 territories, it accounts for about 14.8% of the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/World" title="World">World</a>'s <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Human" title="Human">human</a> <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Population" title="Population">population</a>. The continent is surrounded by the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mediterranean_Sea" title="Mediterranean Sea">Mediterranean Sea</a> to the north, both the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Suez_Canal" title="Suez Canal">Suez Canal</a> and the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Red_Sea" title="Red Sea">Red Sea</a> along the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sinai_Peninsula" title="Sinai Peninsula">Sinai Peninsula</a> to the northeast, the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indian_Ocean" title="Indian Ocean">Indian Ocean</a> to the southeast, and the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic Ocean</a> to the west. Not counting the disputed territory of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Western_Sahara" title="Western Sahara">Western Sahara</a>, there are 53 countries, including <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Madagascar" title="Madagascar">Madagascar</a> and various island groups, associated with the continent.</p>
<p>Africa, particularly central <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eastern_Africa" title="Eastern Africa" class="mw-redirect">eastern Africa</a>, is widely regarded within the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scientific_community" title="Scientific community">scientific community</a> to be the origin of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Human" title="Human">humans</a> and the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hominidae" title="Hominidae">Hominidae</a> tree (great <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ape" title="Ape">apes</a>), as <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Evidence" title="Evidence">evidenced</a> by the discovery of the earliest <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hominids" title="Hominids" class="mw-redirect">hominids</a> and their ancestors, as well as later ones that have been dated to around seven million years ago – including <i><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sahelanthropus_tchadensis" title="Sahelanthropus tchadensis">Sahelanthropus tchadensis</a></i>, <i><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Australopithecus_africanus" title="Australopithecus africanus">Australopithecus africanus</a></i>, <i><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Australopithecus_afarensis" title="Australopithecus afarensis">A. afarensis</a></i>, <i><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Homo_erectus" title="Homo erectus">Homo erectus</a></i>, <i><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Homo_habilis" title="Homo habilis">H. habilis</a></i> and <i><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Homo_ergaster" title="Homo ergaster">H. ergaster</a></i> – with the earliest <i><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Homo_sapiens" title="Homo sapiens" class="mw-redirect">Homo sapiens</a></i> (human) found in <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ethiopia" title="Ethiopia">Ethiopia</a> being dated to ca. 200,000 years ago.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Africa#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Africa straddles the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Equator" title="Equator">equator</a> and encompasses numerous climate areas; it is the only continent to stretch from the northern <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Temperate" title="Temperate" class="mw-redirect">temperate</a> to southern temperate zones.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Africa#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
