<p><b>Zimbabwe</b> (pronounced <span title="Pronunciation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:IPA_for_English" title="Wikipedia:IPA for English">/zɪmˈbɑːbweɪ/</a></span>), (officially the <b>Republic of Zimbabwe</b> and formerly <b><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Southern_Rhodesia" title="Southern Rhodesia">Southern Rhodesia</a></b>, the <b><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rhodesia" title="Rhodesia">Republic of Rhodesia</a></b> and <b><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zimbabwe_Rhodesia" title="Zimbabwe Rhodesia">Zimbabwe Rhodesia</a></b>) is a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Landlocked_country" title="Landlocked country">landlocked country</a> located in the southern part of the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Continent" title="Continent">continent</a> of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>, between the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zambezi" title="Zambezi">Zambezi</a> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Limpopo_River" title="Limpopo River">Limpopo</a> rivers. It is bordered by <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">South Africa</a> to the south, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Botswana" title="Botswana">Botswana</a> to the southwest, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zambia" title="Zambia">Zambia</a> to the northwest and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mozambique" title="Mozambique">Mozambique</a> to the east. Zimbabwe has three <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Official_languages" title="Official languages" class="mw-redirect">official languages</a>: <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_African_English" title="South African English">English</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shona_language" title="Shona language">Shona</a> (a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bantu_languages" title="Bantu languages">Bantu language</a>), and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Northern_Ndebele_language" title="Northern Ndebele language">Ndebele</a>.</p>
<p>Zimbabwe began as a part of the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/British_Empire" title="British Empire">British</a> <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/British_colonies" title="British colonies" class="mw-redirect">crown colony</a> of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rhodesia" title="Rhodesia">Rhodesia</a>. Today, Zimbabwe is governed by Prime Minister Morgan Tsvangirai's administration, with <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_Presidents_of_Zimbabwe" title="List of Presidents of Zimbabwe">President</a> <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Robert_Mugabe" title="Robert Mugabe">Robert Mugabe</a> as Head of State. Mugabe has been in power since the country's long <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rhodesian_Bush_War" title="Rhodesian Bush War">war for independence</a>. His rule has been characterized by <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Economy_of_Zimbabwe" title="Economy of Zimbabwe">economic</a> mismanagement, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hyperinflation_in_Zimbabwe" title="Hyperinflation in Zimbabwe">hyperinflation</a>, and widespread reports of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Human_rights_in_Zimbabwe" title="Human rights in Zimbabwe">human rights</a> abuses.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zimbabwe#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> The collapse of the nation's economy and widespread poverty and unemployment has increased support for <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_Zimbabwe" title="Prime Minister of Zimbabwe">Prime Minister</a> <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Morgan_Tsvangirai" title="Morgan Tsvangirai">Morgan Tsvangirai</a> and his opposition party, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Movement_for_Democratic_Change" title="Movement for Democratic Change">Movement for Democratic Change</a>, which in late 2008 agreed to share power with Mugabe.</p>
