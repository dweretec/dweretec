			<div class="dablink">This article is about the economic mechanism.  For other uses, see <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trade_(disambiguation)" title="Trade (disambiguation)">Trade (disambiguation)</a>.</div>
<p><b>Trade</b> is the voluntary exchange of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Good_(economics)" title="Good (economics)">goods</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Service_(economics)" title="Service (economics)">services</a>, or both. Trade is also called <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Commerce" title="Commerce">commerce</a> or <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Transaction" title="Transaction">transaction</a>. A mechanism that allows trade is called a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Market" title="Market">market</a>. The original form of trade was <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Barter_(economics)" title="Barter (economics)" class="mw-redirect">barter</a>, the direct exchange of goods and services. Later one side of the barter were the metals, precious metals (poles, coins), bill, paper money. Modern traders instead generally negotiate through a medium of exchange, such as <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Money" title="Money">money</a>. As a result, <b>buying</b> can be separated from <b>selling</b>, or <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Earning" title="Earning">earning</a>. The invention of money (and later credit, paper money and non-physical money) greatly simplified and promoted trade. Trade between two traders is called bilateral trade, while trade between more than two traders is called multilateral trade.</p>
<p>Trade exists for man due to specialization and division of labor, most people concentrate on a small aspect of production, trading for other products. Trade exists between regions because different regions have a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Comparative_advantage" title="Comparative advantage">comparative advantage</a> in the production of some tradable commodity, or because different regions' size allows for the benefits of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mass_production" title="Mass production">mass production</a>. As such, trade at <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Market_price" title="Market price">market prices</a> between locations benefits both locations.</p>
<p>Trading can also refer to the action performed by <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trader_(finance)" title="Trader (finance)">traders</a> and other market agents in the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Financial_markets" title="Financial markets" class="mw-redirect">financial markets</a>.</p>
