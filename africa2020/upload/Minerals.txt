<p>A <b>mineral</b> is a naturally occurring solid formed through <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Geology" title="Geology">geological</a> processes that has a characteristic chemical composition, a highly ordered atomic structure, and specific physical properties. A <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rock_(geology)" title="Rock (geology)">rock</a>, by comparison, is an aggregate of minerals and/or <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mineraloid" title="Mineraloid">mineraloids</a>, and need not have a specific chemical composition. Minerals range in composition from pure <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chemical_element" title="Chemical element">elements</a> and simple <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Salt_(chemistry)" title="Salt (chemistry)">salts</a> to very complex <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Silicate" title="Silicate">silicates</a> with thousands of known forms.<sup id="cite_ref-Klein_0-0" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Minerals#cite_note-Klein-0"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> The study of minerals is called <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mineralogy" title="Mineralogy">mineralogy</a>.</p>
