<p><b>Finance</b> is a field that deals with the allocation of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Asset" title="Asset">assets</a> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Liability_(financial_accounting)" title="Liability (financial accounting)">liabilities</a> over time under conditions of certainty and uncertainty. Finance can also be defined as the science of money management. A key point in finance is the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Time_value_of_money" title="Time value of money">time value of money</a>, which states that purchasing power of one unit of currency can vary over time. Finance aims to price assets based on their risk level and their expected <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rate_of_return" title="Rate of return">rate of return</a>. Finance can be broken into three different sub-categories: <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Public_finance" title="Public finance">public finance</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Corporate_finance" title="Corporate finance">corporate finance</a> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Personal_finance" title="Personal finance">personal finance</a>.</p>
