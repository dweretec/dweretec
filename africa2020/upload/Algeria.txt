coast</a>. Its capital and most populous city is <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Algiers" title="Algiers">Algiers</a>. With a total area of 2,381,741 square kilometres (919,595&#160;sq&#160;mi), 90% of which is desert, Algeria is the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_countries_and_dependencies_by_area" title="List of countries and dependencies by area">tenth-largest country in the world</a> and the largest in the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arab_world" title="Arab world">Arab world</a>, Africa (following the partition of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sudan" title="Sudan">Sudan</a>) and on the Mediterranean.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Algeria#cite_note-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup> The country is bordered in the northeast by <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tunisia" title="Tunisia">Tunisia</a>, in the east by <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Libya" title="Libya">Libya</a>, in the west by <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Morocco" title="Morocco">Morocco</a>, in the southwest by <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Western_Sahara" title="Western Sahara">Western Sahara</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mauritania" title="Mauritania">Mauritania</a>, and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mali" title="Mali">Mali</a>, in the southeast by <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Niger" title="Niger">Niger</a>, and in the north by the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mediterranean_Sea" title="Mediterranean Sea">Mediterranean Sea</a>.</p>
<p>The territory of today's Algeria was the home of many prehistoric cultures, including <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aterian" title="Aterian">Aterian</a> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Capsian_culture" title="Capsian culture">Capsian</a> and the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Proto-Berber_language" title="Proto-Berber language">Proto-Berber</a> cultures. Its area has known many empires and dynasties, including ancient <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Numidia" title="Numidia">Numidians</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phoenicia" title="Phoenicia">Phoenicians</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Punic" title="Punic" class="mw-redirect">Carthaginians</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ancient_Rome" title="Ancient Rome">Romans</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vandals" title="Vandals">Vandals</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Byzantine_Empire" title="Byzantine Empire">Byzantines</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Umayyad" title="Umayyad" class="mw-redirect">Umayyads</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Abbasid" title="Abbasid" class="mw-redirect">Abbasids</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fatimid" title="Fatimid" class="mw-redirect">Fatimids</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hammadids" title="Hammadids" class="mw-redirect">Hammadids</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Almoravids" title="Almoravids" class="mw-redirect">Almoravids</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Almohads" title="Almohads" class="mw-redirect">Almohads</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ottoman_Turks" title="Ottoman Turks" class="mw-redirect">Ottomans</a> and the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/French_Algeria" title="French Algeria">French colonial empire</a>. In recent decades, Algeria has experienced increased identity recognition demands, in response to which, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tamazight" title="Tamazight" class="mw-redirect">Tamazight</a>, the language of their 13,000 year old people, has been constitutionalized as a national language. Algeria is a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Semi-presidential_system" title="Semi-presidential system">semi-presidential republic</a> consisting of 48 provinces and 1541 communes. With a population of 39.5 million, it is the 33rd most populated country on Earth. <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Abdelaziz_Bouteflika" title="Abdelaziz Bouteflika">Abdelaziz Bouteflika</a> has been the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/President_of_Algeria" title="President of Algeria">President of Algeria</a> since 1999 and has won four consecutive elections.</p>
<p>Algeria's economy is largely based on <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydrocarbons" title="Hydrocarbons" class="mw-redirect">hydrocarbons</a>, due to which manufacturing has suffered from <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dutch_disease" title="Dutch disease">Dutch disease</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Algeria#cite_note-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup> The country supplies large amounts of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Natural_gas" title="Natural gas">natural gas</a> to Europe, and energy exports are the backbone of the economy. According to <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/OPEC" title="OPEC">OPEC</a> Algeria has the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_countries_by_proven_oil_reserves" title="List of countries by proven oil reserves">17th largest</a> reserves of oil in the world, and the second largest in <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>, while it has the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_countries_by_natural_gas_proven_reserves" title="List of countries by natural gas proven reserves">9th largest reserves</a> of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Natural_gas" title="Natural gas">natural gas</a>. <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sonatrach" title="Sonatrach">Sonatrach</a>, the national oil company, is the largest company in Africa.</p>
<p>Algeria has the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_countries_by_number_of_military_and_paramilitary_personnel" title="List of countries by number of military and paramilitary personnel">second largest military</a> in North Africa with the largest defence budget in Africa.<sup id="cite_ref-AlgeriaSpending_12-0" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Algeria#cite_note-AlgeriaSpending-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup> Algeria has had a peaceful <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Algeria_and_weapons_of_mass_destruction" title="Algeria and weapons of mass destruction">nuclear program</a> since the 1990s.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Algeria#cite_note-13"><span>[</span>13<span>]</span></a></sup> Algeria is a member of the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/African_Union" title="African Union">African Union</a>, the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arab_League" title="Arab League">Arab League</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/OPEC" title="OPEC">OPEC</a>, and the United Nations, and is a founding member of the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arab_Maghreb_Union" title="Arab Maghreb Union">Arab Maghreb Union</a>.</p>
