<p><b>Accommodation</b> may refer to:</p>
<ul>
<li>Accommodation (in British usage) or accommodations (in American usage), <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lodging" title="Lodging">lodging</a> in a dwelling or similar living quarters afforded to travellers in hotels or on cruise ships, or prisoners, etc.</li>
<li><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Accommodation_(religion)" title="Accommodation (religion)">Accommodation (religion)</a>, a theological principle linked to divine revelation within the Christian church</li>
<li><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Accommodation_(law)" title="Accommodation (law)">accommodation (law)</a>, a term used in United States contract law</li>
<li><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Accommodation_(eye)" title="Accommodation (eye)">Accommodation (eye)</a>, the process by which the eye increases optical power to maintain a clear image (focus) on an object as it draws near</li>
<li>Accommodation in psychology, the process by which existing mental structures and behaviors are modified to adapt to new experiences according to Jean Piaget, in the learning broader theory of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Constructivism_(learning_theory)" title="Constructivism (learning theory)">Constructivism</a></li>
<li>Classroom accommodations under an <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Individualized_Education_Program#Classroom_accommodations" title="Individualized Education Program">Individualized Education Program</a></li>
<li><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Communication_Accommodation_Theory" title="Communication Accommodation Theory">Communication Accommodation Theory</a>, the process by which people change their language behaviour to be more or less similar to that of the people with whom they are interacting.</li>
<li>Accommodation, a linguistics term meaning grammatical acceptance of unstated values as in <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Presupposition#Accommodation_of_presuppositions" title="Presupposition">accommodation of presuppositions</a></li>
<li><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Biblical_Accommodation" title="Biblical Accommodation">Biblical Accommodation</a>, the adaptation of text from the Bible to signify ideas different from those originally expressed.</li>
<li><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PS_Accommodation" title="PS Accommodation">PS <i>Accommodation</i></a> was a pioneer Canadian steamboat built by John Molson</li>
</ul>
