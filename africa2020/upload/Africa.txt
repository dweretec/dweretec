<p><b>Africa</b> is the world's second-largest and second-most-populous <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Continent" title="Continent">continent</a>. At about 30.2 million km<sup>2</sup> (11.7 million sq&#160;mi) including adjacent islands, it covers six percent of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Earth" title="Earth">Earth</a>'s total surface area and 20.4 percent of its total land area.<sup id="cite_ref-Sayre_2-0" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Africa#cite_note-Sayre-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> With 1.1 billion people as of 2013, it accounts for about 15% of the world's human population.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Africa#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> The continent is surrounded by the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mediterranean_Sea" title="Mediterranean Sea">Mediterranean Sea</a> to the north, both the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Suez_Canal" title="Suez Canal">Suez Canal</a> and the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Red_Sea" title="Red Sea">Red Sea</a> along the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sinai_Peninsula" title="Sinai Peninsula">Sinai Peninsula</a> to the northeast, the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indian_Ocean" title="Indian Ocean">Indian Ocean</a> to the southeast, and the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic Ocean</a> to the west. The continent includes <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Madagascar" title="Madagascar">Madagascar</a> and various <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Archipelago" title="Archipelago">archipelagos</a>. It has 54 <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diplomatic_recognition" title="Diplomatic recognition">fully recognized</a> <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sovereign_state" title="Sovereign state">sovereign states</a> ("<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Country" title="Country">countries</a>"), nine <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Territory_(country_subdivision)" title="Territory (country subdivision)">territories</a> and two <i>de facto</i> independent <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_states_with_limited_recognition" title="List of states with limited recognition">states with limited or no recognition</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Africa#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Africa's population is the youngest among all the continents; 50% of Africans are 19 years old or younger.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Africa#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Algeria" title="Algeria">Algeria</a> is Africa's largest country by area, and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nigeria" title="Nigeria">Nigeria</a> by population. Africa, particularly central <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eastern_Africa" title="Eastern Africa" class="mw-redirect">Eastern Africa</a>, is widely accepted as the place of origin of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Human" title="Human">humans</a> and the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hominidae" title="Hominidae">Hominidae</a> <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Clade" title="Clade">clade</a> (<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Great_ape" title="Great ape" class="mw-redirect">great apes</a>), as <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Evidence" title="Evidence">evidenced</a> by the discovery of the earliest <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hominids" title="Hominids" class="mw-redirect">hominids</a> and their ancestors, as well as later ones that have been dated to around seven million years ago, including <i><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sahelanthropus_tchadensis" title="Sahelanthropus tchadensis" class="mw-redirect">Sahelanthropus tchadensis</a></i>, <i><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Australopithecus_africanus" title="Australopithecus africanus">Australopithecus africanus</a></i>, <i><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Australopithecus_afarensis" title="Australopithecus afarensis">A. afarensis</a></i>, <i><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Homo_erectus" title="Homo erectus">Homo erectus</a></i>, <i><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Homo_habilis" title="Homo habilis">H. habilis</a></i> and <i><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Homo_ergaster" title="Homo ergaster">H. ergaster</a></i> – with the earliest <i><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Homo_sapiens" title="Homo sapiens">Homo sapiens</a></i> (modern human) found in <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ethiopia" title="Ethiopia">Ethiopia</a> being dated to circa 200,000 years ago.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Africa#cite_note-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup> Africa straddles the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Equator" title="Equator">equator</a> and encompasses numerous climate areas; it is the only continent to stretch from the northern <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Temperate" title="Temperate" class="mw-redirect">temperate</a> to southern temperate zones.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Africa#cite_note-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
