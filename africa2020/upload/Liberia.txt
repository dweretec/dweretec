'a' in 'about'" style="border-bottom:1px dotted">ə</span></a></span><span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">/</a></span></span>, officially the <b>Republic of Liberia</b>, is a country in <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/West_Africa" title="West Africa">West Africa</a> bordered by <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sierra_Leone" title="Sierra Leone">Sierra Leone</a> to its west, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guinea" title="Guinea">Guinea</a> to its north and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ivory_Coast" title="Ivory Coast">Ivory Coast</a> to its east. It covers an area of 111,369 square kilometres (43,000&#160;sq&#160;mi) and is home to about 4 million people.<sup id="cite_ref-WorldBank_2-1" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Liberia#cite_note-WorldBank-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a> is the official language; 15 indigenous languages are also spoken within Liberia. Its coastline is composed mostly of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mangrove" title="Mangrove">mangroves</a>, while its more sparsely populated inland consists of forests opening onto a plateau of drier grasslands. The climate is hot and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tropical_rainforest_climate" title="Tropical rainforest climate">equatorial</a>, with significant rainfall during the May–October <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wet_season" title="Wet season">rainy season</a> and harsh <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Harmattan" title="Harmattan">harmattan</a> winds the remainder of the year. Liberia possesses about forty percent of the remaining <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Upper_Guinean_forest" title="Upper Guinean forest" class="mw-redirect">Upper Guinean rainforest</a>.</p>
<p>Beginning in 1822, the area was settled by <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/African_Americans" title="African Americans" class="mw-redirect">African Americans</a>, most of whom were free blacks. The settlers established a new home with the help of the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/American_Colonization_Society" title="American Colonization Society">American Colonization Society</a>, a private organization whose leaders encouraged African Americans to resettle in Africa.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Liberia#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In 1847, the settlers disbanded from the support of American Colonization Society and formalized their settlement as the Republic of Liberia, establishing a government modeled on that of the United States and naming its capital city <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Monrovia" title="Monrovia">Monrovia</a> after <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/James_Monroe" title="James Monroe">James Monroe</a>, the fifth <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/President_of_the_United_States" title="President of the United States">president of the United States</a> and a prominent supporter of the American Colonization Society. The colonists and their descendants, known as <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Americo-Liberians" title="Americo-Liberians" class="mw-redirect">Americo-Liberians</a>, led the political, social, cultural and economic sectors of the country and ruled the nation for over 130 years as a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dominant_minority" title="Dominant minority">dominant minority</a>.</p>
<p>Liberia began to modernize in the 1940s following investment by the United States during <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a> and economic liberalization under President <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/William_Tubman" title="William Tubman">William Tubman</a>. Liberia was a founding member of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/League_of_Nations" title="League of Nations">League of Nations</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations</a> and the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Organisation_of_African_Unity" title="Organisation of African Unity">Organisation of African Unity</a>. In 1980 a military <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coup" title="Coup" class="mw-redirect">coup</a> overthrew the True-Whig Party leadership, marking the beginning of political instability. Five years of military rule by the People Redemption Council and five years of civilian rule by the National Democratic Party of Liberia were followed by two civil wars – the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/First_Liberian_Civil_War" title="First Liberian Civil War">First</a> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Second_Liberian_Civil_War" title="Second Liberian Civil War">Second Liberian Civil Wars</a>. These resulted in the deaths of between 250,000 and 520,000 people and devastated <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Economy_of_Liberia" title="Economy of Liberia">Liberia's economy</a>. A <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Accra_Comprehensive_Peace_Agreement" title="Accra Comprehensive Peace Agreement">peace agreement in 2003</a> led to democratic elections in 2005. Today, about 85% of the population live below the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_poverty_line" title="International poverty line" class="mw-redirect">international poverty line</a>. Liberia's economic and political stability has recently been threatened by a deadly <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ebola_virus_epidemic_in_West_Africa" title="Ebola virus epidemic in West Africa">Ebola virus epidemic</a> which originated in Guinea in December 2013 and entered Liberia in March 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Liberia#cite_note-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup>The outbreak was declared over on May 8, 2015, after 42 days with no new cases.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Liberia#cite_note-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
