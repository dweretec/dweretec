The country and its <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Swazi_people" title="Swazi people">people</a> take their names from <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mswati_II" title="Mswati II">Mswati II</a>, the 19th-century king under whose rule Swazi territory was expanded and unified.<sup id="cite_ref-Kuper86_5-0" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Swaziland#cite_note-Kuper86-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>At no more than 200 kilometres (120&#160;mi) north to south and 130 kilometres (81&#160;mi) east to west, Swaziland is one of the smallest countries in Africa. Despite its size, however, its climate and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Topography" title="Topography">topography</a> is diverse, ranging from a cool and mountainous <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Highveld" title="Highveld">highveld</a> to a hot and dry <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Veld#Lowveld" title="Veld">lowveld</a>. The population is primarily ethnic <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Swazi_people" title="Swazi people">Swazis</a> whose language is <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Swati_language" title="Swati language" class="mw-redirect">siSwati</a>. They established their kingdom in the mid-18th century under the leadership of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ngwane_III" title="Ngwane III">Ngwane III</a>; the present boundaries were drawn up in 1881.<sup id="cite_ref-Bonner82_6-0" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Swaziland#cite_note-Bonner82-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup> After the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anglo-Boer_War" title="Anglo-Boer War" class="mw-redirect">Anglo-Boer War</a>, Swaziland was a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Kingdom_of_Great_Britain_and_Ireland" title="United Kingdom of Great Britain and Ireland">British</a> <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Protectorate" title="Protectorate">protectorate</a> from 1903 until 1967. It regained its independence on 6 September 1968.<sup id="cite_ref-Gillis99_7-0" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Swaziland#cite_note-Gillis99-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The country is an <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Absolute_monarchy" title="Absolute monarchy">absolute monarchy</a>, currently ruled by <i><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ngwenyama" title="Ngwenyama">Ngwenyama</a></i> ("King") <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mswati_III" title="Mswati III">Mswati III</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Swaziland#cite_note-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Swaziland#cite_note-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup> He is <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Head_of_state" title="Head of state">head of state</a> and appoints the country's prime minister and a number of representatives of both chambers (<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Senate_of_Swaziland" title="Senate of Swaziland">Senate</a> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/House_of_Assembly_of_Swaziland" title="House of Assembly of Swaziland">House of Assembly</a>) in <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Parliament_of_Swaziland" title="Parliament of Swaziland">the country's parliament</a>. Elections are held every five years to determine the House of Assembly majority. The current <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Constitution_of_Swaziland" title="Constitution of Swaziland" class="mw-redirect">constitution</a> was adopted in 2005.</p>
<p>Swaziland is a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Developing_country" title="Developing country">developing country</a> with a small economy. Its <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gross_domestic_product" title="Gross domestic product">GDP per capita</a> of $6,367 means it is classified as a country with a lower-middle income.<sup id="cite_ref-imf2_1-5" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Swaziland#cite_note-imf2-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> As a member of the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Southern_African_Customs_Union" title="Southern African Customs Union">Southern African Customs Union</a> (SACU) and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Common_Market_for_Eastern_and_Southern_Africa" title="Common Market for Eastern and Southern Africa">Common Market for Eastern and Southern Africa</a> (COMESA), its main local trading partner is South Africa. Swazilands currency, the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Swazi_lilangeni" title="Swazi lilangeni">lilangeni</a>, is <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fixed_exchange-rate_system" title="Fixed exchange-rate system">pegged</a> to the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_African_rand" title="South African rand">South African rand</a>. Swaziland's major overseas trading partners are the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Swaziland#cite_note-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup> and the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Swaziland#cite_note-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup> The majority of the country's employment is provided by its agricultural and manufacturing sectors. Swaziland is a member of the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Southern_African_Development_Community" title="Southern African Development Community">Southern African Development Community</a>, the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/African_Union" title="African Union">African Union</a> and the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Commonwealth_of_Nations" title="Commonwealth of Nations">Commonwealth of Nations</a>.</p>
<p>The Swazi population faces major health issues: <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/HIV/AIDS" title="HIV/AIDS">HIV/AIDS</a> and, to a lesser extent, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tuberculosis" title="Tuberculosis">tuberculosis</a> are serious challenges.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Swaziland#cite_note-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Swaziland#cite_note-13"><span>[</span>13<span>]</span></a></sup> As of 2013, Swaziland has an estimated life expectancy of 50 years.<sup id="cite_ref-rank_14-0" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Swaziland#cite_note-rank-14"><span>[</span>14<span>]</span></a></sup> The population of Swaziland is fairly young with a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Median_age" title="Median age" class="mw-redirect">median age</a> of 20.5 years and people aged 14 years or younger constituting 37.4% of the country's total population.<sup id="cite_ref-Demo_15-0" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Swaziland#cite_note-Demo-15"><span>[</span>15<span>]</span></a></sup> The present population growth rate is 1.195%.<sup id="cite_ref-Demo_15-1" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Swaziland#cite_note-Demo-15"><span>[</span>15<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Swaziland#cite_note-16"><span>[</span>16<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Swaziland is well known for its culture.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Swaziland#cite_note-17"><span>[</span>17<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Swaziland#cite_note-18"><span>[</span>18<span>]</span></a></sup> <i><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Umhlanga_(ceremony)" title="Umhlanga (ceremony)">Umhlanga</a></i>, held in August/September<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Swaziland#cite_note-19"><span>[</span>19<span>]</span></a></sup> and <i><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Incwala" title="Incwala">incwala</a></i>, the kingship dance held in December/January, are the nation's most important events.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Swaziland#cite_note-20"><span>[</span>20<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
