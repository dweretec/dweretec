<p>The <b>Gospel of Matthew</b> (Gk. <span lang="grc" xml:lang="grc">Κατὰ Ματθαῖον εὐαγγέλιον</span>, <i>Kata Matthaion Euangelion</i> or <span lang="grc" xml:lang="grc">τὸ εὐαγγέλιον κατὰ Ματθαῖον</span>, <i>To Euangelion kata Matthaion</i>) is one of the four <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Canonical" title="Canonical">canonical</a> gospels in the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_Testament" title="New Testament">New Testament</a>. This <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Synoptic_gospel" title="Synoptic gospel" class="mw-redirect">synoptic gospel</a> is an account of the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_Testament_view_on_Jesus%27_life" title="New Testament view on Jesus' life">life</a> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ministry_of_Jesus" title="Ministry of Jesus">ministry</a> of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jesus_of_Nazareth" title="Jesus of Nazareth" class="mw-redirect">Jesus of Nazareth</a>. It details his story from his <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Genealogy_of_Jesus" title="Genealogy of Jesus">genealogy</a> to his <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Great_Commission" title="Great Commission">Great Commission</a>.</p>
<p>The <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Early_Christian" title="Early Christian" class="mw-redirect">Early Christian</a> tradition attributes this Gospel to <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Matthew_the_Evangelist" title="Matthew the Evangelist">Matthew</a>, one of Jesus' disciples.<sup id="cite_ref-0" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gospel_of_Matthew#cite_note-0"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
