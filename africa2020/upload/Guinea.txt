Formerly known as <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/French_Guinea" title="French Guinea">French Guinea</a> (<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>: <span lang="fr"><i>Guinée française</i></span>), it is today sometimes called <b>Guinea-Conakry</b> to distinguish it from its neighbour <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guinea-Bissau" title="Guinea-Bissau">Guinea-Bissau</a> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Equatorial_Guinea" title="Equatorial Guinea">Equatorial Guinea</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guinea#cite_note-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup> Guinea has a population of 10.5 million and an area of 245,860 square kilometres (94,927&#160;sq&#160;mi).<sup id="cite_ref-guinea_stats_1-1" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guinea#cite_note-guinea_stats-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Guinea is a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Republic" title="Republic">republic</a>. The president is directly elected by the people and is <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Head_of_state" title="Head of state">head of state</a> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Head_of_government" title="Head of government">head of government</a>. The unicameral <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_Assembly_(Guinea)" title="National Assembly (Guinea)">Guinean National Assembly</a> is the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Legislative_body" title="Legislative body" class="mw-redirect">legislative body</a> of the country, and its members are directly elected by the people. The judicial branch is led by the Guinea Supreme Court, the highest and final court of appeal in the country.<sup id="cite_ref-reuters.com_8-0" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guinea#cite_note-reuters.com-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Guinea is a predominantly <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Islam" title="Islam">Islamic</a> country, with <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Muslim" title="Muslim">Muslims</a> representing 85 percent of the population.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guinea#cite_note-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup> Guinea's people belong to twenty-four <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Demographics_of_Guinea" title="Demographics of Guinea">ethnic groups</a>. French, the official language of Guinea, is the main language of communication in schools, in government administration, in the media, and among the country's <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Security_forces" title="Security forces" class="mw-redirect">security forces</a>, but more than twenty-four indigenous languages are also spoken.</p>
<p>Guinea's economy is largely dependent on agriculture and mineral production.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guinea#cite_note-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup> It is the world's second largest producer of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bauxite" title="Bauxite">bauxite</a>, and has rich deposits of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diamonds" title="Diamonds" class="mw-redirect">diamonds</a> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gold" title="Gold">gold</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-guineaconakrysupport.com_11-0" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guinea#cite_note-guineaconakrysupport.com-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Human_rights_in_Guinea" title="Human rights in Guinea">Human rights in Guinea</a> remain controversial. In 2011 the United States government claimed that <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Torture" title="Torture">torture</a> by security forces, and abuse of women and children (e.g. <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Female_genital_mutilation" title="Female genital mutilation">female genital mutilation</a>) were ongoing abuses of human rights.<sup id="cite_ref-State_2011_12-0" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guinea#cite_note-State_2011-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The country is named after the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guinea_(region)" title="Guinea (region)">Guinea region</a>. Guinea is a traditional name for the region of Africa that lies along the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gulf_of_Guinea" title="Gulf of Guinea">Gulf of Guinea</a>. It stretches north through the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tropical_and_subtropical_moist_broadleaf_forests" title="Tropical and subtropical moist broadleaf forests">forested tropical regions</a> and ends at the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sahel" title="Sahel">Sahel</a>. The English term Guinea comes directly from the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">Portuguese</a> word <i>Guiné</i>, which emerged in the mid-15th century to refer to the lands inhabited by the <i>Guineus</i>, a generic term for the black African peoples below the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Senegal_River" title="Senegal River">Senegal River</a>, as opposed to the 'tawny' Zenaga Berbers, above it, whom they called <i>Azenegues</i> or <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Moors" title="Moors">Moors</a>.</p>
<p>The country is currently at the core of the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2014_Ebola_outbreak" title="2014 Ebola outbreak" class="mw-redirect">2014 Ebola outbreak</a> giving it a bleak prospect for the upcoming years in terms of humanitarian situation and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/GDP" title="GDP" class="mw-redirect">GDP</a> growth.</p>
