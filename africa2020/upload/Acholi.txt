<p><b>Acholi</b> (also <b>Acoli</b>) is an <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ethnic_group" title="Ethnic group">ethnic group</a> from the districts of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gulu_District" title="Gulu District">Gulu</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kitgum_District" title="Kitgum District">Kitgum</a> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pader_District" title="Pader District">Pader</a> in northern <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Uganda" title="Uganda">Uganda</a> (an area commonly referred to as <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acholiland" title="Acholiland" class="mw-redirect">Acholiland</a>), and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Magwe_County" title="Magwe County" class="mw-redirect">Magwe County</a> in southern <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sudan" title="Sudan">Sudan</a>. The 1991 Uganda census counted 746,796 Acholi; a further 45,000 Acholi live outside of Uganda.<sup id="cite_ref-0" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acholi#cite_note-0"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
