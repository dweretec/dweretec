id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sudan#cite_note-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup> officially the <b>Republic of the Sudan</b><sup id="cite_ref-cia_12-0" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sudan#cite_note-cia-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup> (<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arabic_language" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nile_Valley" title="Nile Valley" class="mw-redirect">Nile Valley</a> of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/North_Africa" title="North Africa">North Africa</a>, bordered by <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egypt</a> to the north, the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Red_Sea" title="Red Sea">Red Sea</a>, <a It is the third largest country in Africa. The <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nile" title="Nile">Nile</a> River divides the country into eastern and western halves.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sudan#cite_note-13"><span>[</span>13<span>]</span></a></sup> Its predominant religion is <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Islam" title="Islam">Islam</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_14-0" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sudan#cite_note-autogenerated1-14"><span>[</span>14<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Sudan was home to numerous ancient civilizations, such as the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kingdom_of_Kush" title="Kingdom of Kush">Kingdom of Kush</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kerma" title="Kerma">Kerma</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nobatia" title="Nobatia">Nobatia</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alodia" title="Alodia">Alodia</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Makuria" title="Makuria" class="mw-redirect">Makuria</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mero%C3%AB" title="Meroë">Meroë</a> and others, most of which flourished along the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nile_River" title="Nile River" class="mw-redirect">Nile River</a>. During the predynastic period Nubia and Nagadan Upper Egypt were identical, simultaneously evolved systems of pharaonic kingship by 3300 BC.<sup id="cite_ref-Keita.2C_S.O.Y._1993_129.E2.80.9354_15-0" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sudan#cite_note-Keita.2C_S.O.Y._1993_129.E2.80.9354-15"><span>[</span>15<span>]</span></a></sup> By virtue of its proximity to <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/History_of_Egypt" title="History of Egypt">Egypt</a>, the Sudan participated in the wider history of the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Near_East" title="Near East">Near East</a> inasmuch as it was <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christianized" title="Christianized" class="mw-redirect">Christianized</a> by the 6th century, and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Islamization_of_Sudan" title="Islamization of Sudan" class="mw-redirect">Islamized</a> in the 7th. As a result of Christianization, the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Old_Nubian_language" title="Old Nubian language">Old Nubian language</a> stands as the oldest recorded <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nilo-Saharan" title="Nilo-Saharan" class="mw-redirect">Nilo-Saharan</a> language (earliest records dating to the 9th century). Sudan was the largest country in Africa and the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arab_world" title="Arab world">Arab world</a> until 2011, when <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Sudan" title="South Sudan">South Sudan</a> separated into an independent country, following an <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Independence_referendum" title="Independence referendum">independence referendum</a>. Sudan is now the third largest country in Africa (after <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Algeria" title="Algeria">Algeria</a> and the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Democratic_Republic_of_the_Congo" title="Democratic Republic of the Congo">Democratic Republic of the Congo</a>) and also the third largest country in the Arab world (after <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Algeria" title="Algeria">Algeria</a> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Saudi_Arabia" title="Saudi Arabia">Saudi Arabia</a>).</p>
<p>Sudan is a member of the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations</a>, the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/African_Union" title="African Union">African Union</a>, the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arab_League" title="Arab League">Arab League</a>, the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Organisation_of_Islamic_Cooperation" title="Organisation of Islamic Cooperation">Organisation of Islamic Cooperation</a> and the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Non-Aligned_Movement" title="Non-Aligned Movement">Non-Aligned Movement</a>, as well as an observer in the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_Trade_Organization" title="World Trade Organization">World Trade Organization</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-cia_12-1" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sudan#cite_note-cia-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sudan#cite_note-16"><span>[</span>16<span>]</span></a></sup> Its capital is <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Khartoum" title="Khartoum">Khartoum</a>, the political, cultural and commercial centre of the nation. It is a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Federal_republic" title="Federal republic">federal</a> <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Presidential_system" title="Presidential system">presidential</a> <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Representative_democracy" title="Representative democracy">representative democratic</a> <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Republic" title="Republic">republic</a>. The <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Politics_of_Sudan" title="Politics of Sudan">politics of Sudan</a> is regulated by a parliamentary organization called the National Assembly.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sudan#cite_note-17"><span>[</span>17<span>]</span></a></sup> The <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Politics_of_Sudan#Legal_system" title="Politics of Sudan">Sudanese legal system</a> is <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Application_of_sharia_law_by_country" title="Application of sharia law by country">based on Islamic law</a>.</p>
<p>Sudan suffers from several challenges. For much of Sudan's history, the nation has suffered from rampant ethnic strife and has been plagued by <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sudan_internal_conflict_(2011%E2%80%93present)" title="Sudan internal conflict (2011–present)" class="mw-redirect">internal conflicts</a> including <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/First_Sudanese_Civil_War" title="First Sudanese Civil War">two civil</a> <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Second_Sudanese_Civil_War" title="Second Sudanese Civil War">wars</a> and the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/War_in_Darfur" title="War in Darfur">War in the Darfur region</a>. Sudan suffers from poor human rights most particularly dealing with the issues of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ethnic_cleansing" title="Ethnic cleansing">ethnic cleansing</a> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Slavery_in_Sudan" title="Slavery in Sudan">slavery</a> in the nation.<sup id="cite_ref-2009_Human_Rights_Report_18-0" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sudan#cite_note-2009_Human_Rights_Report-18"><span>[</span>18<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
