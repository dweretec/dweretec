<p><b>Food</b> is any substance, usually composed of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carbohydrate" title="Carbohydrate">carbohydrates</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fat" title="Fat">fats</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Protein" title="Protein">proteins</a> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Water" title="Water">water</a>, that can be <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eating" title="Eating">eaten</a> or <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Drinking" title="Drinking">drunk</a> by an animal, including humans, for <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nutrition" title="Nutrition">nutrition</a> or pleasure. Items considered food may be sourced from <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plants" title="Plants" class="mw-redirect">plants</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Animals" title="Animals" class="mw-redirect">animals</a> or other categories such as <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fungus" title="Fungus">fungus</a> or fermented products like <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ethanol" title="Ethanol">alcohol</a>. Although many human cultures sought food items through <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hunter-gatherer" title="Hunter-gatherer">hunting and gathering</a>, today most cultures use <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Farming" title="Farming" class="mw-redirect">farming</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ranching" title="Ranching" class="mw-redirect">ranching</a>, and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fishing" title="Fishing">fishing</a>, with <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hunting" title="Hunting">hunting</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Foraging" title="Foraging">foraging</a> and other methods of a local nature included but playing a minor role.</p>
<p>Most traditions have a recognizable <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cuisine" title="Cuisine">cuisine</a>, a specific set of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cooking" title="Cooking">cooking</a> traditions, preferences, and practices, the study of which is known as <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gastronomy" title="Gastronomy">gastronomy</a>. Many cultures have diversified their foods by means of preparation, cooking methods and manufacturing. This also includes a complex food trade which helps the cultures to economically survive by-way-of food, not just by consumption.</p>
<p>Many cultures study the dietary analysis of food <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Habit_(psychology)" title="Habit (psychology)">habits</a>. While humans are <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Omnivore" title="Omnivore">omnivores</a>, religion and social constructs such as <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Morality" title="Morality">morality</a> often affect which foods they will consume. Food safety is also a concern with <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Foodborne_illness" title="Foodborne illness">foodborne illness</a> claiming many lives each year. In many languages, food is often used metaphorically or figuratively, as in "food for thought".</p>
