<p><b>Mao Zedong</b> (<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Simplified_Chinese_characters" title="Simplified Chinese characters">simplified Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hans" xml:lang="zh-Hans">毛泽东</span>;&#160;<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Traditional_Chinese_characters" title="Traditional Chinese characters">traditional Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hant" xml:lang="zh-Hant">毛澤東</span>;&#160;<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pinyin" title="Pinyin">pinyin</a>: Máo Zédōng;&#160;<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wade-Giles" title="Wade-Giles" class="mw-redirect">Wade-Giles</a>: <b>Mao Tse-tung</b>) <span class="unicode" style="white-space:nowrap;"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Zh-Mao_Zedong.ogg" title="Zh-Mao_Zedong.ogg"><img alt="Zh-Mao_Zedong.ogg" src="http://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/11px-Loudspeaker.svg.png" width="11" height="11" /></a> <a href="http://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/f/fe/Zh-Mao_Zedong.ogg" class="internal" title="Zh-Mao Zedong.ogg">pronunciation</a> <small class="metadata audiolinkinfo" style="cursor:help;">(<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Media_help" title="Wikipedia:Media help"><span style="cursor:help;">help</span></a>·<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Zh-Mao_Zedong.ogg" title="File:Zh-Mao Zedong.ogg"><span style="cursor:help;">info</span></a>)</small></span> (26 December 1893&#160;– 9 September 1976) was a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Han_Chinese" title="Han Chinese">Chinese</a> revolutionary, political theorist and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Communism" title="Communism">Communist</a> leader. He led the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/People%27s_Republic_of_China" title="People's Republic of China">People's Republic of China</a> (PRC) from its establishment in 1949 until his death in 1976. His theoretical contribution to <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marxism" title="Marxism">Marxism</a>-<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Leninism" title="Leninism">Leninism</a>, military strategies, and his brand of Communist policies are now collectively known as <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maoism" title="Maoism">Maoism</a>.</p>
<p>Mao remains a controversial figure to this day, with a contentious and ever-evolving legacy. He is officially held in high regard in China as a great revolutionary, political strategist, military mastermind, and savior of the nation. Many Chinese also believe that through his policies, he laid the economic, technological and cultural foundations of modern China, transforming the country from a backward agrarian society into a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Major_power" title="Major power" class="mw-redirect">major world power</a>. Additionally, Mao is viewed by many in China as a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Poet" title="Poet" class="mw-redirect">poet</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Philosopher" title="Philosopher" class="mw-redirect">philosopher</a>, and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Visionary" title="Visionary">visionary</a>, owing the latter primarily to the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cult_of_personality" title="Cult of personality">cult of personality</a> fostered during his time in power.<sup id="cite_ref-0" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mao#cite_note-0"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> As a consequence, his portrait continues to be featured prominently on <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tiananmen" title="Tiananmen">Tiananmen</a> and on all <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Renminbi" title="Renminbi">Renminbi</a> bills.</p>
<p>Conversely, Mao's social-political programs, such as the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Great_Leap_Forward" title="Great Leap Forward">Great Leap Forward</a> and the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cultural_Revolution" title="Cultural Revolution">Cultural Revolution</a>, are blamed for causing severe <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Famine" title="Famine">famine</a> and damage to the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_culture" title="Chinese culture" class="mw-redirect">culture</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_society" title="Chinese society" class="mw-redirect">society</a> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Economy_of_the_People%27s_Republic_of_China" title="Economy of the People's Republic of China">economy</a> of China. Mao's policies and political purges from 1949 to 1975 are widely believed to have caused the deaths of between 40 to 60 million people.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mao#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-deathtoll_2-0" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mao#cite_note-deathtoll-2"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Fenby_3-0" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mao#cite_note-Fenby-3"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> Since <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Deng_Xiaoping" title="Deng Xiaoping">Deng Xiaoping</a> assumed power in 1978, many Maoist policies have been abandoned in favour of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Economic_reform_in_the_People%27s_Republic_of_China" title="Economic reform in the People's Republic of China">economic reforms</a>.</p>
<p>Mao is regarded as one of the most influential figures in modern world history,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mao#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> and named by <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Time_Magazine" title="Time Magazine" class="mw-redirect">Time Magazine</a> as one of the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Time_100:_The_Most_Important_People_of_the_Century" title="Time 100: The Most Important People of the Century">100 most influential</a> people of the 20th century.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mao#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
