Africa, is a country in southern Africa whose western border is the Atlantic Ocean. It shares land borders with <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zambia" title="Zambia">Zambia</a> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Angola" title="Angola">Angola</a> to the north, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Botswana" title="Botswana">Botswana</a> to the east and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">South Africa</a> to the south and east. Although it does not border <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zimbabwe" title="Zimbabwe">Zimbabwe</a>, less than 200 metres of the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zambezi" title="Zambezi">Zambezi River</a> (essentially a small bulge in Botswana to achieve a Botswana/Zambia micro-border) separates them at their closest points. It gained independence from South Africa on 21 March 1990, following the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Namibian_War_of_Independence" title="Namibian War of Independence">Namibian War of Independence</a>. Its capital and largest city is <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Windhoek" title="Windhoek">Windhoek</a>. Namibia is a member state of the United Nations (UN), the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Southern_African_Development_Community" title="Southern African Development Community">Southern African Development Community</a> (SADC), the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/African_Union" title="African Union">African Union</a> (AU), and the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Commonwealth_of_Nations" title="Commonwealth of Nations">Commonwealth of Nations</a>.</p>
<p>The dry lands of Namibia were inhabited since early times by <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/San_people" title="San people">San</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Damara_(people)" title="Damara (people)" class="mw-redirect">Damara</a>, and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nama_people" title="Nama people">Namaqua</a>, and since about the 14th century AD by <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Immigrating" title="Immigrating" class="mw-redirect">immigrating</a> <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bantu_peoples" title="Bantu peoples">Bantu</a> who came with the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bantu_expansion" title="Bantu expansion">Bantu expansion</a>. Most of the territory became a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/German_Empire" title="German Empire">German Imperial</a> protectorate in 1884 and remained a German colony until the end of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a>. In 1920, the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/League_of_Nations" title="League of Nations">League of Nations</a> mandated the country to South Africa, which imposed its laws and, from 1948, its <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Africa_under_apartheid" title="South Africa under apartheid" class="mw-redirect">apartheid</a> policy. The port of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Walvis_Bay" title="Walvis Bay">Walvis Bay</a> and the offshore <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Penguin_Islands" title="Penguin Islands">Penguin Islands</a> had been annexed by the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/British_Cape_Colony" title="British Cape Colony" class="mw-redirect">Cape Colony</a> under the British crown by 1878 and had become an integral part of the new <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Union_of_South_Africa" title="Union of South Africa">Union of South Africa</a> at its creation in 1910.</p>
<p>Uprisings and demands by African leaders led the UN to assume direct responsibility over the territory. It recognised the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SWAPO" title="SWAPO">South West Africa People's Organisation</a> (SWAPO) as the official representative of the Namibian people in 1973. Namibia, however, remained under South African administration during this time as South-West Africa. Following internal violence, South Africa installed an interim administration in Namibia in 1985. Namibia obtained full independence from South Africa in 1990, with the exception of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Walvis_Bay" title="Walvis Bay">Walvis Bay</a> and the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Penguin_Islands" title="Penguin Islands">Penguin Islands</a>, which remained under South African control until 1994.</p>
<p>Namibia has a population of 2.1&#160;million people and a stable <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Multi-party_system" title="Multi-party system">multi-party</a> <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Parliamentary_democracy" title="Parliamentary democracy" class="mw-redirect">parliamentary democracy</a>. Agriculture, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Herding" title="Herding">herding</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tourism_in_Namibia" title="Tourism in Namibia">tourism</a> and the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mineral_industry_of_Namibia" title="Mineral industry of Namibia" class="mw-redirect">mining industry</a> – including mining for gem diamonds, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Uranium_mining" title="Uranium mining">uranium</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gold_mining" title="Gold mining">gold</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Silver_mining" title="Silver mining">silver</a>, and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Base_metal" title="Base metal">base metals</a> – form the basis of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Economy_of_Namibia" title="Economy of Namibia">Namibia's economy</a>. Given the presence of the arid <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Namib_Desert" title="Namib Desert">Namib Desert</a>, it is one of the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_sovereign_states_and_dependent_territories_by_population_density" title="List of sovereign states and dependent territories by population density">least densely populated</a> countries in the world. Namibia enjoys high political, economic and social stability.</p>
