<p><b>Equatorial Guinea</b> (<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>: <span lang="es" xml:lang="es"><i>Guinea Ecuatorial</i></span>,<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Equatorial_Guinea#cite_note-7"><span>[</span>a<span>]</span></a></sup> <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>: <span lang="fr" xml:lang="fr"><i>Guinée équatoriale</i></span>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">Portuguese</a>: <span lang="pt" xml:lang="pt"><i>Guiné Equatorial</i></span>), officially the <b>Republic of Equatorial Guinea</b> (<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>: <span lang="es" xml:lang="es"><i>República de Guinea Ecuatorial</i></span>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>: <span lang="fr" xml:lang="fr"><i>République de Guinée équatoriale</i></span>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">Portuguese</a>: <span lang="pt" xml:lang="pt"><i>República da Guiné Equatorial</i></span>),<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Equatorial_Guinea#cite_note-8"><span>[</span>b<span>]</span></a></sup> is a country located in <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Middle_Africa" title="Middle Africa" class="mw-redirect">Central Africa</a>, with an area of 28,000 square kilometres (11,000&#160;sq&#160;mi). Formerly the colony of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanish_Guinea" title="Spanish Guinea">Spanish Guinea</a>, its post-independence name evokes its location near both the equator and the Gulf of Guinea. Equatorial Guinea is the only sovereign African state in which <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a> is an official language. As of 2012, the country has a population of 1.6 million.<sup id="cite_ref-unpop_4-1" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Equatorial_Guinea#cite_note-unpop-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Equatorial Guinea consists of two parts, an insular and a mainland region. The <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Islands_of_Equatorial_Guinea" title="Islands of Equatorial Guinea" class="mw-redirect">insular region</a> consists of the islands of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bioko" title="Bioko">Bioko</a> (formerly <i>Fernando Pó</i>) in the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gulf_of_Guinea" title="Gulf of Guinea">Gulf of Guinea</a> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Annob%C3%B3n_Province" title="Annobón Province">Annobón</a>, a small volcanic island south of the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Equator" title="Equator">equator</a>. Bioko Island is the northernmost part of Equatorial Guinea and is the site of the country's capital, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Malabo" title="Malabo">Malabo</a>. The island nation of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Tom%C3%A9_and_Pr%C3%ADncipe" title="São Tomé and Príncipe">São Tomé and Príncipe</a> is located between Bioko and Annobón. The mainland region, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/R%C3%ADo_Muni" title="Río Muni">Río Muni</a>, is bordered by <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cameroon" title="Cameroon">Cameroon</a> on the north and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gabon" title="Gabon">Gabon</a> on the south and east. It is the location of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bata,_Equatorial_Guinea" title="Bata, Equatorial Guinea">Bata</a>, Equatorial Guinea's largest city, and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oyala" title="Oyala">Oyala</a>, the country's planned future capital. Rio Muni also includes several small offshore islands, such as <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Corisco" title="Corisco">Corisco</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elobey_Grande" title="Elobey Grande">Elobey Grande</a>, and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elobey_Chico" title="Elobey Chico">Elobey Chico</a>.</p>
<p>Since the mid-1990s, Equatorial Guinea has become one of sub-Sahara's largest oil producers. With a population of almost two million, it is the richest country per capita in Africa,<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Equatorial_Guinea#cite_note-9"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup> and its gross domestic product (GDP) per capita ranks 69th in the world;<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Equatorial_Guinea#cite_note-10"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup> However, the wealth is distributed very unevenly and few people have benefited from the oil riches. The country ranks 144th on the UN's 2014 <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Human_Development_Index" title="Human Development Index">Human Development Index</a>. The UN says that less than half of the population has access to clean drinking water and that 20% of children die before reaching five.</p>
<p>The authoritarian <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Regime" title="Regime">regime</a> ruling Equatorial Guinea has one of the worst human rights records in the world, consistently ranking among the "worst of the worst" in <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Freedom_House" title="Freedom House">Freedom House</a>'s <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Freedom_in_the_World" title="Freedom in the World">annual survey of political and civil rights</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Equatorial_Guinea#cite_note-11"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup> <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reporters_Without_Borders" title="Reporters Without Borders">Reporters Without Borders</a> ranks President <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Teodoro_Obiang_Nguema_Mbasogo" title="Teodoro Obiang Nguema Mbasogo">Teodoro Obiang Nguema Mbasogo</a> among its "predators" of press freedom.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Equatorial_Guinea#cite_note-12"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup> <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Human_trafficking_in_Equatorial_Guinea" title="Human trafficking in Equatorial Guinea">Human trafficking</a> is a significant problem, with the US Trafficking in Persons Report, 2012, stating that "Equatorial Guinea is a source and destination for women and children subjected to forced labor and sex trafficking." The report rates Equatorial Guinea as a "Tier 3" country, the lowest (worst) ranking: "Countries whose governments do not fully comply with the minimum standards and are not making significant efforts to do so."<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Equatorial_Guinea#cite_note-13"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The country is a member of the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/African_Union" title="African Union">African Union</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Organisation_internationale_de_la_Francophonie" title="Organisation internationale de la Francophonie">Francophonie</a> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Community_of_Portuguese_Language_Countries" title="Community of Portuguese Language Countries">CPLP</a>.</p>
