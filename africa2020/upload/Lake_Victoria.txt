<p><b>Lake Victoria</b> or <b>Victoria Nyanza</b> (also known as <b>Ukerewe</b>, <b>The Eye of the Rhino,</b> <b>Nalubaale,</b> <b>Sango</b>, or <b>Lolwe</b>) is one of the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/African_Great_Lakes" title="African Great Lakes">African Great Lakes</a>. The lake was named for <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Queen_Victoria_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Queen Victoria of the United Kingdom" class="mw-redirect">Queen Victoria of the United Kingdom</a>, by <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Hanning_Speke" title="John Hanning Speke">John Hanning Speke</a>, the first European to visit this lake.</p>
<p>With a surface area of 68,800&#160;square kilometres (26,600 sq&#160;mi), Lake Victoria is Africa’s largest lake by area, and it is the largest <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tropics" title="Tropics">tropical</a> lake in the world. Lake Victoria is the world's second largest <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Freshwater" title="Freshwater">freshwater</a> lake by surface area (Only <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lake_Superior" title="Lake Superior">Lake Superior</a> in North America is larger.) In terms of its volume, Lake Victoria is the world's <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_lakes_by_volume" title="List of lakes by volume">eighth largest continental lake</a>, and it contains about 2,750 cubic kilometres (2.2 billion <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acre-feet" title="Acre-feet" class="mw-redirect">acre-feet</a>) of water.</p>
<p>Lake Victoria receives most of its water from direct <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Precipitation_(meteorology)" title="Precipitation (meteorology)">precipitation</a> or from thousands of small <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stream" title="Stream">streams</a>. The largest stream flowing into this lake is the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kagera_River" title="Kagera River">Kagera River</a>, the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/River_delta" title="River delta">mouth</a> of which lies on the lake's western shore. The only river to leave the lake, the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/White_Nile" title="White Nile">White Nile</a> (known as the "Victoria Nile" as it leaves the lake), flows out at <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jinja,_Uganda" title="Jinja, Uganda">Jinja, Uganda</a>, on the lake's north shore.<sup id="cite_ref-0" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lake_Victoria#cite_note-0"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Lake Victoria occupies a shallow depression in the <a href="/w/index.php?title=East_African_Plateau&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="East African Plateau (page does not exist)">East African Plateau</a>, and has a maximum depth of 84&#160;metres (276 ft) and an average depth of 20&#160;metres (66 ft). Its <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Drainage_basin" title="Drainage basin">catchment area</a> covers 184,000 square kilometres (71,040&#160;sq&#160;mi). The lake has a shoreline of 4,828&#160;kilometres (3,000 mi), with islands constituting 3.7% of this length,<sup id="cite_ref-Hickling61_1-0" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lake_Victoria#cite_note-Hickling61-1"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> and is divided among three countries: <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kenya" title="Kenya">Kenya</a> (6% or 4,100&#160;km<sup>2</sup>/1,600 sq&#160;mi), <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Uganda" title="Uganda">Uganda</a> (45% or 31,000&#160;km<sup>2</sup>/12,000 sq&#160;mi) and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tanzania" title="Tanzania">Tanzania</a> (49% or 33,700&#160;km<sup>2</sup>/13,000 sq&#160;mi).<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lake_Victoria#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Lake Victoria supports Africa's largest inland <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fishery" title="Fishery">fishery</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Geheb97_3-0" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lake_Victoria#cite_note-Geheb97-3"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
