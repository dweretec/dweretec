English"><span title="/ə/ 'a' in 'about'" style="border-bottom:1px dotted">ə</span></a></span><span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">/</a></span></span>, officially the <b>Republic of Angola</b> (<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">Portuguese</a>: <span lang="pt" xml:lang="pt"><i>República de Angola</i></span> <small>pronounced:&#160;</small><span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Portuguese_and_Galician" title="Help:IPA for Portuguese and Galician">[ʁɛˈpublikɐ dɨ ɐ̃ˈɡɔlɐ]</a></span>; <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kikongo" title="Kikongo" class="mw-redirect">Kikongo</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kimbundu" title="Kimbundu">Kimbundu</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Umbundu" title="Umbundu">Umbundu</a>: <i>Repubilika ya Ngola</i>), is a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Country" title="Country">country</a> in <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Southern_Africa" title="Southern Africa">Southern Africa</a>. It is the seventh-largest country in Africa, and is bordered by <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Namibia" title="Namibia">Namibia</a> to the south, the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Democratic_Republic_of_the_Congo" title="Democratic Republic of the Congo">Democratic Republic of the Congo</a> to the north, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zambia" title="Zambia">Zambia</a> to the east, and the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic Ocean</a> to west. The <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Enclave_and_exclave" title="Enclave and exclave">exclave</a> province of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cabinda_Province" title="Cabinda Province">Cabinda</a> has borders with the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Republic_of_the_Congo" title="Republic of the Congo">Republic of the Congo</a> and the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Democratic_Republic_of_the_Congo" title="Democratic Republic of the Congo">Democratic Republic of the Congo</a>. The capital and largest city is <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Luanda" title="Luanda">Luanda</a>, which is home to 2.8 million of Angola's 24.3 million people.</p>
<p>Although its territory has been inhabited since the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paleolithic_Era" title="Paleolithic Era" class="mw-redirect">Paleolithic Era</a>, modern Angola has its roots in Portuguese colonization, which began with mostly coastal settlements and trading posts established in the 16th century. In the 19th century, settlers slowly and hesitantly began to establish themselves in the interior. Angola as a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portuguese_West_Africa" title="Portuguese West Africa" class="mw-redirect">Portuguese colony</a> encompassing the present territory was not established until the early 20th century, after <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mbunda_people" title="Mbunda people">Mbunda</a> resistance ended following the abduction of their King, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mwene_Mbandu_Kapova_I_of_Mbunda" title="Mwene Mbandu Kapova I of Mbunda">Mwene Mbandu I Lyondthzi Kapova</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-SeeRene_5-0" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Angola#cite_note-SeeRene-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> Independence was achieved in 1975, after a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Angolan_War_of_Independence" title="Angolan War of Independence">protracted liberation war</a>. That same year, Angola descended into an intense <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Angolan_Civil_War" title="Angolan Civil War">civil war that lasted until 2002</a>.</p>
<p>Angola has vast mineral and petroleum reserves, and its economy has on average grown at a double-digit pace since the 1990s, especially following the end of the civil war. In spite of this, standards of living remain low for the majority of the population, and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Life_expectancy" title="Life expectancy">life expectancy</a> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Infant_mortality" title="Infant mortality">infant mortality rates</a> in Angola are among the worst in the world.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Angola#cite_note-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup> Angola is considered to be economically disparate, with the majority of the nation's <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wealth_concentration" title="Wealth concentration">wealth concentrated</a> in a disproportionately small sector of the population.</p>
<p>Angola is a member state of the United Nations, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/OPEC" title="OPEC">OPEC</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/African_Union" title="African Union">African Union</a>, the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Community_of_Portuguese_Language_Countries" title="Community of Portuguese Language Countries">Community of Portuguese Language Countries</a>, the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Latin_Union" title="Latin Union">Latin Union</a> and the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Southern_African_Development_Community" title="Southern African Development Community">Southern African Development Community</a>.</p>
