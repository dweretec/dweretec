<p>The <b>Republic of Kenya</b> is a country in <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/East_Africa" title="East Africa">East Africa</a>. Lying along the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indian_Ocean" title="Indian Ocean">Indian Ocean</a>, at the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Equator" title="Equator">equator</a>, Kenya is bordered by <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ethiopia" title="Ethiopia">Ethiopia</a> (north), <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Somalia" title="Somalia">Somalia</a> (northeast), <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tanzania" title="Tanzania">Tanzania</a> (south), <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Uganda" title="Uganda">Uganda</a> plus <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lake_Victoria" title="Lake Victoria">Lake Victoria</a> (west), and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sudan" title="Sudan">Sudan</a> (northwest). The capital city is <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nairobi" title="Nairobi">Nairobi</a>. Kenya spans an area about 85% the size of France or Texas. The population has grown rapidly in recent decades to nearly 38 million. Kenya has numerous wildlife reserves, containing thousands of animal species.</p>
<p>The country is named after <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mount_Kenya" title="Mount Kenya">Mount Kenya</a>, a significant landmark and the second among the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Highest_mountain_peaks_of_Africa" title="Highest mountain peaks of Africa">highest mountain peaks of Africa</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-times_kenya_colony_4-0" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kenya#cite_note-times_kenya_colony-4"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kenya#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup> and both were originally usually pronounced <span title="Pronunciation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:IPA_for_English" title="Wikipedia:IPA for English">/ˈkiːnjə/</a></span><sup id="cite_ref-oed_kenya_6-0" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kenya#cite_note-oed_kenya-6"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup> in English, though the native pronunciation and the one intended by the original transcription <i>Kenia</i> was <span title="Pronunciation in IPA" class="IPA"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:IPA" title="Wikipedia:IPA">[ˈkɛnja]</a></span>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kenya#cite_note-7"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup> During the presidency of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jomo_Kenyatta" title="Jomo Kenyatta">Jomo Kenyatta</a> in the 1960s, the current English pronunciation of <span title="Pronunciation in IPA" class="IPA"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:IPA_for_English" title="Wikipedia:IPA for English">/ˈkɛnjə/</a></span> became widespread because his name retained the native pronunciation.<sup id="cite_ref-foottit_8-0" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kenya#cite_note-foottit-8"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup> Before 1920, the area now known as Kenya was known as the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/British_East_Africa" title="British East Africa">British East Africa</a> Protectorate and so there was no need to mention <i>mount</i> when referring to the mountain.<sup id="cite_ref-times_kenya_colony_4-1" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kenya#cite_note-times_kenya_colony-4"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
