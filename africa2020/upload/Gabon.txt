<p><b>Gabon</b> (<span class="nowrap"><span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">/</a></span><span class="IPA nopopups"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English#Key" title="Help:IPA for English"><span title="'g' in 'guy'" style="border-bottom:1px dotted">ɡ</span></a></span><span class="IPA nopopups"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English#Key" title="Help:IPA for English"><span title="/ə/ 'a' in 'about'" style="border-bottom:1px dotted">ə</span></a></span><span class="IPA nopopups"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English#Key" title="Help:IPA for English"><span title="/ˈ/ primary stress follows" style="border-bottom:1px dotted">ˈ</span></a></span><span class="IPA nopopups"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English#Key" title="Help:IPA for English"><span title="'b' in 'buy'" style="border-bottom:1px dotted">b</span></a></span><span class="IPA nopopups"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English#Key" title="Help:IPA for English"><span title="/ɒ/ short 'o' in 'body'" style="border-bottom:1px dotted">ɒ</span></a></span><span class="IPA nopopups"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English#Key" title="Help:IPA for English"><span title="'n' in 'nigh'" style="border-bottom:1px dotted">n</span></a></span><span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">/</a></span></span>; <small>French pronunciation:&#160;​</small><span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Help:IPA_for_French" title="Help:IPA for French">[ɡabɔ̃]</a></span>), officially the <b>Gabonese Republic</b> (<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>: <span lang="fr"><i>République gabonaise</i></span>), is a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sovereign_state" title="Sovereign state">sovereign state</a> on the west coast of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Central_Africa" title="Central Africa">Central Africa</a>. Located on the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Equator" title="Equator">equator</a>, Gabon is bordered by <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Equatorial_Guinea" title="Equatorial Guinea">Equatorial Guinea</a> to the northwest, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cameroon" title="Cameroon">Cameroon</a> to the north, the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Republic_of_the_Congo" title="Republic of the Congo">Republic of the Congo</a> on the east and south, and the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gulf_of_Guinea" title="Gulf of Guinea">Gulf of Guinea</a> to the west. It has an area of nearly 270,000 square kilometres (100,000&#160;sq&#160;mi) and its population is estimated at 1.5 million people. Its capital and largest city is <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Libreville" title="Libreville">Libreville</a>.</p>
<p>Since its independence from France in 1960, Gabon has had <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_heads_of_state_of_Gabon" title="List of heads of state of Gabon">three presidents</a>. In the early 1990s, Gabon introduced a multi-party system and a new democratic constitution that allowed for a more transparent electoral process and reformed many governmental institutions. Gabon was also a non-permanent member of the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations_Security_Council" title="United Nations Security Council">United Nations Security Council</a> for the 2010–2011 term.</p>

(June 2012)">ambiguous</span></a></i>]</sup> have helped make Gabon one of the most prosperous countries in <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sub-Saharan_Africa" title="Sub-Saharan Africa">Sub-Saharan Africa</a>, with the 4th highest <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Human_Development_Index" title="Human Development Index">HDI</a><sup id="cite_ref-HDI_3-1" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gabon#cite_note-HDI-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> and the third highest GDP per capita (PPP) (after Equatorial Guinea and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Botswana" title="Botswana">Botswana</a>) in the region.</p>
