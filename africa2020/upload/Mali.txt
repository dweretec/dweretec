<p><b>Mali</b>, officially the <b>Republic of Mali</b> (<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>: <span lang="fr" xml:lang="fr"><i>République du Mali</i></span>), is a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Landlocked" title="Landlocked" class="mw-redirect">landlocked</a> nation in <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/West_Africa" title="West Africa">Western Africa</a>. Mali is the seventh largest country in Africa, bordering <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Algeria" title="Algeria">Algeria</a> on the north, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Niger" title="Niger">Niger</a> on the east, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Burkina_Faso" title="Burkina Faso">Burkina Faso</a> and the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/C%C3%B4te_d%27Ivoire" title="Côte d'Ivoire">Côte d'Ivoire</a> on the south, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guinea" title="Guinea">Guinea</a> on the south-west, and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Senegal" title="Senegal">Senegal</a> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mauritania" title="Mauritania">Mauritania</a> on the west. Its size is just over 1,240,000&#160;km² with an estimated population of about 13,000,000. Its capital is <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bamako" title="Bamako">Bamako</a>.</p>
<p>Mali consists of eight regions and its borders on the north reach deep into the middle of the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sahara" title="Sahara">Sahara</a>, while the country's southern region, where the majority of inhabitants live, features the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Niger_river" title="Niger river" class="mw-redirect">Niger</a> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Senegal_river" title="Senegal river" class="mw-redirect">Senegal</a> rivers. The country's economic structure centers around <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Agriculture" title="Agriculture">agriculture</a> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fishing" title="Fishing">fishing</a>. Some of Mali's natural resources include <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gold" title="Gold">gold</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Uranium" title="Uranium">uranium</a>, and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Salt" title="Salt">salt</a>. Mali is considered to be one of the poorest nations in the world.</p>
<p>Present-day Mali was once part of three West African empires that controlled <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trans-Saharan_trade" title="Trans-Saharan trade">trans-Saharan trade</a>: the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ghana_Empire" title="Ghana Empire">Ghana Empire</a>, the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mali_Empire" title="Mali Empire">Mali Empire</a> (from which Mali is named), and the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Songhai_Empire" title="Songhai Empire">Songhai Empire</a>. In the late 1800s, Mali fell under French control, becoming part of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/French_Sudan" title="French Sudan">French Sudan</a>. Mali gained independence in 1959 with Senegal, as the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mali_Federation" title="Mali Federation">Mali Federation</a>. A year later, the Mali Federation became the independent nation of Mali. After a long period of one-party rule, a 1991 coup led to the writing of a new constitution and the establishment of Mali as a democratic, multi-party state. About half the population live below the international <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Poverty_line" title="Poverty line" class="mw-redirect">poverty line</a> of US$1.25 a day.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mali#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
