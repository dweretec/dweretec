<span lang="fr"><i>Maurice</i></span>), officially the <b>Republic of Mauritius</b> (<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>: <span lang="fr"><i>République de Maurice</i></span>), is an <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Island_nation" title="Island nation" class="mw-redirect">island nation</a> in the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indian_Ocean" title="Indian Ocean">Indian Ocean</a> about 2,000 kilometres (1,200&#160;mi) off the southeast coast of the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/African_continent" title="African continent" class="mw-redirect">African continent</a>. The country includes the island of Mauritius, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rodrigues" title="Rodrigues">Rodrigues</a> (560 kilometres (350&#160;mi) east), the islands of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Agalega" title="Agalega" class="mw-redirect">Agalega</a>, and the archipelago of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/St_Brandon" title="St Brandon" class="mw-redirect">Saint Brandon</a>. The islands of Mauritius, Rodrigues, and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/R%C3%A9union" title="Réunion">Réunion</a> (170&#160;km (110&#160;mi) southwest) form part of the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mascarene_Islands" title="Mascarene Islands">Mascarene Islands</a>. The area of the country is 2,040&#160;km<sup>2</sup>. The capital and largest city is <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Port_Louis" title="Port Louis">Port Louis</a>.</p>
<p>Mauritius claims sovereignty over the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chagos_Archipelago" title="Chagos Archipelago">Chagos Archipelago</a> (United Kingdom) and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tromelin" title="Tromelin" class="mw-redirect">Tromelin</a> Island (France). The United Kingdom excised the Chagos Archipelago from Mauritian territory prior to Mauritian independence in 1965. The UK gradually <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Depopulation_of_Diego_Garcia" title="Depopulation of Diego Garcia" class="mw-redirect">depopulated the archipelago's indigenous population</a> and leased its biggest island, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diego_Garcia" title="Diego Garcia">Diego Garcia</a>, to the United States. The US soon thereafter established a military base on Diego Garcia.</p>
<p>The island of Mauritius was visited during the medieval period by the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arab_people" title="Arab people" class="mw-redirect">Arabs</a> and then by the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kingdom_of_Portugal" title="Kingdom of Portugal">Portuguese</a>, who named it <i>Dina Arobi</i> and <i>Cirne</i>, respectively. The island was uninhabited until the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dutch_Republic" title="Dutch Republic">Dutch Republic</a> established a colony in 1638, with the Dutch naming the island after <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maurice_of_Nassau,_Prince_of_Orange" title="Maurice of Nassau, Prince of Orange" class="mw-redirect">Prince Maurice van Nassau</a>. The Dutch colony was abandoned in 1710, and, five years later, the island became a French colony and was named <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Isle_de_France_(Mauritius)" title="Isle de France (Mauritius)">Isle de France</a>. Due to its strategic position, Mauritius was known as the "star and key" of the Indian Ocean.<sup id="cite_ref-nao_9-0" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mauritius#cite_note-nao-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Mauritius became an important base on the trade routes from Europe to the East before the opening of the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Suez_Canal" title="Suez Canal">Suez Canal</a> and was involved in the power struggle between the French and the British. The French won the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_Grand_Port" title="Battle of Grand Port">Battle of Grand Port</a>, their only naval victory over the British during these wars, but they <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Invasion_of_Isle_de_France" title="Invasion of Isle de France">could not prevent the British from landing</a> at Cap Malheureux three months later, and formally surrendered on the fifth day of the invasion, 3 December 1810, on terms allowing settlers to keep their land and property, the use of the French language, and the law of France in criminal and civil matters. Under British rule, the island's name reverted to Mauritius. The country became an independent state on 12 March 1968, following the adoption of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Constitution_of_Mauritius" title="Constitution of Mauritius">a new constitution</a> and became a republic in 1992 within the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Commonwealth_of_Nations" title="Commonwealth of Nations">Commonwealth</a>.</p>
<p>The <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mauritians" title="Mauritians">people of Mauritius</a> are <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Multiracial" title="Multiracial">multiethnic</a> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Multiculturalism" title="Multiculturalism">multicultural</a>. Most Mauritians are multilingual; <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mauritian_Creole" title="Mauritian Creole">Mauritian Creole</a>, English, French, and Asian languages are used. The island's government is closely modelled on the Westminster parliamentary system, and Mauritius is highly ranked for <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Democracy_Index" title="Democracy Index">democracy</a> and for <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_countries_by_economic_freedom" title="List of countries by economic freedom">economic</a> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Freedom_in_the_World" title="Freedom in the World">political freedom</a>. Along with the other Mascarene Islands, Mauritius is known for <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wildlife_of_Mauritius" title="Wildlife of Mauritius">its varied flora and fauna</a>, with many species <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Endemism" title="Endemism">endemic</a> to the island. The island is widely known as the only known home of the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dodo" title="Dodo">dodo</a>, which, along with several other avian species, was made extinct by human activities relatively shortly after the island's settlement.</p>
