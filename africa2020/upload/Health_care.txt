<p><b>Health care</b> (or <b>healthcare</b>) is the diagnosis, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Medical_case_management" title="Medical case management">treatment</a>, and prevention of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Disease" title="Disease">disease</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Illness" title="Illness" class="mw-redirect">illness</a>, injury, and other <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Disability" title="Disability">physical and mental impairments</a> in human beings. Health care is delivered by practitioners in <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Allied_health" title="Allied health" class="mw-redirect">allied health</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dentistry" title="Dentistry">dentistry</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Midwifery" title="Midwifery" class="mw-redirect">midwifery</a> (<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Obstetrics" title="Obstetrics">obstetrics</a>), <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Doctor_of_Medicine" title="Doctor of Medicine">medicine</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nursing" title="Nursing">nursing</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Optometry" title="Optometry">optometry</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pharmacy" title="Pharmacy">pharmacy</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Psychology" title="Psychology">psychology</a> and other <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Health_profession" title="Health profession" class="mw-redirect">health professions</a>. It refers to the work done in providing <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Primary_care" title="Primary care">primary care</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Secondary_care" title="Secondary care" class="mw-redirect">secondary care</a>, and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tertiary_care" title="Tertiary care" class="mw-redirect">tertiary care</a>, as well as in <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Public_health" title="Public health">public health</a>.</p>
<p>Access to health care varies across countries, groups, and individuals, largely influenced by social and economic conditions as well as the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Health_policy" title="Health policy">health policies</a> in place. Countries and jurisdictions have different policies and plans in relation to the personal and population-based health care goals within their societies. <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Health_care_system" title="Health care system" class="mw-redirect">Health care systems</a> are organizations established to meet the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Health" title="Health">health</a> needs of target populations. Their exact configuration varies between national and subnational entities. In some countries and jurisdictions, health care planning is distributed among market participants, whereas in others, planning occurs more centrally among governments or other coordinating bodies. In all cases, according to the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_Health_Organization" title="World Health Organization">World Health Organization</a> (WHO), a well-functioning health care system requires a robust financing mechanism; a well-trained and adequately-paid <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Health_Human_Resources" title="Health Human Resources" class="mw-redirect">workforce</a>; reliable information on which to base decisions and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Health_policy" title="Health policy">policies</a>; and well maintained <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Health_facilities" title="Health facilities" class="mw-redirect">health facilities</a> and logistics to deliver quality medicines and technologies.<sup id="cite_ref-WHO_1-0" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Health_care#cite_note-WHO-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Health care can contribute to a significant part of a country's <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Economic_system" title="Economic system">economy</a>. In 2011, the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Health_care_industry" title="Health care industry">health care industry</a> consumed an average of 9.3 percent of the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gross_domestic_product" title="Gross domestic product">GDP</a> or <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Us_dollar" title="Us dollar" class="mw-redirect">US$</a> 3,322 (<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Purchasing_power_parity" title="Purchasing power parity">PPP-adjusted</a>) per capita across the 34 members of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Organisation_for_Economic_Co-operation_and_Development" title="Organisation for Economic Co-operation and Development">OECD</a> countries. The USA (17.7%, or US$ PPP 8,508), the Netherlands (11.9%, 5,099), France (11.6%, 4,118), Germany (11.3%, 4,495), Canada (11.2%, 5669), and Switzerland (11%, 5,634) were the top spenders, however <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Life_expectancy" title="Life expectancy">life expectancy in total population at birth</a> was highest in Switzerland (82.8 years), Japan and Italy (82.7), Spain and Iceland (82.4), France (82.2) and Australia (82.0), while OECD's average exceeds 80 years for the first time ever in 2011: 80.1 years, a gain of 10 years since 1970. The USA (78.7 years) ranges only on place 26 among the 34 OECD member countries, but has the highest costs by far. All OECD countries have achieved universal (or almost universal) health coverage, except Mexico and the USA.<sup id="cite_ref-OECDHealthGlance_2-0" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Health_care#cite_note-OECDHealthGlance-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-OECDLifeExpectancy_3-0" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Health_care#cite_note-OECDLifeExpectancy-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> (see also <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Health_system#International_comparisons" title="Health system">international comparisons</a>.)</p>
<p>Health care is conventionally regarded as an important determinant in promoting the general physical and mental <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Health" title="Health">health</a> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Well-being" title="Well-being">well-being</a> of people around the world. An example of this was the worldwide eradication of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Smallpox" title="Smallpox">smallpox</a> in 1980, declared by the WHO as the first disease in human history to be completely eliminated by deliberate health care interventions.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Health_care#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
