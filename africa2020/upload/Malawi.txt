<p>The <b>Republic of Malawi</b> (pronounced <span title="Pronunciation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:IPA_for_English" title="Wikipedia:IPA for English">/məˈlɑːwi/</a></span>; <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chichewa_language" title="Chichewa language">Chichewa</a> <span title="Pronunciation in IPA" class="IPA"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:IPA" title="Wikipedia:IPA">[malaβi]</a></span><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" title="" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Cleanup" title="Wikipedia:Cleanup">need tone</a></i>]</sup>) is a landlocked country in southeast <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a> that was formerly known as <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nyasaland" title="Nyasaland">Nyasaland</a>. It is bordered by <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zambia" title="Zambia">Zambia</a> to the northwest, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tanzania" title="Tanzania">Tanzania</a> to the northeast, and it borders <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mozambique" title="Mozambique">Mozambique</a> on the east, south and west. The country is separated from Tanzania and Mozambique by <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lake_Malawi" title="Lake Malawi">Lake Malawi</a>. Its size is over 118,000&#160;km² with an estimated population of more than	13,900,000. Its capital is <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lilongwe" title="Lilongwe">Lilongwe</a>, the biggest city is <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Blantyre" title="Blantyre">Blantyre</a>. The name Malawi comes from the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maravi" title="Maravi">Maravi</a>, an old name of the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nyanja_people" title="Nyanja people" class="mw-redirect">Nyanja people</a> that inhabit the area.</p>
<p>Malawi was first <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Settler" title="Settler">settled</a> during the 10th century and remained under native rule until 1891 when it was <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Colonized" title="Colonized" class="mw-redirect">colonized</a> by the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/British_people" title="British people">British</a>, who ruled the country until 1964. Upon gaining <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Independence" title="Independence">independence</a> it became a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Single-party_state" title="Single-party state">single-party state</a> under the presidency of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hastings_Banda" title="Hastings Banda">Hastings Banda</a>, who remained president until 1994, when he was ousted from power. <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bingu_Mutharika" title="Bingu Mutharika" class="mw-redirect">Bingu Mutharika</a>, elected in 2004, is the current president. Malawi has a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Democratic" title="Democratic" class="mw-redirect">democratic</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Multi-party" title="Multi-party" class="mw-redirect">multi-party</a> <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Government" title="Government">government</a>. Malawi has a small <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Military" title="Military">military</a> force that includes an <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Army" title="Army">army</a>, a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Navy" title="Navy">navy</a> and an <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Air_wing" title="Air wing" class="mw-redirect">air wing</a>. Malawi's <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Foreign_policy" title="Foreign policy">foreign policy</a> is <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pro-Western" title="Pro-Western" class="mw-redirect">pro-Western</a> and includes positive <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diplomatic" title="Diplomatic" class="mw-redirect">diplomatic</a> relations with most countries and participation in several <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_organization" title="International organization">international organizations</a>.</p>
<p>Malawi is among the world's least developed and most <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_countries_and_dependencies_by_population_density" title="List of countries and dependencies by population density">densely populated countries</a>. The economy is heavily based in agriculture, with a largely <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rural" title="Rural" class="mw-redirect">rural</a> population. The Malawian government depends heavily on outside aid to meet <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Economic_development" title="Economic development">development</a> needs, although this need (and the aid offered) has decreased since 2000. The Malawian government faces challenges in growing the economy, improving education, health care and the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Environmental_protection" title="Environmental protection">environmental protection</a> and becoming financially independent. Malawi has several programs developed since 2005 that focus on these issues, and the country's outlook appears to be improving, with improvements in economic growth, education and healthcare seen in 2007 and 2008.</p>
<p>Malawi has a low <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Life_expectancy" title="Life expectancy">life expectancy</a> and high <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Infant_mortality" title="Infant mortality">infant mortality</a>. There is a high prevalence of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/HIV/AIDS" title="HIV/AIDS" class="mw-redirect">HIV/AIDS</a>, which is a drain on the labor force and government expenditures, and is expected to have a significant impact on <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gross_domestic_product" title="Gross domestic product">gross domestic product</a> (GDP) by 2010. There is a diverse population of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Native_people" title="Native people" class="mw-redirect">native peoples</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Asian_people" title="Asian people">Asians</a> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/European_ethnic_groups" title="European ethnic groups" class="mw-redirect">Europeans</a>, with several languages spoken and an array of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Religious" title="Religious" class="mw-redirect">religious</a> beliefs. Although there was tribal conflict in the past, by 2008 it had diminished considerably and the concept of a Malawian nationality had begun to form. Malawi has a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Culture" title="Culture">culture</a> combining native and colonial aspects, including sports, art, dance and music.</p>
