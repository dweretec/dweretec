It is bordered by <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Libya" title="Libya">Libya</a> to the north, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sudan" title="Sudan">Sudan</a> to the east, the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Central_African_Republic" title="Central African Republic">Central African Republic</a> to the south, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cameroon" title="Cameroon">Cameroon</a> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nigeria" title="Nigeria">Nigeria</a> to the southwest and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Niger" title="Niger">Niger</a> to the west. It is the fifth largest country in Africa in terms of area.</p>
<p>Chad is divided into multiple regions: a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Desert" title="Desert">desert</a> zone in the north, an arid <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sahel" title="Sahel">Sahelian</a> belt in the centre and a more fertile <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sudan_(region)" title="Sudan (region)">Sudanese</a> <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Savanna" title="Savanna">savanna</a> zone in the south. <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lake_Chad" title="Lake Chad">Lake Chad</a>, after which the country is named, is the largest <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wetland" title="Wetland">wetland</a> in Chad and the second-largest in Africa. <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/N%27Djamena" title="N'Djamena">N'Djamena</a>, the capital, is the largest city. Chad is home to over 200 different <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_ethnic_groups_in_Chad" title="List of ethnic groups in Chad">ethnic</a> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Languages_of_Chad" title="Languages of Chad">linguistic groups</a>. <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arabic_language" title="Arabic language">Arabic</a> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a> are the official languages. <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Islam" title="Islam">Islam</a> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christianity" title="Christianity">Christianity</a> are the most widely practiced religions.</p>
<p>Beginning in the 7th millennium BC, human populations moved into the Chadian basin in great numbers. By the end of the 1st millennium BC, a series of states and empires rose and fell in Chad's Sahelian strip, each focused on controlling the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trans-Saharan_trade" title="Trans-Saharan trade">trans-Saharan trade</a> routes that passed through the region. France conquered the territory by 1920 and incorporated it as part of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/French_Equatorial_Africa" title="French Equatorial Africa">French Equatorial Africa</a>. In 1960, Chad obtained independence under the leadership of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fran%C3%A7ois_Tombalbaye" title="François Tombalbaye">François Tombalbaye</a>. Resentment towards his policies in the Muslim north culminated in the eruption of a long-lasting <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Tombalbaye_Regime#Rebellion_in_Chad" title="The Tombalbaye Regime" class="mw-redirect">civil war</a> in 1965. In 1979, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Transitional_Government_of_National_Unity" title="Transitional Government of National Unity">the rebels</a> conquered the capital and put an end to the south's hegemony. However, the rebel commanders fought amongst themselves until <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hiss%C3%A8ne_Habr%C3%A9" title="Hissène Habré">Hissène Habré</a> defeated his rivals. He was overthrown in 1990 by his general <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Idriss_D%C3%A9by" title="Idriss Déby">Idriss Déby</a>. Since 2003, the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Darfur_conflict" title="Darfur conflict" class="mw-redirect">Darfur crisis</a> in Sudan has spilt over the border and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/War_in_Chad_(2005%E2%80%93present)" title="War in Chad (2005–present)" class="mw-redirect">destabilised the nation</a>, with hundreds of thousands of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sudanese_refugees" title="Sudanese refugees" class="mw-redirect">Sudanese refugees</a> living in and around camps in eastern Chad.</p>
<p>While many political parties are active, power lies firmly in the hands of President Déby and his political party, the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Patriotic_Salvation_Movement" title="Patriotic Salvation Movement">Patriotic Salvation Movement</a>. Chad remains plagued by political violence and recurrent attempted <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coup_d%27%C3%A9tat" title="Coup d'état">coups d'état</a>. Chad is one of the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_countries_by_GDP_(nominal)_per_capita" title="List of countries by GDP (nominal) per capita">poorest</a> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Corruption_Perceptions_Index" title="Corruption Perceptions Index">most corrupt</a> countries in the world; most inhabitants live in poverty as <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Agriculture_in_Chad" title="Agriculture in Chad">subsistence herders and farmers</a>. Since 2003, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Petroleum" title="Petroleum">crude oil</a> has become the country's primary source of export earnings, superseding the traditional <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cotton" title="Cotton">cotton</a> industry.</p>
