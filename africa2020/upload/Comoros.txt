<p>The <b>Comoros</b> (pronounced <span title="Pronunciation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:IPA_for_English" title="Wikipedia:IPA for English">/kɒˈməroʊz/</a></span><small>&#160;(<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Media:En-us-Comoros.ogg" title="Media:En-us-Comoros.ogg"><img alt="Speaker Icon.svg" src="http://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Speaker_Icon.svg/13px-Speaker_Icon.svg.png" width="13" height="13" /></a> <a href="http://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/3/39/En-us-Comoros.ogg" class="internal" title="En-us-Comoros.ogg">listen</a>)</small>; <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arabic_language" title="Arabic language">Arabic</a>: <span lang="ar" xml:lang="ar"><big>جزر القمر</big></span>‎, <i>Juzur al-Qumur</i>), officially the <b>Union of the Comoros</b> (<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>: <span lang="fr" xml:lang="fr"><i>Union des Comores</i></span>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arabic_language" title="Arabic language">Arabic</a>: <span lang="ar" xml:lang="ar"><big>الاتّحاد القمريّ</big></span>‎, <i>al-Itti<span class="Unicode">ḥ</span>ād al-Qumuriyy</i>) is an island nation in the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indian_Ocean" title="Indian Ocean">Indian Ocean</a>, located off the eastern coast of Africa, on the northern end of the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mozambique_Channel" title="Mozambique Channel">Mozambique Channel</a>, between northern <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Madagascar" title="Madagascar">Madagascar</a> and northeastern <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mozambique" title="Mozambique">Mozambique</a>. The nearest countries to the Comoros are <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mozambique" title="Mozambique">Mozambique</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tanzania" title="Tanzania">Tanzania</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Madagascar" title="Madagascar">Madagascar</a>, and the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Seychelles" title="Seychelles">Seychelles</a>. It is the southernmost member state of the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arab_League" title="Arab League">Arab League</a>. At 1,862&#160;km<sup>2</sup> (719&#160;sq&#160;mi)<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Comoros#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup>, (excluding Mayotte) the Comoros is the third-smallest African nation by area. And with a population estimated at 798,000, it is the sixth-smallest African nation by population—although it has one of the highest population densities in Africa. Its name derives from the Arabic word <span lang="ar-Latn" xml:lang="ar-Latn"><i>qamar</i></span> ("moon").<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Comoros#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The country officially consists of the four islands in the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Volcanic_island" title="Volcanic island" class="mw-redirect">volcanic</a> <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Comoros_archipelago" title="Comoros archipelago" class="mw-redirect">Comoros archipelago</a>: <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ngazidja" title="Ngazidja" class="mw-redirect">Ngazidja</a> (<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>: <span lang="fr" xml:lang="fr"><i><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Grande_Comore" title="Grande Comore">Grande Comore</a></i></span>), <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mwali" title="Mwali" class="mw-redirect">Mwali</a> (<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>: <span lang="fr" xml:lang="fr"><i><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Moh%C3%A9li" title="Mohéli">Mohéli</a></i></span>), <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nzwani" title="Nzwani" class="mw-redirect">Nzwani</a> (<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>: <span lang="fr" xml:lang="fr"><i><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anjouan" title="Anjouan">Anjouan</a></i></span>), and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mahor%C3%A9" title="Mahoré" class="mw-redirect">Mahoré</a> (<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>: <span lang="fr" xml:lang="fr"><i><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mayotte" title="Mayotte">Mayotte</a></i></span>), as well as many smaller islands.<sup id="cite_ref-GA31_first-resolution_4-0" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Comoros#cite_note-GA31_first-resolution-4"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Comoros#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup> However, the government of the Union of the Comoros (or its predecessors, since independence) has never administered the island of Mayotte, which France considers an <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Overseas_collectivity" title="Overseas collectivity">overseas community</a> and still administers. Mayotte was the only island in the archipelago that voted against independence from France, and France has vetoed United Nations Security Council resolutions that would affirm Comorian sovereignty over the island.<sup id="cite_ref-GlobalPolicy_SC-Vetoes_6-0" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Comoros#cite_note-GlobalPolicy_SC-Vetoes-6"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-article33_7-0" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Comoros#cite_note-article33-7"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup> In addition, a 29 March 2009 referendum on Mayotte's becoming an <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Overseas_department" title="Overseas department">overseas department</a> of France in 2011 was passed overwhelmingly by the people of Mayotte.</p>
<p>The archipelago is notable for its diverse culture and history, as a nation formed at the crossroads of many civilizations. Though in the contested island of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mayotte" title="Mayotte">Mayotte</a> the sole official language is French, the "Union of the Comoros" has three official languages: <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Comorian_language" title="Comorian language">Comorian</a> (<i>Shikomor</i>), <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arabic" title="Arabic" class="mw-redirect">Arabic</a> and French.</p>
<p>The "Union of the Comoros" is the only state to be a member of each of the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/African_Union" title="African Union">African Union</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Francophonie" title="Francophonie" class="mw-redirect">Francophonie</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Organisation_of_the_Islamic_Conference" title="Organisation of the Islamic Conference">Organisation of the Islamic Conference</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arab_League" title="Arab League">Arab League</a>, and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indian_Ocean_Commission" title="Indian Ocean Commission">Indian Ocean Commission</a>. However, it has had a troubled history since independence in 1975, marked by numerous <i><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coups_d%27%C3%A9tat" title="Coups d'état" class="mw-redirect">coups d'état</a></i>.<br />
About half the population live below the international poverty line of US$1.25 a day.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Comoros#cite_note-8"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
