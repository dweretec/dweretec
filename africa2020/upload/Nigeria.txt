class="mw-redirect">constitutional republic</a> comprising <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/States_of_Nigeria" title="States of Nigeria">36 states</a> and its <a title="Abuja">Abuja</a>. Nigeria is located in <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/West_Africa" title="West Africa">West Africa</a> and shares land borders with the Republic of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Benin" title="Benin">Benin</a> in the west, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chad" title="Chad">Chad</a> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cameroon" title="Cameroon">Cameroon</a> in the east, and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Niger" title="Niger">Niger</a> in the north. Its coast in the south lies on the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gulf_of_Guinea" title="Gulf of Guinea">Gulf of Guinea</a> in the Atlantic Ocean.</p>
<p>Present-day Nigeria has been the site of numerous kingdoms and tribal states for millennia. The modern state originated from British colonial rule beginning in the 19th century, and the merging of the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Southern_Nigeria_Protectorate" title="Southern Nigeria Protectorate">Southern Nigeria Protectorate</a> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Northern_Nigeria_Protectorate" title="Northern Nigeria Protectorate">Northern Nigeria Protectorate</a> in 1914. The British set up administrative and legal structures whilst practicing <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indirect_rule" title="Indirect rule">indirect rule</a> through traditional <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chiefdoms" title="Chiefdoms" class="mw-redirect">chiefdoms</a>. Nigeria became a formally independent federation in 1960, and plunged into a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nigerian_Civil_War" title="Nigerian Civil War">civil war</a> from 1967–1970. It has since alternated between democratically-elected civilian governments and military dictatorships, with its 2011 presidential elections being viewed as the first to be conducted reasonably freely and fairly.<sup id="cite_ref-New_York_7-0" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nigeria#cite_note-New_York-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Nigeria is often referred to as the "Giant of Africa", owing to its large population and economy.<sup id="cite_ref-giant_8-0" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nigeria#cite_note-giant-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup> With approximately 174 million inhabitants, Nigeria is the most populous country in <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a> and the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_countries_by_population" title="List of countries by population" class="mw-redirect">seventh most populous country in the world</a>. Nigeria has one of the largest <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Youth_in_Nigeria" title="Youth in Nigeria">populations of youth</a> in the world.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nigeria#cite_note-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Profile_10-0" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nigeria#cite_note-Profile-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup> The country is viewed as a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Multinational_state" title="Multinational state">multinational state</a>, as it is inhabited by over 500 ethnic groups, of which the three largest are the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hausa_people" title="Hausa people">Hausa</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Igbo_people" title="Igbo people">Igbo</a> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yoruba_people" title="Yoruba people">Yoruba</a>; these ethnic groups speak over 500 different languages, and are identified with wide variety of cultures.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nigeria#cite_note-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nigeria#cite_note-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup> Regarding religion, Nigeria is divided roughly in half between <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christianity" title="Christianity">Christians</a>, who live mostly in the southern and central parts of the country, and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Islam" title="Islam">Muslims</a>, concentrated mostly in the northern and southwestern regions. A minority of the population practice religions indigenous to Nigeria, such as those native to <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Odinani" title="Odinani">Igbo</a> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yoruba_religion" title="Yoruba religion">Yoruba</a> peoples.</p>
<p>In 2014, Nigeria's economy (GDP) became the largest in Africa, worth more than $500 billion and $1 trillion in terms of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nominal_GDP" title="Nominal GDP" class="mw-redirect">nominal GDP</a> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Purchasing_power_parity" title="Purchasing power parity">purchasing power parity</a> respectively. It overtook <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">South Africa</a> to become the world's 21st (<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_countries_by_GDP_(nominal)" title="List of countries by GDP (nominal)">nominal</a>) and 20th (<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_countries_by_GDP_(PPP)" title="List of countries by GDP (PPP)">PPP</a>) largest economy in 2015;<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nigeria#cite_note-13"><span>[</span>13<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nigeria#cite_note-14"><span>[</span>14<span>]</span></a></sup> furthermore, the debt-to-GDP ratio is only 11 percent (8 percent below the 2012 ratio).<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nigeria#cite_note-15"><span>[</span>15<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-NigeriaTradesmark_16-0" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nigeria#cite_note-NigeriaTradesmark-16"><span>[</span>16<span>]</span></a></sup> The country's oil reserves have played a major role in its growing wealth and influence. Nigeria is considered to be an <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Emerging_markets" title="Emerging markets">emerging market</a> by the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_Bank" title="World Bank">World Bank</a><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nigeria#cite_note-17"><span>[</span>17<span>]</span></a></sup> and has been identified as a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Regional_power" title="Regional power">regional power</a> on the African continent.<sup id="cite_ref-NigeriaTradesmark_16-1" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nigeria#cite_note-NigeriaTradesmark-16"><span>[</span>16<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nigeria#cite_note-18"><span>[</span>18<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nigeria#cite_note-19"><span>[</span>19<span>]</span></a></sup> Nigeria is considered to be a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Middle_power" title="Middle power">middle power</a> in the world,<sup id="cite_ref-Cooper_et_al_20-0" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nigeria#cite_note-Cooper_et_al-20"><span>[</span>20<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nigeria#cite_note-21"><span>[</span>21<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Mace-Belanger_22-0" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nigeria#cite_note-Mace-Belanger-22"><span>[</span>22<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Solomon_23-0" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nigeria#cite_note-Solomon-23"><span>[</span>23<span>]</span></a></sup> and has also been identified as an <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Emerging_power" title="Emerging power">emerging power</a>;<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nigeria#cite_note-24"><span>[</span>24<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nigeria#cite_note-25"><span>[</span>25<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nigeria#cite_note-26"><span>[</span>26<span>]</span></a></sup> it is a member of the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/MINT_(economics)" title="MINT (economics)">MINT</a> group of countries, which are widely seen as the globe's next "<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/BRIC" title="BRIC">BRIC</a>-like" economies. It is also listed among the "<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Next_Eleven" title="Next Eleven">Next Eleven</a>" economies set to become among the biggest in the world. Nigeria is a member of the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Commonwealth_of_Nations" title="Commonwealth of Nations">Commonwealth of Nations</a>, the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/African_Union" title="African Union">African Union</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/OPEC" title="OPEC">OPEC</a>, and the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations</a> among other international organizations.</p>
<p>Since 2002, the North East of the country has seen sectarian violence by <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Boko_Haram" title="Boko Haram">Boko Haram</a>, an Islamist movement that seeks to abolish the secular system of government and establish <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sharia_law" title="Sharia law" class="mw-redirect">Sharia law</a> in the country.<sup id="cite_ref-AJAN_27-0" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nigeria#cite_note-AJAN-27"><span>[</span>27<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Olugbode_28-0" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nigeria#cite_note-Olugbode-28"><span>[</span>28<span>]</span></a></sup> Nigerian President <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Goodluck_Jonathan" title="Goodluck Jonathan">Goodluck Jonathan</a> in May 2014 claimed that <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Boko_Haram" title="Boko Haram">Boko Haram</a> attacks have left at least 12,000 people dead and 8,000 people crippled.<sup id="cite_ref-May_17.2C_2014_29-0" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nigeria#cite_note-May_17.2C_2014-29"><span>[</span>29<span>]</span></a></sup> At the same time, neighboring countries, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Benin" title="Benin">Benin</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chad" title="Chad">Chad</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cameroon" title="Cameroon">Cameroon</a> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Niger" title="Niger">Niger</a> joined Nigeria in a united effort to combat Boko Haram in the aftermath of a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2014_Chibok_kidnapping" title="2014 Chibok kidnapping" class="mw-redirect">world media highlighted kidnapping of 276 schoolgirls</a> and the spread of Boko Haram attacks to these countries.<sup id="cite_ref-AfricaUnited_30-0" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nigeria#cite_note-AfricaUnited-30"><span>[</span>30<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
