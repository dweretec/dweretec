<p><b>Madagascar</b>, officially the <b>Republic of Madagascar</b> (<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Malagasy_language" title="Malagasy language">Malagasy</a>: <span lang="mg"><i>Repoblikan'i Madagasikara</i></span> <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Malagasy" title="Help:IPA for Malagasy">[republiˈkʲan madaɡasˈkʲarə̥]</a></span>; <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>: <span lang="fr"><i>République de Madagascar</i></span>) and previously known as the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Malagasy_Republic" title="Malagasy Republic">Malagasy Republic</a>, is an <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Island_country" title="Island country">island country</a> in the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indian_Ocean" title="Indian Ocean">Indian Ocean</a>, off the coast of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Southeast_Africa" title="Southeast Africa" class="mw-redirect">Southeast Africa</a>. The nation comprises the island of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Madagascar_(island)" title="Madagascar (island)" class="mw-redirect">Madagascar</a> (the fourth-<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_islands_by_area" title="List of islands by area">largest island</a> in the world), as well as numerous smaller peripheral islands. Following the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prehistoric" title="Prehistoric" class="mw-redirect">prehistoric</a> breakup of the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Supercontinent" title="Supercontinent">supercontinent</a> <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gondwana" title="Gondwana">Gondwana</a>, Madagascar split from <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India" title="India">India</a> around 88&#160;million years ago, allowing native plants and animals to evolve in relative isolation. Consequently, Madagascar is a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Biodiversity_hotspot" title="Biodiversity hotspot">biodiversity hotspot</a>; over 90&#160;percent of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wildlife_of_Madagascar" title="Wildlife of Madagascar">its wildlife</a> is found nowhere else on Earth. The island's diverse ecosystems and unique wildlife are threatened by the encroachment of the rapidly growing human population and other environmental threats.</p>
<p>Initial human settlement of Madagascar occurred between 350 BC and AD 550 by <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Austronesian_peoples" title="Austronesian peoples">Austronesian peoples</a> arriving on <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Outrigger_canoe" title="Outrigger canoe">outrigger canoes</a> from <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Borneo" title="Borneo">Borneo</a>. These were joined around AD 1000 by <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bantu_peoples" title="Bantu peoples">Bantu</a> migrants crossing the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mozambique_Channel" title="Mozambique Channel">Mozambique Channel</a> from East Africa. Other groups continued to settle on Madagascar over time, each one making lasting contributions to Malagasy cultural life. The <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Malagasy_people" title="Malagasy people">Malagasy ethnic group</a> is often divided into eighteen or more <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ethnic_groups_of_Madagascar" title="Ethnic groups of Madagascar" class="mw-redirect">sub-groups</a> of which the largest are the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Merina_people" title="Merina people">Merina</a> of the central highlands.</p>
<p>Until the late 18th century, the island of Madagascar was ruled by a fragmented assortment of shifting socio-political alliances. Beginning in the early 19th century, most of the island was united and ruled as the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Merina_Kingdom" title="Merina Kingdom">Kingdom of Madagascar</a> by a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_Malagasy_monarchs" title="List of Malagasy monarchs" class="mw-redirect">series</a> of Merina <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Andriana" title="Andriana">nobles</a>. The monarchy collapsed in 1897 when the island was absorbed into the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/French_colonial_empire" title="French colonial empire">French colonial empire</a>, from which the island gained independence in 1960. The autonomous state of Madagascar has since undergone four major constitutional periods, termed Republics. Since 1992 the nation has officially been governed as a constitutional democracy from its capital at <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Antananarivo" title="Antananarivo">Antananarivo</a>. However, in a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2009_Malagasy_political_crisis" title="2009 Malagasy political crisis">popular uprising in 2009</a> president <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marc_Ravalomanana" title="Marc Ravalomanana">Marc Ravalomanana</a> was made to resign and presidential power was transferred in March 2009 to <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Andry_Rajoelina" title="Andry Rajoelina">Andry Rajoelina</a> in a move widely viewed by the international community as a <i><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coup_d%27%C3%A9tat" title="Coup d'état">coup d'état</a></i>. Constitutional governance was restored in January 2014 when <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hery_Rajaonarimampianina" title="Hery Rajaonarimampianina">Hery Rajaonarimampianina</a> was named president following a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Malagasy_general_election,_2013" title="Malagasy general election, 2013">2013 election</a> deemed fair and transparent by the international community.</p>
<p>In 2012, the population of Madagascar was estimated at just over 22&#160;million, 90&#160;percent of whom live on less than two dollars per day. <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Malagasy_language" title="Malagasy language">Malagasy</a> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a> are both <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Official_language" title="Official language">official languages</a> of the state. The majority of the population adheres to <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Malagasy_mythology" title="Malagasy mythology">traditional beliefs</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christianity_in_Madagascar" title="Christianity in Madagascar">Christianity</a>, or an amalgamation of both. Ecotourism and agriculture, paired with greater investments in education, health and private enterprise, are key elements of Madagascar's development strategy. Under Ravalomanana these investments produced substantial economic growth but the benefits were not evenly spread throughout the population, producing tensions over the increasing cost of living and declining living standards among the poor and some segments of the middle class. As of 2014, the economy has been weakened by the recently concluded political crisis and quality of life remains low for the majority of the Malagasy population.</p>
