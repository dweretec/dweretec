<p><b>Western Sahara</b> (<span class="unicode" style="white-space:nowrap;"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Assahhraalgharbiyya.ogg" title="assahhraalgharbiyya.ogg"><img alt="assahhraalgharbiyya.ogg" src="http://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/11px-Loudspeaker.svg.png" width="11" height="11" /></a> <a href="http://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/b/b6/Assahhraalgharbiyya.ogg" class="internal" title="Assahhraalgharbiyya.ogg">Arabic: <big><b>الصحراء الغربية</b></big></a> <small class="metadata audiolinkinfo" style="cursor:help;">(<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Media_help" title="Wikipedia:Media help"><span style="cursor:help;">help</span></a>·<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Assahhraalgharbiyya.ogg" title="File:Assahhraalgharbiyya.ogg"><span style="cursor:help;">info</span></a>)</small></span>; <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arabic_transliteration" title="Arabic transliteration" class="mw-redirect">transliterated</a>: <b><span class="Unicode">as-Ṣaḥrā' al-Gharbīyah</span></b>; <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>: <span lang="es" xml:lang="es"><i>Sahara Occidental</i></span>) is a territory of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/North_Africa" title="North Africa">North Africa</a>, bordered by <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Morocco" title="Morocco">Morocco</a> to the north, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Algeria" title="Algeria">Algeria</a> to the northeast, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mauritania" title="Mauritania">Mauritania</a> to the east and south, and the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic Ocean</a> to the west. Its surface area amounts to 266,000&#160;square kilometres (103,000&#160;sq&#160;mi). It is one of the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_countries_and_dependencies_by_population_density" title="List of countries and dependencies by population density">most sparsely populated territories in the world</a>, mainly consisting of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Desert" title="Desert">desert</a> flatlands. The largest city is <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/El_Aai%C3%BAn" title="El Aaiún">El Aaiún</a> (Laâyoune), which is home to over half of the population of the territory, in total estimated at just more than 500,000.<sup id="cite_ref-unpop_0-1" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Western_Sahara#cite_note-unpop-0"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Western Sahara has been on the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations_list_of_Non-Self-Governing_Territories" title="United Nations list of Non-Self-Governing Territories">United Nations list of non-self-governing territories</a> since the 1960s when it was a Spanish colony.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Western_Sahara#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> The Kingdom of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Morocco" title="Morocco">Morocco</a> and the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polisario_Front" title="Polisario Front">Polisario Front</a> independence movement, with its <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sahrawi_Arab_Democratic_Republic" title="Sahrawi Arab Democratic Republic">Sahrawi Arab Democratic Republic</a> (SADR) government, dispute control of the territory.</p>
<p>Since a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations</a>-sponsored ceasefire agreement in 1991, most of the territory has been controlled by Morocco, and the remainder by the Polisario/SADR, backed by <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Algeria" title="Algeria">Algeria</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Western_Sahara#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> Internationally, major powers such as the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> have taken a generally ambiguous and neutral position on each side's claims, and have pressed both parties to agree on a peaceful resolution. Both Morocco and Polisario have sought to boost their claims by accumulating formal recognition, essentially from African, Asian, and Latin American states in the developing world. Polisario has won formal recognition for SADR from <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Legal_status_of_Western_Sahara#States_recognizing_the_SADR" title="Legal status of Western Sahara">46 states</a>, and was extended membership in the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/African_Union" title="African Union">African Union</a>, while Morocco has won recognition for its position from the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arab_League" title="Arab League">Arab League</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Western_Sahara#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Western_Sahara#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> In both instances, recognitions have over the past two decades been extended and withdrawn according to changing international trends.</p>
