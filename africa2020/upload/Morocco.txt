text-decoration: none">al-Maġrib</span></i>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Berber_languages" title="Berber languages">Berber</a>: <span lang="ber">ⵍⵎⴰⵖⵔⵉⴱ</span><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Morocco#cite_note-9"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup> <span title="Berber transliteration" class="Unicode" style="white-space:normal; text-decoration: none"><i>Lmaġrib</i></span>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>: <span lang="fr"><i>Maroc</i></span><sup id="cite_ref-French_2-1" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Morocco#cite_note-French-2"><span>[</span>Notes 1<span>]</span></a></sup>), officially the <b>Kingdom of Morocco</b>,<sup id="cite_ref-CIA_3-1" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Morocco#cite_note-CIA-3"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> is a country in the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maghreb" title="Maghreb">Maghreb</a> region of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/North_Africa" title="North Africa">North Africa</a>. Geographically, Morocco is characterized by a rugged mountainous interior and large portions of desert. It is one of only three countries (with Spain and France) to have both Atlantic and Mediterranean coastlines. The Arabic name <i>al-Mamlakah al-Maghribiyah</i> (<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arabic_language" title="Arabic language">Arabic</a>: <span lang="ar" dir="rtl"><span style="font-size:large;">المملكة المغربية</span></span>‎, meaning "The Western Kingdom") and <i>Al-<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maghreb" title="Maghreb">Maghrib</a></i> (<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arabic_language" title="Arabic language">Arabic</a>: <span lang="ar" dir="rtl"><span style="font-size:large;">المغرب</span></span>‎, meaning "The West") are commonly used as alternate names.</p>
<p>Morocco has a population of over 33 million and an area of 446,550&#160;km<sup>2</sup> (172,410&#160;sq&#160;mi). Its political capital is <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rabat" title="Rabat">Rabat</a>, although the largest city is <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Casablanca" title="Casablanca">Casablanca</a>; other major cities include <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marrakesh" title="Marrakesh">Marrakesh</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tangier" title="Tangier">Tangier</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tetouan" title="Tetouan" class="mw-redirect">Tetouan</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sal%C3%A9" title="Salé">Salé</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fes" title="Fes">Fes</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Agadir" title="Agadir">Agadir</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Meknes" title="Meknes">Meknes</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oujda" title="Oujda">Oujda</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kenitra" title="Kenitra">Kenitra</a>, and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nador" title="Nador">Nador</a>. A historically prominent regional power, Morocco has a history of independence not shared by its neighbours. Its distinct culture is a blend of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arab_people" title="Arab people" class="mw-redirect">Arab</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Berber_people" title="Berber people" class="mw-redirect">indigenous Berber</a>, Sub-Saharan African, and European influences.</p>
<p>Morocco claims the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations_list_of_Non-Self-Governing_Territories" title="United Nations list of Non-Self-Governing Territories">non-self-governing territory</a> of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Western_Sahara" title="Western Sahara">Western Sahara</a> as its <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Southern_Provinces" title="Southern Provinces">Southern Provinces</a>. Morocco annexed the territory in 1975, leading to a guerrilla war with indigenous forces until a cease-fire in 1991. <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Western_Sahara_peace_process" title="Western Sahara peace process">Peace processes</a> have thus far failed to break the political deadlock.</p>
<p>Morocco is a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Constitutional_monarchy" title="Constitutional monarchy">constitutional monarchy</a> with an elected parliament. The <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_rulers_of_Morocco" title="List of rulers of Morocco">King of Morocco</a> holds vast executive and legislative powers, especially over the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Military_of_Morocco" title="Military of Morocco" class="mw-redirect">military</a>, foreign policy and religious affairs. <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Executive_power" title="Executive power" class="mw-redirect">Executive power</a> is exercised by the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Moroccan_government" title="Moroccan government" class="mw-redirect">government</a>, while <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Legislature" title="Legislature">legislative power</a> is vested in both the government and the two chambers of parliament, the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Assembly_of_Representatives_of_Morocco" title="Assembly of Representatives of Morocco" class="mw-redirect">Assembly of Representatives</a> and the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Assembly_of_Councillors" title="Assembly of Councillors" class="mw-redirect">Assembly of Councillors</a>. The king can issue decrees called <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Moroccan_Dahir" title="Moroccan Dahir">dahirs</a> which have the force of law. He can also dissolve the parliament after consulting the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_heads_of_government_of_Morocco" title="List of heads of government of Morocco">Prime Minister</a> and the president of the Constitutional court.</p>
<p>Morocco's predominant religion is Islam, while the official languages are <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Berber_languages" title="Berber languages">Berber</a> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arabic_language" title="Arabic language">Arabic</a>. <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Moroccan_Arabic" title="Moroccan Arabic">Moroccan Arabic</a>, referred to as <i>Darija</i>, and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a> are also widely spoken.</p>
