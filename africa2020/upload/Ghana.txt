<p>The <b>Republic of Ghana</b> is a country in <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/West_Africa" title="West Africa">West Africa</a> which borders <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/C%C3%B4te_d%27Ivoire" title="Côte d'Ivoire">Côte d'Ivoire</a> (Ivory Coast) to the west, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Burkina_Faso" title="Burkina Faso">Burkina Faso</a> to the north, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Togo" title="Togo">Togo</a> to the east, and the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gulf_of_Guinea" title="Gulf of Guinea">Gulf of Guinea</a> to the south. The word <i>Ghana</i> means "Warrior King,"<sup id="cite_ref-warriorking_3-0" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ghana#cite_note-warriorking-3"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> and was the source of the name "Guinea" (via French <i>Guinoye</i>), which has been used to refer to the West African coast (reflected in the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gulf_of_Guinea" title="Gulf of Guinea">Gulf of Guinea</a>).</p>
<p>Ghana was inhabited in pre-colonial times by a number of ancient predominantly <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Akan" title="Akan">Akan</a> Kingdoms, including the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Akwamu" title="Akwamu">Akwamu</a> on the eastern coast, the inland <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ashanti_Empire" title="Ashanti Empire">Ashanti Empire</a> and various <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fante" title="Fante">Fante</a> and, also non Akan states like the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ga" title="Ga" class="mw-redirect">Ga</a> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ewe" title="Ewe">Ewe</a> along the coast and inland. Trade with European states flourished after contact with the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portuguese_Empire" title="Portuguese Empire">Portuguese</a> in the 15th century, and the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Kingdom_of_Great_Britain_and_Ireland" title="United Kingdom of Great Britain and Ireland">British</a> established a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crown_colony" title="Crown colony">crown colony</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gold_Coast_(British_colony)" title="Gold Coast (British colony)">Gold Coast</a>, in 1874.<sup id="cite_ref-colestablish_4-0" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ghana#cite_note-colestablish-4"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gold_Coast_(British_colony)" title="Gold Coast (British colony)">Gold Coast</a> achieved independence from the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a> in 1957, becoming the first <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sub-Saharan_Africa" title="Sub-Saharan Africa">Sub-Saharan African</a> nation to do so<sup id="cite_ref-independence_5-0" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ghana#cite_note-independence-5"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup> <sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ghana#cite_note-6"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ghana#cite_note-7"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup> and the name Ghana was chosen for the new nation to reflect the ancient <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Empire_of_Ghana" title="Empire of Ghana" class="mw-redirect">Empire of Ghana</a>, which once extended throughout much of west Africa. Ghana is a member of many international organisations including the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Commonwealth_of_Nations" title="Commonwealth of Nations">Commonwealth of Nations</a>, the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Economic_Community_of_West_African_States" title="Economic Community of West African States">Economic Community of West African States</a>, the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/African_Union" title="African Union">African Union</a> and the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations</a>. Ghana is the second largest producer of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cocoa" title="Cocoa">cocoa</a> in the world and is also home to <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lake_Volta" title="Lake Volta">Lake Volta</a>, the largest artificial lake in the world<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ghana#cite_note-8"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup>. Ghana is also very well known in the football world. On October 16, 2009, it became the first African nation to win the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/FIFA_U-20_World_Cup" title="FIFA U-20 World Cup">FIFA U-20 World Cup</a> by defeating <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a> 4-3 in a penalty shootout<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ghana#cite_note-9"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup>.</p>
