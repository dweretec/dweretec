<p><b>Exploration</b> is the act of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Search" title="Search" class="mw-redirect">searching</a> or <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Travel" title="Travel">traveling</a> a terrain (including <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Space" title="Space">space</a>, see <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Space_exploration" title="Space exploration">space exploration</a>) for the purpose of discovery of resources or <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Information" title="Information">information</a>. Exploration occurs in all non-sessile animal species, including humans. In human <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/History" title="History">history</a>, its peak is seen during the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Age_of_Discovery" title="Age of Discovery">Age of Discovery</a> for Europe's contact with the rest of the world, and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Major_explorations_after_the_Age_of_Discovery" title="Major explorations after the Age of Discovery">Major explorations after the Age of Discovery</a> for scientific exploration in the modern era.</p>
