<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arabic_language" title="Arabic language">Arabic</a>: <span lang="ar" dir="rtl">مِصر</span>‎ <i><span title="DIN 31635 Arabic" class="Unicode" style="white-space:normal; text-decoration: none">Miṣr</span></i>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Egyptian_Arabic_language" title="Egyptian Arabic language" class="mw-redirect">Egyptian Arabic</a>: <span lang="arz">مَصر</span> <span title="DIN transliteration" class="Unicode" style="white-space:normal; text-decoration: none"><i>Maṣr</i></span>) is a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Transcontinental_country" title="Transcontinental country" class="mw-redirect">transcontinental country</a> spanning the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/North_Africa" title="North Africa">northeast corner of Africa</a> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Western_Asia" title="Western Asia">southwest corner of Asia</a>, via a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Land_bridge" title="Land bridge">land bridge</a> formed by the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sinai_Peninsula" title="Sinai Peninsula">Sinai Peninsula</a>. It is the world's only contiguous <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eurafrasian" title="Eurafrasian" class="mw-redirect">Eurafrasian</a> nation and most of Egypt's territory of 1,010,408 square kilometres (390,000&#160;sq&#160;mi) lies within the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nile_Valley" title="Nile Valley" class="mw-redirect">Nile Valley</a>. It is a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mediterranean" title="Mediterranean" class="mw-redirect">Mediterranean country</a> and is bordered by the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gaza_Strip" title="Gaza Strip">Gaza Strip</a> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Israel" title="Israel">Israel</a> to the northeast, the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gulf_of_Aqaba" title="Gulf of Aqaba">Gulf of Aqaba</a> to the east, the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Red_Sea" title="Red Sea">Red Sea</a> to the east and south, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sudan" title="Sudan">Sudan</a> to the south and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Libya" title="Libya">Libya</a> to the west.</p>
<p>With over 88 million inhabitants, Egypt is the most populous country in <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/North_Africa" title="North Africa">North Africa</a> and the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arab_World" title="Arab World" class="mw-redirect">Arab World</a>, the third-most populous in Africa, and the fifteenth-most populous in the world. The great majority of its people live near the banks of the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nile" title="Nile">Nile</a> River, an area of about 40,000 square kilometres (15,000&#160;sq&#160;mi), where the only <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arable_land" title="Arable land">arable land</a> is found. The large regions of the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sahara" title="Sahara">Sahara</a> <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Desert" title="Desert">desert</a>, which constitute most of Egypt's territory, are sparsely inhabited. About half of Egypt's residents live in urban areas, with most spread across the densely populated centres of greater <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cairo" title="Cairo">Cairo</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alexandria" title="Alexandria">Alexandria</a> and other major cities in the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nile_Delta" title="Nile Delta">Nile Delta</a>.</p>
<p>Egypt has <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/History_of_Egypt" title="History of Egypt">one of the longest histories</a> of any modern country, arising in the tenth millennium BCE as one of the world's first <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nation_states" title="Nation states" class="mw-redirect">nation states</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Egypt#cite_note-14"><span>[</span>14<span>]</span></a></sup> Considered a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cradle_of_civilization" title="Cradle of civilization">cradle of civilization</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ancient_Egypt" title="Ancient Egypt">Ancient Egypt</a> experienced some of the earliest developments of writing, agriculture, urbanisation, organised religion and central government in history. Iconic monuments such as the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Giza_Necropolis" title="Giza Necropolis">Giza Necropolis</a> and its <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Great_Sphinx_of_Giza" title="Great Sphinx of Giza">Great Sphinx</a>, as well the ruins of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Memphis,_Egypt" title="Memphis, Egypt">Memphis</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thebes,_Egypt" title="Thebes, Egypt">Thebes</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Karnak" title="Karnak">Karnak</a>, and the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Valley_of_the_Kings" title="Valley of the Kings">Valley of the Kings</a>, reflect this legacy and remain a significant focus of archaeological study and popular interest worldwide. Egypt's rich cultural heritage is an integral part of its national identity, having endured and at times assimilated various foreign influences, including Greek, Persian, Roman, Arab, Ottoman, and European.</p>
<p>Modern Egypt is considered to be a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Regional_power" title="Regional power">regional</a> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Middle_power" title="Middle power">middle power</a>, with significant cultural, political, and military influence in <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/North_Africa" title="North Africa">North Africa</a>, the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Middle_East" title="Middle East">Middle East</a> and the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Muslim_world" title="Muslim world">Muslim world</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Egypt#cite_note-15"><span>[</span>15<span>]</span></a></sup> Its economy is <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Economy_of_Egypt" title="Economy of Egypt">one of the largest and most diversified</a> in the Middle East, with sectors such as tourism, agriculture, industry and services at almost equal production levels. In <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Egyptian_Revolution_of_2011" title="Egyptian Revolution of 2011">2011</a>, longtime President <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hosni_Mubarak" title="Hosni Mubarak">Hosni Mubarak</a> stepped down amid mass protests. Later elections saw the rise of the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Muslim_Brotherhood" title="Muslim Brotherhood">Muslim Brotherhood</a>, which was ousted by the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Egyptian_Armed_Forces" title="Egyptian Armed Forces">army</a> a year later amid mass protests.</p>
